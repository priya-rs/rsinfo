Yates is located 90 miles south of Midland, Texas, at the southeastern tip of the Central Basin Platform of the Permian Basin, as shown on the map in Figure 1. The field has produced from the Seven Rivers, Queen, and Grayburg formations, but the main producing formation is the San Andres carbonate, which is a naturally fractured, dolomite reservoir. The YFU 4045 Hydrocarbon Miscible (HCM) Pilot is located within a mile of each of the aforementioned wells, as indicated on the Yates Field Top of San Andres Structure Map in Figure 2.

Primary production was initially by volumetric expansion as the pressure dropped from 800 psi to the original bubble point of around 650 psi.

Produced gas was recycled back into the reservoir to mitigate the pressure decline beginning in July 1976. By injecting at the top of the structure the gas injection assisted the ongoing GOGD process.

Since gas reservoir volume factors (3.0 RB/MSCF for CO2, 3.6 RB/MSCF for recycle gas) are high at the low Yates (640 psi in 2004) reservoir pressure, these increases in gas injection had a much larger effect on the reservoir processing rate at Yates than they would in the deeper, higher pressure reservoirs where CO2 has been injected elsewhere in the Permian Basin. Figure 5 shows how the reservoir production and injection rates increased from around 500,000 to 1,800,000 RBPD with the 2nd Phase of CO2 injection beginning in 2004. However, the oil cut as a percentage of the reservoir fluid production continued on its same hyperbolic decline, indicating that the production increase was not due to a miscible process.

Another field, the Rainbow Keg River B Field (Nagel 1988), was already on an established waterflood decline prior to the start of HCM injection, as shown by the data in Figure 6. After the HCM slug had been injected, there was a significant increase in both the oil rate and a flattening of the Reservoir Barrel (RB) oil cut decline. Incremental recovery from HCM injection in the Keg River B project is estimated to be around 12 percent of the OOIP, with a gross HCM utilization of around 1 STB/BBL HCM.

The YFU 4045 injector is bounded by three oil producing horizontal wells (YFU 4002, 4063, 4064), as shown in Figure 7. Selection of this pattern was also based on its definitive, historical response to CO2 injection and established areal connectivity.

The YFU 4068 S-shaped well trajectory to its bottom hole location 177' W-NW of YFU 4045 is shown in Figure 8. The well was cased through the entire San Andres main pay and transition zone with fiberglass boron-free and metallic-free tubulars, collars, and pipe dope in order to allow induction resistivity logging. However, the primary logging measurements were performed using a carbon oxygen (C/O) pulsed neutron logging tool.

The coreflood results are shown in Figure 9, which was conducted by injecting many volumes of immiscible CO2, followed by Y-Grade injection, which recovered incremental oil.

The oil production response has been favorable, with the oil rate impacted by the Y-Grade injection increasing from 47 BOPD up to a peak rate of 100 BOPD, as shown in Figure 10. During this initial Y- Grade injection period, YFU 4063ST1 and 4064ST1"s GOR's declined to the point where they could both produce continuously and were taken off the reactivation schedule. YFU 4064 was on reactivation for one day, shut-in for 6 days, then 2 days, shut-in for 5 days before falling below the field GOR cutoff and producing continuously thereafter.

Although oil response from the Pilot was favorable, the cumulative oil and gas phase Y-Grade production was not equally distributed between the three producers. This is shown by the cumulative production through late June 2017 in Figure 11. The imbalance indicated that the areal sweep was misaligned, so the pilot wasn't providing an estimate of the true recovery potential of HCM injection at Yates.

The interpretations of several of these monitor logging runs are shown in Figure 15, along with the YFU 4068 porosity curve, and the four major logging interval designations. The upper most interval includes the Queen and Grayburg tie-in intervals, so named because they are lower intervals where little if any fluid saturation changes were anticipated. Then there is the Secondary Gas Cap interval, which is the thickest interval with the highest porosity, and should be where the largest remaining residual oil target exists.

The calculated oil saturations from the monthly monitor logs were averaged across the HCM Processed Interval, and are shown plotted with time in Figure 16. The six months of Y-Grade injection appears to have reduced the oil saturation by 8-10 percent. In comparison, the shallower San Andres reservoir above the HCM injection exhibited much less oil desaturation.

A fully compositional, dual permeability model was developed to study the YFU 4045 HCM Pilot performance. A 30 × 39 × 47 grid was used, as shown in Figure 17. Well trajectories were input based on their HDH deviation surveys. The layers are areally homomogeneous, but are varied vertically by tying to the YFU 4045 porosity curve. Permeabilities were calculated from the field k-phi transform. Increasing the porosity also reduced the Y-Grade back production to allow it to match the actual performance, as shown by Figure 19. Without this change, the model Y- Grade back production was around twice the actual rate, because the Y-Grade moved down the fractures with little entering the matrix. With the higher permeability, more Y- Grade was absorbed into the matrix, reducing the Y-Grade back production.

When this entire 1000 feet (21 grid blocks) of completion was opened in the model, total fluid production was far too high. Reducing the number of open grid blocks to 6 (every 4th block being open) resulted in a more representative total fluid rate. The GOR was too low and WOR too high, so the well was completed two layers shallower in the model, resulting in the history match shown in Figure 20. The oil-water contact appears to be lower in this part of the model area. An improved reservoir description should improve this match quality.
