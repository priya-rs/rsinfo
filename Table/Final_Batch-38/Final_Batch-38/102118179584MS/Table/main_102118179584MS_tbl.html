Three different types of foaming agents were chosen through preliminary screening, which included nonionic, anionic and anionic-nonionic surfactants. They were investigated using various assessment methods at different concentrations. A Spinning Drop Interface Tension meter TX-500C (CNG Company, USA) and a foam meter device (FOAMSCAN, TECLIS Instruments Company, France) were used in the experiments. A sandpack flooding set-up was used in the experiments as shown in Figure 1. To evaluate the blocking capacity of foams, only formation water (salinity of 15,000 -30,000 mg/L) was saturated in the sandpack tubes. Pressure drops across the sandpack were measured during water and foam injection for FR against water flooding. The detailed experimental conditions applied in the sandpack flooding are shown in Table 2.

A high gas/liquid ratio cannot form a stable foam, and a low one may fail to generate sufficient foams. An optimized gas/liquid ratio for a specified foam agent and gas system needs to be determined via foam flooding experiments. The Enhanced-V foam system was tested using the three sandpack tubes with different gas/liquid ratios. The experimental schemes are shown in Table 7.

The average concentration of the foaming agents (enhanced with polymer) was around 0.6% and the average gas/liquid ratio used was of 1:1. The performance of typical production wells in terms of water cut is shown in Table 8. The reduction of water cut for single wells ranged from 2.4% to 10.2% after foam injection, which indicated that the injection of nitrogen foam can effectively block the high permeability zones and has a positive effect on reducing water coning.
