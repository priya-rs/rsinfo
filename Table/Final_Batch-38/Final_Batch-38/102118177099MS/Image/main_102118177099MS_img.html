The lower La Grita Member of Early Cretaceous Capacho Formation was deposited on western Venezuela which span from uppermost Albian to Cenomanian. Its statigraphic limits are established at top with Seboruco Member of Cenomanian and at base with Aguardiente Formation of Aptian - Albian (fig. 3). It has a wide distribution in some states such as Zulia, T´chira and Mérida. Also, this member has an average thickness above 80 feet (24.38 m), increasing progressively at south.

In the Cretaceous sequences are denoted some thrust (oriented west - east) that truncate with the main faults. The figure 4 shows the structural map of La Grita Member established in the deep range proposed by Kuuskraa et al. (2013), between 3500 – 16500 feet, where can be observed that major tectonic complexity is in the south and center section of the area. However, at western the tectonic affectation is relatively low according to seismic interpretation generated (fig. 5) where La Grita Member reflector has a condition horizontal or sub horizontal (viewing zones flat).

Figure 6 shows a stratigraphic section in direction of the sedimentation, SW – NE. In this section can be noticed as La Grita Member gradually thins at northeast, while that at southwest the stratigraphic unit increases or thickens specifically in the deposits of calcareous shale. Also, the figure exhibits the continuity of level of bioclastic limestones at base of unit representing an important marker in the area.

Also, TOC was derivated from resistivity logs, transit time, formation density and neutron according to methodology of Passey et al. (1990). The level of maturity (LOM) was estimated at 10.2 corresponding to a state of full maturity of kerogen in the interval. As a result, it generated a curve of TOC using the logs of well P_B1, which oscillate between values of 4 to 8% into La Grita Member (fig. 7). This curve created fits with the values of geochemical data obtained of core samples, confirming the organic richness of the stratigraphic unit of interest.

The mineralogical proportion of La Grita Member obtained from core samples is displayed in the ternary diagram proposed by Jarvie et al. (2007) in figure 9. The mineralogical distribution viewing in the diagram ternary shows a mixture between quartz/feldspars and calcite, indicating a kind of rock with low content of clay and good conditions for fracturing.

Moreover, the brittleness analysis was developed using the methodology of Grieser and Bray (2007) and Kuuskraa et al. (2013), both indicated that the stratigraphic unit of interest can be classified as brittle. Figure 10 shows that the values of Poisson’s ratio and Young’s modulus of La Grita Member have been located in the brittle region. This result made match with the brittleness index determined by mineralogical analysis, wich has an average fraction of 0.77.
