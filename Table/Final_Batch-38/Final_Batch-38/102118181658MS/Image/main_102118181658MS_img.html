Typically, the Scandium tracer is injected into the first half to two-thirds of the proppant volume, and the Iridium tracer is injected into the last half to one-third of the proppant. The two tracers emit gamma rays at different energy levels and intensities (Figure 1) and their spatial placement both vertically and radially are confirmed using a spectral gamma ray logging tool.

Noble Energy has had great success in the Mississippi Canyon area in the Gulf of Mexico. Currently, Noble has six fields (five (5) operated and one (1) non-operated) in this area. The locations and Noble’s acreage position are depicted on Figure 4.

Fortunately, the FP was traced because no other definitive method was available to confirm integrity. The contignecy logging tools were deployed on electric line immediately after the job was concluded. Both spectral gamma ray and density logs, illustrated in Figure 5, confirmed that the a failure had occurred resulting in annular pack reverse circulation out of the well, leaving an annulus void (no proppant) across the upper 50-ft of screen.

Note, that the spotty Scandium response on the second log due to a partial tracing of the proppant. The partial tracing, identified by the STMU plot, can be seen in Figure 6. The Scandium counts should have remained high until the Iridium started at 02:16:00. This is a diagnostic plot that is always captured, but is not typically utilized, except when there are issues.
