A regional top seal is formed by the Cretaceous Nahr Umr Formation and extremely widespread as part of trangressive shale (Murris, 1980). In the lower part is bounded by Upper Permian - Early Triassic Khuff Formation, then gradually changing from fully sandstone with little streaks of shale to be interbedded between sand and shale, and then bounded by fully shale of the latest Aptian - Albian Nahr Umr Formation above (Fig. 1).

Basically, in the XYZ reservoir can divided to be 3 main zonation which has the specified lithology characteristics i.e. dominantly sandstone sequence with some or more streaks of shale in the lower part; then gradually to be shally sand in the middle part; and then will be found another sandstone sequence with some streaks of shale in the upper part. The distribution of shally sand can be clearly correlated from one well to others, but in the some wells especially that drilled by geo-steering cannot be correlated due to most contain fully sandstone (Fig. 2). These typically sandstone and shally sand distribution is one of parameter which is considered in the determination of well location.

Currently, this field already drilled 26 wells which consist of 5 vertical wells; 2 slanted wells and 19 horizontal wells. The horizontal wells, basically targeting MKhuff carbonate and XYZ sands whereas the vertical and slanted wells directly drilled to XYZ sands only. The CSS applied in the 3 vertical wells which drilled to XYZ sands directly through Nahr Umr without enter MKhuff first like the horizontal wells (Fig.3)

Overall, XYZ sand reservoir has good to very good porosity and also permeability but in the other side it has high viscosity with varies from 600 up to 1400 cp. The average porosity is about 27% and the average permeability is about 2500 mD. The reservoir is bounded on the south by an aquifer of moderate to strong. These 3 CSS wells designed to be around 75 to 100 meters to the adjacent wells, then still have space around 20 meters away from owc and still have moderate production performance of adjacent wells (Fig.4).
