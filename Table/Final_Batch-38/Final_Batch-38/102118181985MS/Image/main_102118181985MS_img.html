In this case, it can be noted that placing perforations interval in the upper part of the zone (blue line) is expected to break away from the target injection zone after 120 months of injection, low placement (black line) is expected to break downwards after approximately same period of injection.

Injection rate must remain constant prior to shut down, even a small change due to recycle activation can compromise the data and jeopardize the analysis. For example, a minor (less than 2%) rate variation shortly before well shutdown (Figure 3) was sufficient to make the data practically unusable – the degree of interpretation uncertainty went unacceptably high.

DTS warmback tracks (Figure 4) analysis allows to measure directly cooled zone height extension and fracture evolution can be monitored in real time. This surveillance is used to alert abnormal height growth and to calibrate both models – thermal fracture propagation and pressure falloff analysis. Once the geometry of both models match with DTS surveillance results, further fractures behavior can be predicted with fair certainty, and corrective actions, if required, can be undertaken.
