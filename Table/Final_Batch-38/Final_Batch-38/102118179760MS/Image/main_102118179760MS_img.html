Core flooding experiments: CFS-700 high pressure/high temperature chemical flooding system was used for core flooding experiments (Fig. 3). Based on the development history of pilot formation, the lab physical simulation displacement procedures were made to investigate the effect of chemical injection modes, design of foam slug and ASP slug on displacement efficiency during foam/ASP flooding. The experimental cores were mounted horizontally into a Hessler sleeve. The behavior of injected foam was monitored by camera through a sapphire visual cell before the inlet of core holder.

Selection of the foaming agents The properties of foam formulation are the key factor to ensure high performance of recovery [7]. Dozens of commercial surfactants (acted as foamers) had been screened by dissolubility tests with formation water firstly. The foam ability and stability were then tested for foamers with good solubility (Fig. 4). From the results of experimental evaluation of comprehensive performance of foamability and foam stability, four foaming agents with good performance though preliminary screening: F3, F6, F10 and F12. By considering the oil resistant and economic properties, F3 was selected as foaming agent forfurther experiments.

Both foamability and stability for F3 foam system were good. Fig. 5 showed that both the initial foam volume and half-time for dewatering were increased as F3 concentration was increased from 0.05% to 0.6% for bulk foam system. To optimize the experimental protocol (blancing the factors such as foamability, stability and acceptable chemical costs), F3 concentration was determined as 0.4% in following experiments.

Selection of foam stabilizers To enhance foam stability, commercially high molecule polymer such as HPAM, KYPAM, and MO4000 were used as the stabilizer. Remarkable increase in stability was observed for foam system when stabilizer was added (Fig. 6). Half-time of dewater increased from 527 seconds to 1,347 seconds in the presence of stabilizer S1 with concentration of 0.09%. The foam stability enhancement of high molecule polymer may be understood by studying the liquid film separation between neighbor gas bubbles. Stability of foam is closely connected to the stability of film and the foam film should be somewhat elastic to ensure withstand deformation without rupturing.
