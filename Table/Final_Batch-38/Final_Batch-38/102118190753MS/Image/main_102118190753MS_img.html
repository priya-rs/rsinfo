For example, in Figure 5 the plot for any well that produced less than 5 MMstb of water would occupy only a small portion of the graph, and comparison of the characteristic profile to identify the contribution of injection water would be difficult to interpret. Thus, normalization is used to make direct comparison easier.

In Figure 6, the plot for Well A illustrates a case of seawater breakthrough via the oil leg with a sharp breakthrough profile. By the time the well achieved 10% of its cumulative water production, the greater part of that water was already coming from supporting injector(s) (around 90%). This indicates a very strong contribution from the injection system and good communication through the oil leg between the injectors and Well A.
