A capillary suction time (CST) test was performed with the sample to design a permanent clay control strategy for this formation. The basic principle behind the CST test is that the lower the Ratio B value, the better the clay swelling inhibition. Therefore, the CST ratio of distilled water (DI) (=1) represents non-inhibiting fluid characteristic. Accordingly, Table 5 shows that the higher concentration of clay control agent has reduced the CST Ratio B, as compared to the lower concentration.

Cased hole gravel packing has been designed as a two-stage process. The two processes were based on the realization that gravel packs must achieve and sustain high productivity for a well with sanding tendencies. To accomplish these productivity goals, the perforations and the area external to the wellbore (Table 6) were designed to be packed with sized gravel in a way that provides fluid communication from the undamaged formation to the wellbore.
