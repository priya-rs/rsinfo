This was addressed with the flow guide feature which is a protrusion on the blade front that provides a conduit for the flow from nozzles to pass over the cutters. CFD indicated an average increase of 15-30% in both velocity (Fig. 2) and shear stress on the face of the cutters that helps with cleaning and cooling. The use of flow guide also reduces flow recirculation in the junk slot area which also improves cuttings evacuation. Full scale pressurized testing (Moslemi et al 2014) indicated an increase in ROP and reduction in bit balling for drilling in Catoosa shale, due to the use of the flow guide feature.

The Williston Basin is located in North Dakota, USA (Fig. 5). The Bakken formation consists of three sections: Upper Bakken, Middle Bakken and Lower Bakken. The Middle Bakken is composed of siltstone cemented with calcite, dolomite and silica while the Upper and Lower Bakken are composed of hard siliceous shales. The Middle Bakken reaches a maximum thickness of 90 ft near the center of the basin (Gaswirth et al 2013).

The typical lateral section of a Bakken well has an average of 10,000 ft in length through the middle Bakken formation drilled at 90° inclination after setting 7” casing string (Fig. 6).

The first 6” 5-bladed, 16 mm cutter drill bit was run at a depth of 11,725 ft and drilled 7,406 ft before being pulled for downhole tool failure. The average ROP was 194.9 ft/h and was dull graded 1-2-WT-A-X-I-NO-BHA (Fig. 7).

The steel bodied 6-bladed, 16 mm cutting structure design was run on a rotary assembly with water based mud and went in at 1,077 m and drilled through carbonate sequences interbedded with shales before reaching the top of the very hard and abrasive sandstone sequence of Al Khalata formation of approxi-mately 130 m thickness. It reached a total depth at 3,684 m, having drilled a total of 2,607 m at 15.4 m/h. The bit was graded 1-2-WT-N-X-I-CT-TD (Fig 10).

The run resulted in a 29% ROP improvement versus direct offsets (Fig. 11) with its first iteration.

The8¾” 6-bladed design with 16 mm cutters went in at 604 m and drilled 878 m at 54.88 m/h. It drilled mostly through hard shales and sandstones with traces of siltstones, and a sandstone sequence with chert stringers in the bottom section, with UCS values ranging from 15-25 Kpsi (Fig 12).

The bit drilled vertically after drilling out the casing shoe and was graded 2-2-CT-S-X-I-BT-PR (Fig. 13). The section total depth was called 23 meters deeper. The bit achieved 23% increased ROP versus direct offsets within a 10 km radius, with an increased interval of more than 200 m (Fig. 14).
