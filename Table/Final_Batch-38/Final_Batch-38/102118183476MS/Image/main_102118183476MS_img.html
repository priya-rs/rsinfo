The porous zones consist of clean limestone and the texture is mainly Packstone (Grainstone) with some Wackestone and Boundstone. Each zone (referred as Reservoir 1, 2 & 3 in this paper) has different permeability with the middle zone (reservoir 2) having comparatively higher permeability, as shown in Figure 1 (a, b, c). The thick impermeable dense zone in between two porous zones provides sealing between two reservoirs.

Historically, the water injection wells used to be completed as deviated dual water injectors, where short string used to be completed in Reservoir 1 and Reservoir 2 & 3 were in long string commingled, as shown in Figure-2.

The permeability contrast between different reservoirs (Reservoir 2> Reservoir 1 & Reservoir 3) resulted in higher injection intake in Reservoir 2, even though initially the pressure difference between different reservoirs was not substantially high as shown in Figure 3 (a, b, c). This phenomenon was verified by PLT logging results conducted in most of the commingled water injection wells. The injection distribution is shown in Figure-4 where Reservoir 2 was taking injection at an average of around 78 percent.

Stage-2: to overcome the challenge of having high split ratio between Reservoirs 2 and 3, the strategy for completing the commingled water injection wells was reviewed; in order to improve the injection in Reservoir 3, it was considered to complete Reservoir 3 as open hole horizontal whilst keeping the Reservoir 2 in open hole deviated section. On the other hand, to improve the injectivity in Reservoir 1, it was decided to complete the short string as horizontal drain in Reservoir 1, as shown in Figure-5.

After technology evaluation and worldwide sound track record, the first ICD completion in water injection well in ADMA-OPCO was implemented in early 2009 with the combination of ICDs across Reservoir 2 and open hole in Reservoir 3 (Figure-6).

Stage – 3 Maturation: After successful deployment in the first well, more than 10 wells have been completed with ICD system to-date. Three different completion configurations were deployed in different wells depending on the injection distribution requirements in the reservoirs and well configuration (Figure-6). Further options were explored to utilize ICD for comingle injection in all three reservoirs through a single drain, particularly in workover wells, where dual completion was not feasible for various reasons, as shown Figure 7 a, b and c

At the initial stage of designing, ICD completions were modelled utilizing commercial ICD modeling software using different completion configurations. The well bore hydraulics design was also performed to generate different injection profiles (an example of plot for VLP curves is shown in Figure-8).

For water injection well ICD modeling, the main data input requirements are reservoir pressure profile along the proposed drain, permeability profile, surface injection pressure and target injection rates. In some cases, where permeability data was not available and/or not matching with the actual well performance of the nearby injectors, nearby wells' injectivity data was used in the model to achieve realistic injection profiles in open hole condition to simulate. By using this technique, the model was built as an open hole and matched with nearby wells' performance, this performance was classified as the base case for building ICD model. As an example the open hole model results are shown in Figure-9.

Once the open hole model was matched, different ICD configurations were modelled for each well as shown in Figure-10. The best simulation results which could satisfy the injection profile were obtained from the combination of ICDs across Reservoir 2 and open hole across Reservoir 3 as illustrated in Figure-11.

In subject field, the caliper log runs were not performed initially for first five wells as there was no historical data available regarding washouts and over gauge hole problem in these reservoir sections. Hence 4-1/2" liners with ICDs were run across Reservoir 2 directly after drilling 6" hole in all wells. This operation sequence saved rig time and cost of running caliper log. After five successful ICD completion deployments, a packer leak was observed in the sixth well through PLT logging which resulted in 65% of water injection in Reservoir 2 compared to the designed injection split of 45% as shown in Figure 12.

Due to open system (ICD across Reservoir 2 & open hole across Reservoir 3 as shown in Figure-6), another problem was encountered with running on/off ICDs where open hole hydraulic (mechanical) packer requires an inner string to set. Running inner string requires additional care to avoid accidental closing of ICDs. This particular problem was observed in one well while POOH with inner string after setting packers that accidently closed the sleeves and hence resulted in no water injection in Reservoir 2 for first 6 months. This was later confirmed by running PLT log (Figure-13 a). Later in this well, coil tubing with ICD shifting tool was run to open on/off ICDs and resumed water injection in Reservoir 2 (Figure-13 b).

A simulation model was run for both cases; one before work over till 2009 and other one after work over. Model was matched and calibrated with the time lapsed PLT log results (Figure-14 & 15). After achieving a good model history match, forecast cases were run to simulate the future performance in both reservoirs 2 & 3. Simulation results showed significant improvement in injection profile in Reservoir 3 post ICD installation against Reservoir 2.

With the re-distribution of injection between both reservoirs post ICD installation, the impact on reservoir pressure was assessed by simulating the pressure in a reservoir segment over the next 15 years. Improvements were observed in the reservoir pressure as shown in Figure-16, hence endorsing the benefit of restricting the water injection in Reservoir 2 and allowing more injection contribution in Reservoir 3.

In order to find the injection split post ICD installation, PLT logging was performed in each well. The logging results summarized a fascinating success story where the injection split between reservoir 2 and 3 in almost all the injectors could meet 50-50% requirement.
