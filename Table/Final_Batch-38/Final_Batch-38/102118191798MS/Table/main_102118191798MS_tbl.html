To summarize, the ACE-augmented hybrid physics-based MVA model results in an optimal proppant mass of 1,166 lb/ft and an average proppant concentration of 0.82 ppg, which is equivalent to a clean fracturing fluid volume of 34 bbl/ft (Table 10). In terms of stage design, this would equate to 291,500 lbs of proppant and 8,500 bbls of clean fluid volume per stage in a 30-stage 7,500 ft lateral. As a side note, from 2011 to 2017, the average proppant loading and fluid volume increased from 1,000 to 1,800 lb/ft and from 25 to 50 bbl/ft respectively, whereas the ave-rage proppant concentration historically remained at about 1 ppg for the Midland Basin Wolfcamp B horizontal wells in the study area.
