The objective of this study is to optimize the water injection of reservoir M1, HN oilfield. This oilfield is located in Ecuadorian Oriente Basin (Fig.1).

According to the sand distribution prediction by seismic (Fig.2), and oil saturation distribution from reservoir simulation (Fig.4), we also consider the structure map and the water source.

This HN:M1 reservoir started to produce in 1995 (Fig.3). At the beginning, there were only several producers.

From the remaining oil saturation distribution, we can conclude that, in vertical the upper of M1 reservoir is better, and in horizontal, the west of M1 is better. But the geologist also mentioned that, the sand in the upper part is poor, meanwhile the west of M1 has a thin sand (Fig. 4).

From the production performance status in 2011 (Fig.5), we can conclude that. Firstly, the watercut of the wells near to the aquifer was high; secondly, there were mainly two parts in the whole reservoir, Area I was low watercut stage area, we should keep the watercut as low as possible, that means to prevent waterflooding was important in this area, and Area II was high watercut stage area, in order to keep the oil production stable, we could increase the liquid since it was flooded out in this area.
