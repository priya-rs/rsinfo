South Sumatera Basin is bounded by crystalline basement and Pre-Tertiary metasediment of Tigapuluh High in the northwest, the Asian Plate; on the southeast edge of the Sunda Shelf (Lingga Island and Bangka) in the northeast, the Bukit Barisan Mountains and Garba Mountains in the south and west, and bounded by Lampung High in the southeast. The position of South Sumatra Basin geographically and its tectonic element boundary can be seen in Fig. 3.

From old to young age, stratigraphy of South Sumatera Basin is composed of basement rock; crystalline lithology type (igneous rock and metamorphic rock) and metasediment (Ryacudu 2005). Then, it is followed by sedimentation in Tertiary, consisting of Lahat Formation and Lemat Formation, Talang Akar Formation, Baturaja Formation, Gumai Formation, Air Benakat Formation, Muaraenim Formation, and Kasai Formation as seen on Fig. 4.

Using stratigraphy sequence correlation method, regional well correlation was conducted covering all of sub basin in South Sumatera Basin. A complete section of Talang Akar formation (TAF) from Lower Talang Akar to Upper Talang Akar only found in Jambi sub basin and South Palembang sub basin, while in two others sub basin lower Talang Akar has not been explore yet. Regional well correlation of Talang Akar Formation inter sub basin is presented on Fig. 5. In this study, stratigraphy sequence correlation just only defines sequence boundary (SB) and maximum-flooding surface (MFS) based on stratigraphic pattern observed on gamma ray, density, and neutron log.

Based on log data, true resistivity log (RT) shows variation of resistivity value in South Sumatera Basin (Fig. 6). Fig. 6 identifies two groups of resistivity value in South Sumatera Basin: High Resistivity Pay and Low Resistivity Pay. Based on well test result statistically (Fig. 6), chance of success for LRPZ is lower than High Resistivity Pay Zone (HRPZ) in South Sumatera Basin. True resistivity (RT) comparation between success well test cases and failed well test cases (wet test) indicates that RT can not be used as single data, but it need to be integrated with the other data to determine reservoir pay zone (indicating for flowing hydrocarbon) for well testing interval candidates.
