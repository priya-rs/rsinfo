To provide input data for scale and corrosion risk assessments planned for the Culzean Field (Figure 1) formation water samples were obtained from two locations in the Joanne Sandstone Member (4799m and 4800.5m TVDSS) of well 22/25a-10Z on this field and subsequently analysed. After an initial evaluation of the quality of the samples/analyses, it was estimated that the concentrations of Pb and Zn in the formation water were 218_11 mg/L Pb (172_9 ppm Pb) and 572_30 mg/L Zn (452_24 ppm Zn) (see Table 1).

However, despite this, when the estimates of Pb and Zn concentration were compared with the few available North Sea formation water Pb and Zn analyses (Table 2) it was evident that the Culzean concentrations are significantly higher. For this reason, the Culzean Pb and Zn concentrations were investigated further to determine whether they are real (in which case why are they so high?) or an artefact.

Mineral sources of Pb and Zn Although Pb and Zn can be brought into sedimentary basins in aqueous fluids, the most important of these by volume (e.g. seawater, evaporated seawater, meteoric water, waters released from compacting shales, i.e. ‘shale water’) contain very little Pb and Zn (Table 3). There is far greater potential for Pb and Zn to be gained by formation water through interaction with minerals in the subsurface.
