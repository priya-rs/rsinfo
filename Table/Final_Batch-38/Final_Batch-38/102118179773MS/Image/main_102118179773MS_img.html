Issaran oil field is located 290 km southeast of Cairo and 3 km inland from the western shore of the Gulf of Suez, covering an area of 20,000 acres (Fig. 1). The major heavy oil accumulation occurs within shallow Miocene formations through 5 reservoir layers as shown in (Fig. 2)

The five layers differ in reservoir characteristics (Fig. 3). The reservoirs include fractured and heterogeneous carbonate reservoirs and an unconsolidated sandstone reservoir. Successful reservoir management, with limited data available, required different steam strategies to be implemented to deal with these challenges and maintain a good level of daily production while optimizing operating costs.

Zeit Sand (ZS) formation is located south east of Issaran field (see Fig. 4) is highly unconsolidated sandstone. The oil gravity is between 10-12 API. The top of reservoir is around 700-800 ft, the thickness as illustrated in (Fig.5) around 15 ft.

Zeit Sand (ZS) formation is located south east of Issaran field (see Fig. 4) is highly unconsolidated sandstone. The oil gravity is between 10-12 API. The top of reservoir is around 700-800 ft, the thickness as illustrated in (Fig.5) around 15 ft.

Cyclic steam injection started with cycle volumes lower than 2,500 bbls of steam due to small formation thickness. Injection rates were limited by a maximum wellhead injection pressure (WHIP) of 500 psig. The phase continued for around 1 year. A total of 94,000 bbls of steam were injected in the cyclic phase to pre-heat the reservoir. (Fig 6.) shows the effect of both the cyclic and the continuous steam injection strategies on the oil performance and the VRR.

Upper Dolomite South (UD South) formation (Fig.2) and (Fig.7) is characterized by depleted fractured dolomite reservoir. The top of reservoir is at around 1000 ft with average reservoir thickness of around 400 ft. (Fig.3)

From August 1983 until 2005, the productivity has been tested by cold production from five distributed wells but it faced a lot of challenges to produce the accumulated oil. So cyclic steam injection strategy into the reservoir was decided and continued development of the area by drilling new wells to cover all the area (Fig.8)

The slug volume may be very small (less than 5000 bbls steam) or big (greater than 50,000 bbls steam) and some of wells took more than one cycle and another took only one cycle. The difference between cycle volumes and cycle repetition is due to the well response to steam and oil production after steam cycle which is linked directly the previous challenges. (Fig.9)

With time, easy oil that was in the main fractures was produced and it was required to apply more heat by increase slug volume, but the cyclic steam injection lost its effect and it became uneconomical. The voidage replacement ratio (VRR) also became less than the optimum value [0.7] (Fig.10) which leads to decline of the produced total fluid and oil (Fig.11) It required changing the steam injection strategy from cyclic steam injection to continuous steam injection strategy
