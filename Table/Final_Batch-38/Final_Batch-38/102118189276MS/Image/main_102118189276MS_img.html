The broad range for the SBM is due to un-uniform mud properties from well to well. The capacitance exhibits significant variation across different products and is very sensitive to transition from high dielectric SBM during the early period of a pump out, to clean response for formation hydrocarbons or water (Figure 6). The FID with capacitance considered as qualitative measurement, therefore only changes in trend used as an indication of pumped out fluid. To verify the critical zones fluids, the samples are captured and opened at surface after pressure bleed off or sent to the PVT lab analysis.

Prior the FT job the well was logged with triple combo (GR-RES-NEU-DEN-CAL). Slim FT was run after but during pressure test at station # 3, engineer start to observed high tension on cable indicating cable partially key seated, see figure 7 (left). The key seat condition was getting worse until tension reach maximum allowable pull then cable became stuck after completion of pretest station # 10. Fishing Cut & Thread operation was commenced to free the cable.

The depth that cable got free, later seen in TC log, obstruction, see figure 7 (right), that there was a large washout follow by well azimuth changing that produced dog leg severity ~3.05 degree. TDL option then selected to run with slim FT after fishing job. TDL BHA was RIH and set at 50 m below cable stuck depth to prevent wireline cable from washout and high dog leg zone, see figure 8. After TDL set and slim FT ran into OH, the high tension that observed previously has been completely eliminated and allow normal slim FT to continue until job complete.

Example # 1, see figure 9, shows the fluid ID with hydrocarbon result when slim FT operated the pump for 5 minutes and observed capacitance drop from ~43.17 pF (mud filtrate) gradually drop down to 34.09 pF (hydrocarbon). After 40 minutes of pump out, the capacitance was stabilized at 28.17 pF indicated clean sample in the flowline. Sample was taken successfully at 45 minutes of pump out as indicated by increased in flowline pressure (green).

Example # 1, see figure 10, shows the fluid ID with hydrocarbon result. After slim FT operated the pump for 10 minutes, capacitance drop from ~55.80 pF (mud filtrate) drop down to 42 pF (hydrocarbon). After 30 minutes of pump out, the capacitance was stabilized at 42.80 pF indicated clean hydrocarbon sample in the flowline. Decision was made to only performed fluid ID then move to next station.
