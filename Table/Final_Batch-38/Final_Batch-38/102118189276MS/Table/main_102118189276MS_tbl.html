The traditional pipe conveyed logging (PCL) via tool pushing with WL conveyance has always been an expensive option for WL logging primarily due to the extra rig time associated with tripping in and out. Nevertherless PCL in harsh borehole environment is not the best option either. The main objections for each conveyance type are summarized in the Table 1.

Table 1 Traditional conveyance methods weaknesses overview

Table 1 Traditional conveyance methods weaknesses overview

WL CONVEYANCE PCL (TOOL PUSHED)
• Less effective when well deviation increase. Tool will lose momentum and speed.
• Longer pressure test, formation fluid/gas identification or sampling operating times increase the risk of cable get key seated or WL tool get stuck.
• If cable or tool became stuck, the only way to free the cable or tool is to commence fishing operation adding more rig time.		 • Tools are attached to the end of drillpipe and are exposed to wellbore while run in hole.
• Any bridge or obstruction encountered can damage the tool directly.
• No drillpipe circulation or rotation allow while running in hole
• WL running through the annulus of drillpipe before getting into drillpipe via side entry sub can potentially damaged by weight of drillpipe itself.
• WL in the annulus must be kept inside casing to reduce the risk of getting crushed by weight of drillpipe and openhole environment. This limit the length of openhole to be logged.
• Multiple latchs of WL to tool attached at the end of drillpipe are required for the above limitations. More latch also posts the risk of damaging downhole wet connects and increase rig time.
• Any small pipe movement during formation testing and sampling can severly damage the tool.

The slim FT allows to measure resistance and capacitance of passing fluids in real time. Since the majority of wells onshore of Thailand are drilled with SBM; therefore resistivity readings are usually not accurate due to sensors clogging by mud. The capacitance is the ability of a system to hold electrical charge and it is proportional to the dielectric constant of a material. For Thailand fields only capacitance was used for FID after various calibrations prior and after the jobs (Table 3).
