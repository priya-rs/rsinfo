The results of PLTs carried out in 176 commingled wells were analysed and for 140 of them quantitative estimation of each horizon contribution was provided. Fig. 6 shows an example of such study and generalized results of the PLT. Analysis showed that there was no obvious maximum between each horizon contribution and it varies in each individual case depending on the geological structure and production history of the neighbouring area.

All wells in the field are covered by regular measurements of bottomhole pressure and flow rate. The data showed that the liquid production flow rates are in steady decline despite the relatively constant bottomhole pressure and reinjection of more than 100% of produced liquids. One of the reasons for that could be skin factor growth in production wells. Figure 7 shows bottomhole pressure and production rate dynamics for one well, where first peak in 2000 was due to re-perforation and second in 2015 due to near wellbore treatment.

To verify this assumption about skin-factor growth, several specific targeted well tests have been performed, including pressure build-up curve and the pressure drawdown curve. Let us consider an example of such a well test. For the quantitative interpretation of bottomhole pressure measurements together with the well production rate changes, the method “Decline-Analyze” and algorithms of the software complexes “Topaze” and “Saphir” of Kappa Engineering company were used. An overview of the study is shown in Fig. 8.
