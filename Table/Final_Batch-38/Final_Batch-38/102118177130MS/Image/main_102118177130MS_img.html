The preferential direction of minimum horizontal stress was determined in the direction of the 30° ± 10° azimuth, as shown in Figure 9.

Consequently, average stress magnitudes from a neighboring field (e.g. Eden Yuturi) were used, assuming a similar behavior to determine the magnitude of horizontal stresses in the Apaika A-011 well; and to validate both the rock properties and the stresses, a wellbore stability analysis was done to represent the borehole behavior during drilling. Figure 10 shows the wellbore stability analysis.

Finally, a system with the following characteristics was chosen: PowerJet Nova 4512, HMX, 21.0 g, OD 4.5", 135/45° Phasing, 12 spf, resulting in an average penetration of 21.21" with an average rock diameter of 1.02". Figure 11 shows the advanced perforating design.

Average particle size distribution showed a D50 of 420 μm, classifying it as a medium grain sandstone; however, there is significant fine-grain distribution including clays, with a D90 of 81 μm (see Figure 12).

Using the M1 Sandstone core analyses, it was described as a clean, non-calcareous, inorganic, dark brown, non-graded, medium to coarse grain, well-sorted, moderately cemented, high porosity sandstone without the presence of fossils. (Figure 13).

To determine critical drawdown (when the first grain of sand is produced), several simulations were performed in Sand Management Advisor based on rock properties, the stress field, reservoir pressure, perforation diameters and grain size (see Figure 14).
