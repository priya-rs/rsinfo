The Karachaganak Field was discovered in 1979 as a world class super-critical condensate reservoir under initial conditions located in the northwestern part of Kazakhstan (Fig. 1). Limited production and exportation from the Karachaganak field in the former Soviet Union (FSU) under the operatorship of KarachaganakGazprom, a subsidiary of Russia’s Gazprom, was begun in the year 1984.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Within the initial completion design dual pressure and temperature (P/T) gauges were deployed. These gauges were positioned above the production packer and were separated by approx. 50m TVD (Appendix 1 Fig. 9 – Wellbore Schematic and Fibre Optic Layout). The gauges provided accurate measurements to monitor reservoir pressure and temperature and also density measurement. The wells were equipped with electronic gauges with a quartz based sensing element. These gauges are widely used in the industry and have an extensive track record.
