We consider a simple 2D example to clarify these points. Let us analyze an heterogenous 2D small model with five spot type of pattern, like the one shown in Figure 1. This layer is assumed to start at irreducible water saturation and flooded by four injectors, I1 to I4. Figure 2 displays the evolution of the saturation profile after 23 months of injection. The distorted breakthrough travels to the producers and eventually a situation like the one depicted in Figure 3 is obtained, where the reservoir has high mean water saturation.

The injection profile in this example is modified randomly between certain levels during the numerical experiment. In this case the boundary condition at the producers is negligible bottom hole pressure, which resembles that of an optimally operated well. Production data is generated solving the fluid motion with Schlumberger Eclipse (black oil) on a fine grid. Now, if a simple CRM model [1] is fitted to this example, one obtains the comparison shown in Figure 4. In this Figure we show the history matched data toghether with predicted rates.

Note that the oil cut is estimated after fitting the total produced rates, the decay law involves only three fitting parameters for each producer. Figure 5 displays the simulation results and the simple model fit toghether with the prediction after the training window.

Figure 6 shows a typical output of the anlysis, namely a map of the connectivities in the last period of time (only the most representative values, with a threshold based on duration of the pair, are shown here). In this Figure we plot an arrow of size proportional to the mean associated rate (connectivity times injection) and color depicting the error range. Thus, it easy to identify the main problems in the field by visual inspection.

Eventually, each producer can be explored in further detail after the detection of a problem, the gobal fit yields to the optimum history match at field level, but the individual results derived from it are easely accessible and can be analyzed separately.
