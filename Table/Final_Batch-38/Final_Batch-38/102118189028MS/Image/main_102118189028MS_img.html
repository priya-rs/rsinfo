As can be seen from Table 1 and analysis of the well injection capacity profiles, the injection capacity of wells No. 942, 1184, and 1246 increased (Fig. 1a) and injection capacity of wells No. 1299 and 1410 decreased (Fig. 1b). The increase in the injection capacity of three wells is connected with the presence of carbonate rocks in the well bottom zone of the formation. Thus, the injection capacity of well No. 1184, where rock carbonation is 12.7%, increased more than 6 times. There are no carbonate rocks in the rocks of the well bottom zone of wells No. 1299 and 1410.

Since visualization of stream lines allows tracing the path of fluid advance from key wells to producing wells, the calculation of stream function values for each grid cell superimposed on the studied area makes it possible to classify the grid cells as drained and undrained ones. The map of current formation fluid map is constructed in accordance with distribution of the stream function values along the area (Fig. 6). The area of drained cells will make the area covered by stimulation. The zone not covered by stimulation is formed by poorly drained cells. This approach provides solution to the problem of visualization of zones of the area covered by stimulation. On Figure 6, the cells with high drainage capacity are marked with red color and weakly drained cells are blue colored.
