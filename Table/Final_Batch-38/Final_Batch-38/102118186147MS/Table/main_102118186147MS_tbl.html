As a reference scenario, mid case costs are evaluated, but without any optimization strategies. This will result in the total abex that should be reserved for all decommissioning projects. The costs considered as mid case input are given in Table 1 – all costs are estimated average costs. Note that two classes of platforms are considered: small and large. The classification is based on the weight, as a rough differentiation between platforms which will be relatively easy to remove, and which will take some effort and need more expensive heavy lift vessels. A topside that weighs over 1200 metric tons, typically a limit for sheerleg vessels in the Southern North Sea, is assumed to be a large platform. Only one type of well is considered, assuming that P&A time and costs will average out.

The mid case input parameters have been verified by a selected number of operators. However, most input parameters may vary a lot in reality. Variations in important input parameters could very well change results and general conclusions. Therefore, several runs were done to check if the model is sensitive to certain parameters. Sensitivities were checked by running low and high case values for a selection of parameters, which are given in Table 2. The results of the sensitivity runs for the operator optimization scenario are given in Fig. 10. The effective cost reduction for the operator optimization scenario compared to the reference scenario only varies slightly. The results of the sensitivity runs for the cross operator optimization scenario are given in Fig. 11 and for the decommissioning company scenario in Fig. 12 Again, the relative cost reductions only vary slightly.

If only revenues and operational expenses would be considered, cessation of production takes place on the moment the production is considered to be cash negative. The example of Table B-1 would have a COP after 2020. However, the model has the freedom to keep on producing uneconomically, or consider an early retirement. These strategies are applied when they are economically attractive. Suppose the platform decommissioning costs are EUR 15 million and the associated well decommissioning costs are EUR 18 million. The abex is defined as the total of the decommissioning costs, which is for this case EUR 33 million.
