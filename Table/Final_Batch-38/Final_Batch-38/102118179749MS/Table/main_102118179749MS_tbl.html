The survey plan and frequency is shown in the following Table-1 (Annexure). The objective of these surveys in the injection wells is to address some of the key questions that this project is trying to understand from these pilots. The steam injection rates along with the temperature data are used to estimate how much heat is being injected in the reservoir. The pressure and temperature data obtained in the PLT surveys provide the estimate of downhole steam quality that is also required to understand how much heat is actually being injected in the pay zone down hole.

Table 1 Injection wells

2 Surface injection temperature. Daily Plots Heat Management Input
3 PLT survey Once in each CSS cycle. Every 3 months during steamflood. Plots Zonal injection in multi-zone completion. Down hole temperature and steam quality assessment for Heat Management. Frequency may need review depending on pilot performance.
4 Steam quality. Once in each CSS cycle and every 3 months during steamflood. Samples of feed water for generator, and samples at well head. Analysis of TDS through chosen laboratory to assess steam quality independently through laboratory. Optional. May be discontinued if several tests show consistency with vendor reported value.
5 PNC log Base log in all wells. One survey post each CSS production cycle, optional. Comparison with base log to evaluate steam migration to upper zones across the “Baffle zone”. Evaluation of steam migration to upper zone in upper zone in case of single zone completion, 10 acre pilot. Not needed in multi-zone completion case of 5-acre pilot.
6 CBL/CET log Optional Evaluation of well integrity and steam migration issues.
7 Pressure Fall-Off Optional Fall-Off interpretation for parameters. Relative permeability to steam, skin, steam chamber size, pressure. Central injector may be given priority for evaluation.

The survey plan and frequency is shown in Table-2 (Annexure). The objective of the surveys is to provide data for analysis of the pilot results. Analysis of the information would be much easier for the 10-acre pilot with completion in a single zone. The analysis would be very complex for the 5-acre pilot where the wells are completed in two zones, namely Zone-1A and Zone-1B in commingled flow.
