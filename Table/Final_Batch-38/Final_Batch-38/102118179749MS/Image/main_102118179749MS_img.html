The reservoir consists of two oil bearing zones, ZONE-1 and ZONE-2. The two zones are separated by a 10-20 ft thick shale. Each of the ZONE-1 and ZONE-2 layers are also divided by baffles (Figure 1). The ZONE-1 baffle divides the zones into ZONE-1A and ZONE-1B. The ZONE-2 baffle divides the ZONE-2 layers into ZONE-2A and ZONE-2B.

Figure 2 below displays the extent of a ‘Greater North’ simulation model built to evaluate different completion scenarios for the pilots. The model included the four old CSS wells (OWell-1, OWell-2, OWell-3 and OWell-4) and five of the six horizontal wells (HWell-5, HWell-4, HWell-3, HWell-2 and HWell-1) and the boundaries of the two new pilot areas. The sixth horizontal well, HWell-6, drilled north-west of the model area was not included the simulation model.

The oil in lower zones is more viscous than the oil in the shallower zones. Table 1 below presents the average oil properties at the reservoir temperature of 90 °F. Figure 3 also presents the oil viscosity change with temperature for the zones for temperatures ranging from 100 to 800 °F.

Specific reference was made to RFT data available from the development (20-acre pattern) wells. Of particular importance was the timing of the RFT data set relative to the onset of production (February 2011 onwards) in the adjacent HWell-5 to -1 horizontal wells (Figure 4).

With the exception of the wells VWELL-100 and VWELL-102, all the RFTs suggested no pressure drop in the ZONE-1B and ZONE-2 zones. Since the ZONE-1/ZONE-2 baffles and the mid shale were set to be impermeable in the model, no pressure depletion in ZONE-1B and ZONE-2 zones was shown in the model. Figures 5 and 6 compare the RFT and simulated pressure data for the wells VWELL-100 and 102.

Three different production/injection scenarios were used in the forecast: Vertical Expansion (layerwise) of ZONE-1A & ZONE-1B, Simultaneous ZONE-1A & ZONE-1B and Simultaneous ZONE-1 & ZONE-2.
