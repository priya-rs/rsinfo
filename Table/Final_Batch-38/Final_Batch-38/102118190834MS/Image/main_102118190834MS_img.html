The Cygnus Field1 (Fig 1) is located in United Kingdom blocks 44/11a and 44/12a, in licence P1055, approximately 12km north of the Munro Field and 15km northwest of the Tyne Field. The Field is operated by Neptune E & P UK Ltd on behalf of partnership (Neptune Energy and Spirit Energy UK).

Catto R. Taggart Catto . 2017 Petroleum Geology of the Cygnus gas field, UK North Sea: from discovery to development. In: Bowman, M. & Levell, B. (Eds) Petroleum Geology of NW Europe: 50 Years of Learning - Proceedings of the 8th Petroleum Geology Conference . Geological Society of London

Cygnus is a light, sweet gas. The reported liquid yield is between 1 and 3 bbl/mmscf and associated condensate is light and it is also assumed that the same gas composition (Fig 2) is present in the Leman and Carboniferous reservoirs.

Technical Memorandum Cygnus Fluid Chemistry by Ester Soru November 4 2011

Composition of formation water (Fig 3) samples from 44/12a-2 and 44/12a-3 wells and MDT pressure data support the view that a sole formation water composition exists throughout the field. Both Leman and Carboniferous reservoirs are supported by the same aquifer. The fluid sample indicates high concentration of Sodium and Chloride ions compared to the other ions present in the fluid. Also, the Cygnus formation fluids contain a high amount of total dissolved solids (Fig 4) averaging at 320,000 mg/l.

Prior to installing the completion string for the first development well offshore, multiple factory acceptance and stack up test were carried out at the workshop and test rig onshore (Attachment 1 - 3). The aim was to test and confirm the integrity of the sub-assemblies, to ascertain the robustness of the operational procedures and simulate the deployment of the upper and lower completion strings. Lessons were captured and updated in the completion execution programs.
