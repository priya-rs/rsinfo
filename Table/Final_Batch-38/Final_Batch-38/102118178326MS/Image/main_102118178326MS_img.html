The H reservoir is located in shallow waters offshore Nigeria. The reservoir has a 4-way fault dip closure and was initially undersaturated (Figure 1). The reservoir is divided into two main zones: HA zone and HC zone. There is also a thin zone (HB) which is absent in some of the wells (Figure 2). The reservoir was discovered in 1970 and put on production in 1971. Peripheral water injection commenced in 1984 to boost the reservoir pressure and improve oil recovery (Figure 3). The recovery factor to date is estimated at 51%.

The H reservoir is located in shallow waters offshore Nigeria. The reservoir has a 4-way fault dip closure and was initially undersaturated (Figure 1). The reservoir is divided into two main zones: HA zone and HC zone. There is also a thin zone (HB) which is absent in some of the wells (Figure 2). The reservoir was discovered in 1970 and put on production in 1971. Peripheral water injection commenced in 1984 to boost the reservoir pressure and improve oil recovery (Figure 3). The recovery factor to date is estimated at 51%.

Initially, the history match was done for only observed pressures and production data. A good reservoir-wide match for pressure, gas production and water cut was obtained (Figure 4). On the well level, seven of the wells had fair-to-good water cut match while two wells (HC-104 and HA-30) had poor water cut match. The model could not match the water breakthrough in HC-104 even after five years of actual water production. In addition the model produced excessive water in HA-30 even though the well never produced water in its production life.

See Figures 5 and 6 for the pressure/production history match plots for HC-104 and HA-30, respectively. Attempts were made to improve the history match by adjusting residual oil saturation, but to no avail. It was then decided to check the model performance with respect to matching observed fluid-contacts in the wells. The result showed that only two wells had fair-to-good fluid-contact match in both HA and HC zones while the rest of the wells (six) had poor fluid-contact match. It was then decided to actively history match the fluid-contact in addition to pressure and production.

The upward adjustment of permeability in HC and downward adjustment of permeability in HA essentially resulted in the redistribution of the injection water such that more water was injected in HC leading to water breakthrough in HC-104 and less water was injected into HA leading to no water breakthrough in HA-30 (See figures 7, 8 and 9). The flowing wellhead pressures of the currently flowing wells (HC-104 and HA-111) were also history matched. Figure 10 shows the pressure/production plot for the currently flowing well (HA-111) in which the flowing wellhead pressure (THP) was history matched to resolve gas-lift injection volumes uncertainty. After the permeability adjustment, there was significant improvement in the fluid-contact match for the six wells that earlier had poor fluid-contact match (See Figures 11–18 for the fluid contact/RFT pressure matches).

Simulation-derived average oil saturation maps (at the end of history) were prepared for the HA zone and HC zone respectively. Based on the average oil saturation maps, one new drill and three workover opportunities were identified (Figures 19 and 20). These opportunities were then assessed with the history-matched simulation model. Based on the simulation model, the do-nothing case oil recovery factor is 56.5% of original oil in place, while the incremental oil recovery from the identified opportunities is 7.5% of original oil in place. Thus, the total estimated oil recovery factor with the identified opportunities is 64% of original oil in place.
