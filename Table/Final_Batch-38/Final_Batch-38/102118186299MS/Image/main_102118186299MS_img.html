Handil field, discovered in 1974, is an oil and gas field located in internal axis of modern Mahakam Delta in East Kalimantan, Indonesia. The field comprises over 550 hydrocarbon accumulations from depth ~ 200mSS to 3300mSS in structurally stacked and compartmentalized fluvio-deltaic sands. Handil reservoirs were divided into several zones, Shallow (very shallow, shallow & upper), Main, Lower, and Deep zone. Shallow zone to Main zone are known as “Oil Pool”, while the bottom of Main zone down to Deep zone are known as “Gas Pool” (Fig.1).

Shallow reservoirs are supported by strong aquifer; hence pressure data is not reliable to assist in defining the compartmentalization in the same reservoir segment. The ultimate drainage area of existing production wells was then introduced to the net actual pay map to estimate the future contact movement. It was calculated based on estimated ultimate reserves (EUR) of each production wells in reservoir level and structurally limited by the top perforation. Decline curve analysis (Fig.5) was used to calculate the 1EUR.

The actual net pay oil map was then cut with the ultimate drainage radius until top of perforation, resulted as sweet spot map or un-swept area which represent the future remaining net pay map. Figure 6 illustrates the sweetspot definition and the example of actual sweet spot map.

Blind test for the sweetspot result was performed on 2016 wells to check the accuracy of the netpay prediction from the geomodel. From this blind test, it can be concluded that the shallow geomodel gives quite good net pay prediction despite its tendency to be slightly pessimistic. The main uncertainties are the fluid status which affected by production and sand occurrence in some reservoirs. Summary of blind test result can be seen in the Figure 7.

The estimated maximum wells for each reservoir compartment will be used as control limit in determine well catalogue. This value will limit the number of wells that penetrating each reservoir compartment. Monte Carlo technique was used to define the maximum wells for each reservoir compartment. Main parameters that used in the calculation are future ROIP and future well reserves. Figure 8 illustrates the workflow to calculate the maximum wells for each compartment.

Figure 9 shows example of future well or perforation reserves calculation methodology. Blind test was performed in 8 reservoir segment to check the validity of the reserves calculation methodology. Main uncertainties in this methodology are the production allocation and the estimated ultimate reserves. Blind test was performed on 2012 – 2014 wells, which have less uncertainty in production allocation since we have representative PLT data and the production decline trend already obvious. Based on the blind test result (Fig.10), post drilling reserves estimation is in good agreement with the actual reserves realization, total difference ~6%.
