In order to test the efficiency of laterals, the base case model was also predicted for ten years as a continuing stripper well. In the base case model, oil production declined gradually, as expected over the ten years’ prediction. When not drilling thru hard inclusions the drilling rate through the Penrose averaged about 2.5 to 3.5 ft/hr, with a peak rate of about 5.5 ft/hr. The remainder of the laterals were drilled to 14.5 ft past the casing. Besides the depth indicators on the surface unit, these distance figures were confirmed by tell-tale signs on the drill-string when it landed out on the top of the whipstock. Total fluid pumped to drill the six Penrose laterals was about 850 bbls, or about 140 bbls per lateral, all of which was being recirculated. As this was a cased hole completed well, some of the total fluid pumped was associated with milling a hole in the case to access the formation. If one excludes this fluid, the equivalent fluid to drill each lateral averaged closer to 70 bbls. For purposes of drilling fluid, untreated fresh water was used for the majority of the procedure as the operator believed that the risk of swellable clays or formation damage was negligible. A surfactant was added to the drilling fluid, however, to increase the lubricity for the mud motor. Table 4 provides details of the 15 laterals drilled into the Delaware zone.
