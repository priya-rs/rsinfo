The most important reservoir is the Morichal member (it contains 75 % of STOIIP). It is divided informally into lower, middle and upper units. The main depositional system in the Morichal member is fluvial-deltaic. The dominant reservoir facies in Morichal member are meandering and braided fluvial channels sandstones, which form stacked reservoir layers up to 300 ft thick separated by sealing shales. Each layer has good internal connectivity (Briceno et al., 1998; Dusseault et al., 2008; Santos, 1991; Yannimaras et al., 2006). Figure 1 shows the log response and reservoir zonation in an example well for this field.

The area for the pilot test was selected base on a reservoir quality index (Molina et al., 2009), representing the potential for thermal EOR technologies. The proposed approach is to drill all the wells in the Morichal Lower unit, which is the member with more sand continuity and better reservoir properties in average. Figure 2 shows the distribution of the quality index in the reservoir, and the location of the selected well pads in the better area (red color).

Well pad O-05-02 is asymmetrical, with longer wells (4,500 ft) in the left side than in the right side (2,200 ft). Well pad N-05-01 is crossed by one of the main fault in the zone. Right side wells are longer (3,000-3,300 ft) than the left side wells (2,000-3,300 ft). In both well pads the well distance is 300 m. The wellheads are located in the center of the well pad. Figure 3 shows the lay out of the wells in the two well pads selected for the CSS pilot test, O-05-02 and N-05-01.

Figure 4 shows the well pad O-05-02 finally selected for the CSS pilot test, with the coded specific steam flowrates to be injected in each well following the design of experiments, and their respectives lengths. Ordinal numbers to the right and left sides show the proposed steam injection sequence.

The CSS pilot test proposed considers 3 complete cycles. So, taking into account the previous 4 years of cold production, the total duration of the CSS pilot test should be about 11 years. Figure 5 shows the schematic planning for the whole CSS pilot test, implemented in well pad O-05-02.

Due to the CSS nature (a single well for injection and production), two different types of upper completions will be used. Figure 6 shows the schematic drawing for the injection stage. It consists of a pre-insulated tubing to reduce the heat losses, with a metal to metal seal.

For the production stage, it will have a tubing string with a metal to metal progressive cavity pump (PCP). Additionally, it is recommended to install a downhole gas separator at the pump inlet, to increase the pump efficiency. Figure 7 shows the schematic drawing for the production stage.

The pilot test surface facilities should treat the hot production in such a way that it could be sent againg to the CPF under the original design specifications. Figure 8 shows the surface facilities flow diagram proposed for the CSS pilot test.

A very detailed monitoring plan, supported by reservoir simulation studies, has been generated, taking into account state-of-the-art monitoring technologies, with the objective to capture the most important parameters to assess this pilot test. Figure 9 shows the proposed monitoring technologies.
