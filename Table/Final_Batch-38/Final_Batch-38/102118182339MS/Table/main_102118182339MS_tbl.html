The Windalia is a greenish-grey sandstone of very fine grain size. The rock is Cretaceous in age and deposited in a middle to outer marine shelf environment. It is sealed by the overlying Windalia Radiolarite and Gearle Siltstones and trapped by a three-way anticline with structural fault closure in the south. Average permeability and porosity are 5.7 mD and 28% respectively (Crank 1973). Average gross thickness is 120 ft (36.6 m). The reservoir fluid is a light 36° API oil with solution GOR of 220 scf/bbl, with initially saturated reservoir pressure of 995 psia (Alexander et al. 1981; Emanuel et al. 1993).
