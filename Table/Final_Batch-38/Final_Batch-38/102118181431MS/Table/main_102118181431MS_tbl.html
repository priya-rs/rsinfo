The key contribution of this research is leveraging the capability of DTS, DAS, and the two in combination to generate multiple flow profile interpretations over identical horizontal well segments and time periods using a variety of physical principles. Utilizing multiple algorithms, that are not strongly dependent, results in higher-confidence, quantitative steam flow profile interpretations for use in steamflood and SAGD operations. Table 2 summarizes the physical principles of those flow profiling algorithms. Several steady state (SS) and transient (TR) models are being utilized to interpret the surveillance data. Selected algorithms are described in more detail and applied to DTS and DAS acquired in the experimental injectors.

In Table 3 the flow profiling results for well "A" are summarized indicating toe-biased steam conformance. The confidence value for the well "A" steam injection flow profile is 93% based upon the Euclidian clustering statistics. This toe biased heating effect is being caused by buoyant steam preferentially exiting the toe-high horizontal injector. A similar analysis was conducted for well "B" which has heel-biased steam. Well "B" also has a toe-high geometry but encountered depletion and high gas saturations in the heel region. The depletion and gas saturations caused steam to preferentially exit the heel of injector "B".
