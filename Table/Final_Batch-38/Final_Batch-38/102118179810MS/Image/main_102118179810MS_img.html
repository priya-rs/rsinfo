Currently the available facilities accommodate around 45 production wells on an area approximately 3 square kilometers (1, 5 km × 2 km). The oil treatment facilities are situated at the edge of the production well area to south east (Figure 3). The station is currently producing oil with associated gas. The gas produced is sour gas (H2S) and treated (sweetened) and reused, exported or flared during down times.

The QRA study demonstrated that two of the three evaluated locations (i.e. option 2B and option 3) are just above 1E-04 per year LSIR (Location Specific Individual Risk) contour and 1E-04 p3er year of 650ppm of H2S whereas option 2D is overlapping with the above mentioned contours (i.e. yellow zone in Figure 4). In general, having the proposed locations, which has lower LSIR contour than 1E-04 per year of 650ppm, would allow construction of the new process units without a need to shut-down the existing facilities.

Rigorous risk and uncertainty assessment was carried out and results are summarized in Figure 8. A total of 41 risk and opportunities have been identified in project of which 22 falls into the overall High category.

Concept selection for Phase 3B, however, depends mainly on Phase 3A injection performance. Phase 3A will provide valuable information to allow optimization of Phase 3B. The key for success of Phase 3A is the ability to measure the effectiveness of the MGI, in terms of translating the measured oil rate and GOR in terms of sweep efficiency. This requires a detailed history match. Updated subsurface models, updated cost estimates, and an assessment of gas availability will be the input into Phase 3B concept selection and FID.
