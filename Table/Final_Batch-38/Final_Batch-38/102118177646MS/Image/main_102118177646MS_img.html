A one bit run was achieved by implementing an application-specific hybrid drill bit. The revolutionary PDC and roller-cone hybrid drill bit combines roller-cone and fixed cutter elements (Fig. 2). It is designed to take advantage of the best attributes of both bit types; the fast, aggressive drilling action of the PDC and the smooth, low-torque action of the TCI.

The central portion of the borehole is cut solely by PDC cutters on the primary blades while the more difficult to drill outer portion is disintegrated by the combined action of the cutting elements on the rolling cutters and fixed blades. Bit specifications are listed in Fig. 3. The roller cones in this bit are equipped with journal bearing, as commonly used in large-size bits, and a metal face seal, which enables more revolutions.

After the run, the hybrid bit cutting structure showed minimum wear on the PDC and TCI cutters. As seen in Fig. 4, no impact damage was found on the cutting structure, indicating the hybrid bit was very stable and reacted very smoothly when drilling through the interbedded formations. For its cutting structures, it was graded 1-1-NO-A-X-NO-TD for PDC and 1-2-WT-G-E-I-BT-TD for roller cones. The bearing was still effective and there was no seal failure, proving the reliability of the metal face seal bearing package.

From 1057 m to around 1975 m, the formation consisted mainly of limestone. With weight on bit (WOB) ranging between 20-30 klb, 180 RPM, 1200 gal/min flow, the bit reached an average ROP of 60 ft/hr (as seen in Fig. 5), similar to the 18 m/hr ROP, the best ROP in offset wells. This interval required the application of more energy during drilling, shown in MSE chart below.

In the shale formation, shown in Fig. 6, WOB was applied more than the previous interval, ranging 40-50 klb, with the same RPM and flow, ROP dropping to less than 50 ft/hr (similar to 15 m/hr). Torque remained stable at 10 klbf, and the energy needed to drill this interval was less than the upper part, as seen in MSE chart.

The performance indicators used were ROP, on-bottom cost per meter, and vibration. The comparison shows that the hybrid bit (in well B-1) drilled 45% faster than the best offset well, which had average ROP 18 m/hr using 437 IADC code roller-cone bit and a PDC, and 200%faster than well A-8, which had average ROP 9 m/hr using 425 IADC code roller-cone bit and a 5-bladed PDC. The hybrid run cost per meter was 43% lower than well A-10, 48% lower than well A-9, and 68%lower than well A-8. Figs. 7 and 8 show the ROP and cost-per-meter comparison.
