OML58 is an NNPC/Total E&P Nigeria Limited Joint-Venture lease operated since 1966 by TEPNG. OML 58 is located in the Niger Delta about 56km from Port Harcourt as shown in Figure 1 below. It consists of 3 oil fields (Obagi, Erema and Olo) and 4 gas fields (Obagi Gas Cap, Obagi Deep, Ibewa and Ubeta). Oil was discovered in 1964 and production started in 1966 through Ogbogu Flow Station (OFS) and reacheda pick of 70 000 bpd in 1983.

OML58 gas fields have different Condensate to Gas Ratio (CGR). It varies from dry gas in Obagi Gas Caps to High CGR for Ibewa and Obagi Deep reservoirs (Figure 6). Even within the same field different reservoirs may show different depletion trends and therefore different evolution of CGR with time. In order to maximize condensate production from OML58 gas fields, an optimization algorithm was integrated in the IAM. At each time step, and after getting reservoir pressure, the simulator compares CGR from different wells and gives priority to high CGR ones. Monthly, this priority list is sent to site and wells are operated accordingly.

WELL-1 is one of the infill wellsplanned to be drilled in Obagi Gas Cap. The reservoir target is an oil rim with a gas cap. Oil production started in 1966 with natural depletion, and then water injection started in 2008. Total well completed in the oil interval reaches 18 with actual cumulative oil production of 77.5 Mbbl which corresponds to 58% oil recovery factor. Reservoir pressure decreased from 265 bar to 125 bar before starting the water injection. Currently it is estimated at 140 bar (Figure 8). Due to the low reservoir pressure WELL-1 cannot be producing the gas at High Pressure (HP) and needs to be produced at Medium Pressure (MP).

Ubeta is a gas condensate field located 8 km from Obagi flow station. It was discovered in 1964 and 4 appraisal wells were drilled. It consists of 5 major reservoirs, excellent permeability sandstones, at depth range 2600-3300 mss (Figure 9). The need to develop Ubeta has been identified for the upcoming years to be able to meet gas supply obligation (LNG+Domestic gas). The development requires the drilling of an optimum number of wells and the construction of a pipeline from Ubeta to OTC.
