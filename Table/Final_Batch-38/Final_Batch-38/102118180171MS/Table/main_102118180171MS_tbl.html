Horizontal drilling has become an economic reality, and waterflood as a secondary recovery method is a proven cost-effective method for improving hydrocarbon recovery. However, this does not mean that horizontal wells with waterflooding guarantee the economic success of a project. Multiple realizations and development schemes must be evaluated through a series of economic filters such as net present value and economic limit analysis, sensitivity and scenario analysis on influential parameters such as the oil price, and operating cost.
