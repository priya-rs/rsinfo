The new Burgan wells profiles are targeting deeper formation, where as the previous majority targeted a shallower formation. Hence, hole sections will be drilled deeper, including the 16 inch intermediate hole section (Figure 2). Moreover, the well profiles will be changed from vertical to slanted profiles. This requires the 16 inch hole to be drilled with a directional assembly.

The torque range required to drill with a PDC is higher than that for a roller cone bit. Large diameter PDC bits tend to generate high drilling vibrations and stabilizing the BHA becomes increasingly challenging. Figure 3 shows plotted results for torque ranges and fluctuations in a drilling simulation lab. The experiment conducted illustrates that when drilling through inter-bedded formations, at constant WOB and RPM, Torque fluctuations are a function of bit type. Roller cone tend to provide the least torque fluctuations in comparison to PDC and hybrid bits, as per the simulation lab results.
