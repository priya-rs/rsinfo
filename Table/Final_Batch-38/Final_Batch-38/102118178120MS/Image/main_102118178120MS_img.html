Effective Half Frac Length (EHFL): It is the length of the stimulated rock volume (SRV) on each side of the wellbore which mainly contributes to the estimated ultimate recovery (EUR) of a well. Understanding effective half frac length properly is required for optimizing the well spacing for the wells drilled within the same stratigraphic level (Figure-2).

The strength of the microseismic events can vary considerably, resulting in an apparently greater event density close to the sensor array. Understanding the detection limit is important when interpreting fracture geometry and comparing stimulation treatments (Cipolla, 2011). The magnitude-versus-distance can be used to estimate a minimum event magnitude that can be detected throughout the total range of distances where microseismic activity has been detected (Figure-3; Step-1).

Before applying this approach to estimate the effective frac half-length, SRV at each 10 ft. interval from both the sides of the wellbore are estimated (as shown by different color boxes). Incremental SRV%of each interval is then calculated with respect to the previous interval and plotted against the half-frac-length and frac height (Figure-4 A, B). It is observed that near the wellbore the SRV is much higher and when we move away from the well bore, the incremental SRV is decreasing. At a certain frac half length, the incremental SRV% becomes negligible and almost constant (Figure-4 C).

Out of these nine stratigraphic units, the lower 4-5 units are effectively drained by the well placed in the lower part of the reservoir. But the upper four units remain un-drained. Out of these four units or zones, zone-3A & zone-3B are identified as best part within the un-drained zone in terms of hydrocarbon filled porosity, TOC, Vclay and hydrocarbon inplace. Any well to be drilled to exploit the above said un-drained zone of the reservoir should be placed within zone-3A & zone-3B (Figure-11)(Sahoo et al, 2015).

With down spacing, the well density increases and ultimately EUR/well decreases due to well interference. However the field recovery goes northward as more reservoir volume is stimulated by drilling more of wells (Figure-12). So the exploitation of the un-drained portion of the shale reservoir through down spacing and staggered drilling is mostly dependent on EUR degradation model and gas/oil price environment.
