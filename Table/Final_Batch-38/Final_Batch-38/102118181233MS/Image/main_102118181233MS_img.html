The Permian Delaware basin is one of the most prolific unconventional oil and gas basins in the US. Fig.1 –shows Eddy and Lea Counties of the Delaware basin, as the leading oil producing counties in the Permian.

The Delaware Basin is a multi-stacked play prospect; that contains several productive zones (see Fig.2&3 below). Hydrocarbon producing zone thickness as high as 1800 feet are common in the basin; comparing this to the Bakken Shale highs of 120 feet in thickness, and Eagle Ford Shale formations of 300 feet thick (NGI’Shale Daily, 2016); the Delaware basin is no doubt a world class oil and gas basin.
