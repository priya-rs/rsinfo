In this study, the example of systematic approach for the investigation of parameters space is proposed in order to found the optimal development strategy for 2 reservoirs A and B. The commercially available software was used to parametrize the task in standalone models cut from integrated reservoir simulation model [1]. Software allows to sumbit multiple runs and analsyze results of all runs together (Fig 1). Another advantage was availability of full parametrization of reservoir simulaton model input files. But it is extremely complex task to design efficient water injection system for such reservoir. In order to solve such task, the systematic multi-realization approach with variable positions of water injectors was used. 5 water injection wells (4 in the top reservoir and 1 in the bottom one) were considered (Fig 2). One of coordinates for each injector was selected as variable (modifier) in order to be able to evaluate performance of injection system with different positions of injection wells. All injection wells were placed near the main development area to support pressure from the flanks and provide sweep in the direction towads producers.

Reservoir B consists of 2 hydraulically vertically separated units. Main dveleopment unit has around 30 ft net pay, homogenious properties and porosity above 20%. Crestal gas injection and periphery water injection are integrated in the scope of base case Reservoir B development scenario. There are 3 gas injectors in the central part of the reservoir (Fig 7) and 5 water injectors on the edge. 10 oil prodcuers are located between gas and water injectors by circle. Gas injection in the central part supposed to move the oil from the central part towards the producers. Due to the miscibility [2] of the injected gas with reservoir oil, good sweep efficiency could be achived. An edge water injection is supporting pressure on the flanks and keeps the gas front from surpassing the oil producers.

Low net oil thickness around 3 gas injectors in the crestal part of the reservoir is indicating good sweep efficiency of the displacement due to the missibility of the gas and reservoir oil (Fig 9 a). As the displacement progressing (Fig 9 b), the sweeped area around the injectors is growing. On the next steps (Fig 9 c, d), the gas has sweeped almost all central part and only few areas with high oil net thickness left.

Minimum Miscibility Pressure (MMP) for injected gas and reservoir oil is around 4500+-100 psia. So after the start-up of gas injection (Fig 10 a), the HCPV averaged reservoir pressure is above MMP. As the development progressing (Fig 10 b) the reservoir pressure is keeping clearly above MMP with sufficient marging. Futher developmet (Fig 10 c) causing reservoir pressure decrease, but the pressure starts to be below below the MMP only in the drainage area of production wells and major part of the oil was displaced gas. Displacement is quite homogenius up to the end (Fig 10 d), and HCPV averaged reservoir pressure is above MMP until 2044. Such development strategy with targeting the average reservoir pressure and injection of gas and water based on this target, allowing to maximize oil recovery.
