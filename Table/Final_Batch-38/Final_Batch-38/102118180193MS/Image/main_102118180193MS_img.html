The Komsomolskoye oil field is located in a remote location of Western Kazakhstan (Figure 1) onshore on the Buzachi Peninsula, some 350 km from the regional city of Aktau and 110 km from the nearest village Kyzan (Figure 2).

Furthermore, the field’s remote location required significant additional investments to connect it to the export pipeline and road system. Therefore, in 1987 the seven dry wells were abandoned and the remaining 10 wells were suspended, or ʺconservedʺ. The conservation period lasted from 1987-2003 and ended with re-entry and re-activation of these wells as part of the existing Komsomolskoye development. See Figures 3 and 4.
