The Oglan 2 Dir. appraisal well was drilled by AOE in 2014. The well confirmed the presence of an interesting accumulation of heavy oil (15.5°API, about 190cp at Reservoir Conditions) in the Hollin Fm (Early Cretaceous). Five bottom hole cores were cut into the reservoir Formation. The well test resulted in1100 BOPD with PI=1.8bbl/day/psia. The development of the Field is planned by the drilling of horizontal production wells. The main Reservoir Information, Field Location and well logs and cores information are described in Figuer 1.

The resulting horizontal stresses azimuth (Figure 2) have been checked and constrained against data contained in1, 2, resulting into Sh direction 20°N and, correspondingly, SH direction 110°N. The anisotropy analysis from advanced sonic log confirms the presence of small anisotropy, again in agreement with the literature available data mentioned above.

In Figure 3 are illustrated, versus depth, the results of the UCS derived from the scratch tests, the UCS from uniaxial tests (UCS Lab), the sonic log (DTC), the compressional travel time (Lab DTC) measured in laboratory and the gamma ray (GR). It appears that UCS values from scratch (at samples depths) are in accordance with the ones obtained from triaxial tests; moreover, the high resolution of UCS from scratch strongly evidences the degree of strength heterogeneity of the Hollin formation and that the lowest UCS value is around 2200 psi. Each color of the UCS scratch curves in the figure corresponds to each of the core tested.

Once it is established that sand control is needed, the subsequent step is to define the most suitable sand control strategy according to the well/reservoir characteristics. The sand control completion selection is based on Eni Sand Control procedures, and the related decision tree, illustrated in Figure 4, was used for choosing the best among the various sand control solutions.

 

In Ecuador, about 50 Fields are producing from Hollin Reservoir and most of the Fields corresponds to the Play Central - Corridor Sacha Shushufindi while Oglan is located in to the Play Occidental (Subandean), as outlined in Figure 6. Hollin is generally known as consolidated sandstone formation and normally no sand production is associated. Indeed, Agip Oil Ecuador produces also from Hollin Reservoir in the Villano Field, and during 17 years of production no sanding problems were reported.
