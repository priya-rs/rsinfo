Oil viscosities range from 5 cp to 10,000 cp (Figure 3, Table 1). Permafrost typically extends down to approximately 2000 feet so there is a strong temperature impact on oil viscosity at shallow depths. The median oil viscosity is approximately 150 cp for the resource with approximately half of the original oil in place having viscosity lower than this.

Our field data shows approximately 20% waterflood recovery at 0.9 HCPVI as compared to case R3 of 35% recovery in Table 3 from SPE paper 107163 (Al-Qurani 2007). This paper is an interesting analysis of liquid CO2 EOR that confirms many of our findings. However, the paper results are optimistic for the actual Schrader Bluff complex fluids and depositional environment. Still the results are useful to compare and contrast with the results shown by our analysis.
