The analysis showed that none of the analogue reservoirs were filled to spill and this helped to rule out the use of the spill point as a constraining factor for hydrocarbon limit. Figure 1 below is a display of the hydrocarbon column trend in Okoro field. This analysis gives a range of hydrocarbon column thicknesses between 135 – 200 ft. which results in a hydrocarbon contact prediction between 9295 – 9360ftss for the B3.2 reservoir.

Strong continuous high amplitude was observed from the crest of the structure to about 9345ftss for B3.2, as such 9345ftss was adopted as high case hydrocarbon water contact i.e. maximum possible hydrocarbon fill. Furthermore, the clouding pattern of the data points into 2 distinctive groups (shown by purple line in figure 2) is indicative of a 2 fluid system (gas and water).
