This stage involved the use of analogue Hydrocarbon columns (HC) from a matured field with similar structural settings to bench mark the possible HC columns in the subject field (Yoko). This method was principally based on the sealing integrity of two critical faults ‘‘A & C’’ (Figure 5) that control hydrocarbon culminations in the target reservoirs. The STAR (Stochastic Trap Analysis and Risking) tool in Petrel was deployed to carry out the analysis using the Juxtapositions option due to the reservoirs stacking patterns.

The STAR scenario was used to evaluate the contacts based on the Net-to-Gross models for different reservoirs (Figure 6).

This method was used to predict contacts in the X1000, X2000 and X3000 reservoirs using the amplitude attribute from updated time and depth structure maps. The X1000 reservoir showed conformity of amplitude to structure (Figure 7).
