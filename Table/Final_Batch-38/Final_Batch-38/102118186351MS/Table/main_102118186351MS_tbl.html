All wells are vertical wells perforated in the entire Z46CH zone. ASP pilot injection sequence was modelled as shown in Table 4 below. The pre-flush brine injection was carried out for 1.5 years until the water cut in the model was above 90% and average remaining oil saturation was of the order of Sorw (i.e. ~30%). Following the pre-flush was 0.3 pore volume of ASP slug injection. The ASP slug was followed by polymer chase injection for 1.2 years. No attempt was made at tapering the polymer drive in this phase of the work.

The Dykstra Parsons coefficient, VDP is used to quantify the range of permeability heterogeneity that might be detrimental to the ASP process (Dykstra, H. and Parsons, R.L. 1950). For Z64CH layer in UZSD, VDP range of 0.3 to 0.8 was calculated from well logs of RA106, RA135, and RA263, representing mid, high, and low case properties as listed in Table 5 below. To evaluate the impact of permeability contrast on flood performance, the reference case sector model was populated with permeability of RA263 and RA135, respectively, to test the low and high case permeability contrast. The results of the analysis showed that the remaining oil saturation (ROS) in the sections of the UZSD that have been swept by water to date is in the 20% - 40% range.
