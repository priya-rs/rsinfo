A polymer flood in Bhagyam would limit polymer injection to the oil leg. This would promote sweep within a closer well spacing and avoid the loss of polymer to the aquifer. This is a change from the current process which uses peripheral injection. Two producers, B-113 and B-118, were selected as polymer injectivity test candidates as they were positioned in the central part of field forming a 5 spot pattern with the surrounding wells representative of the field water cut (Fig. 2).

 

The surface facility was designed to give priority to high water quality, good provisions for sampling and the ability to verify the quality of the injection fluid. A provision was made to inject at temperatures above the wax appearance temperature in order to mitigate the risk of near wellbore wax drop-out. There is significant variation in oil viscosity with depth in Bhagyam field; it varies from ~15 cp at crest to ~500 cp at the oil water contact. In order to capture this viscosity variation in the simulation model (Fig. 23), three oil components (Heavy oil, Light oil and Solution gas) were defined and the mole fractions of the oil components were varied with depth.

 

The well injectivity would be significantly impacted by the non-newtonian characteristics of the polymer solution in porous media. In order to decide the optimum level of grid refinement to capture this effect, simulation runs were made with different levels of grid refinement around the well B–118 (Fig. 25). In general, well head injection pressure was found to decrease with increase in level of refinement. The calculated fluid velocity in the grid cells increased with increase in level of refinement and hence level of shear thinning of polymer increased. It was observed that 1/9th refinement of grid size and 1/11th refinement of grid size produced similar results, hence, 1/9th grid size was taken as the optimum level of grid refinement for this case.

During initial injection period, well head injection pressure at 1/3rd grid refinement was found to be higher than well head injection pressure with no refinement (Fig. 26). This behavior was observed as the level of polymer dilution in a coarser grid is greater than that in a refined grid during the initial injection period.
