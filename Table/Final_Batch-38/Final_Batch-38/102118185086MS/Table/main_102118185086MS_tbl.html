The good result could also be attributed to the use of low salinity water (about 500 ppm) which could alter the reservoir to a more water-wet condition. The oil production performance at the end of the simulation run shows the importance of increasing patterns to increase recovery which incidentally results in a higher volume of CO2 stored. The inclusion of a CO2 gas compressor constraint controlled significantly the amount of gas produced as compared with previous studies (Ampomah et al., 2016b). However, the amount of gas produced was not fully recycled due to the limited number of CO2 flood patterns. The detailed description of various scenarios is summarized in Table 2.

Optimum operational parameters yielded from this study were similar to Ampomah et al., (2016c). The oil production profile was similar to Case 2 until late 2023 where the optimum parameters started to influence the results (Figure 7). The predominant optimum parameters were the WAG cycle and producer well bottomhole pressures. This optimum case resulted in 25% incremental oil recovery, a significant increase compared to prior cases (Figure 7). Although the amount of CO2 purchased was the same as in Case 2B, a significant amount of the produced CO2 was recycled, resulting in 94% storage (Table 3).
