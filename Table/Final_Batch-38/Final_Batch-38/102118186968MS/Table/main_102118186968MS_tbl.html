The Jatibarang volcanic reservoir exhibits complex geological phenomenon of volcanic reservoirs. In order to assess the potential of the volcanic Jatibarang reservoir, we need to contrast the main lithologies present and the indication of fracture presence. As the result of an integrated study, there were three main facies that are types of tuff (vitric tuff, lithic tuff, and crystal tuff), pyroclastic breccia and lava andesit facies and its characteristic as a reservoir that is shown in Table 1.

Hundreds of individual fractures have been measured and interpreted from image logs to provide an understanding of fractures type, orientation, density, connectivity, and for geomechanical analysis. Besides on image log analysis, fractures in the study area can be divided to become five types of fractures that are conductive fracture, resistive fracture, breakout, tensile fracture, and microfault (see Table 2).
