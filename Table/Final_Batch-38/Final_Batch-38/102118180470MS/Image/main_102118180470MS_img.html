In addition to the mixture velocity limitation of the EWE line, the well stock faces a second constraint due to supply pressure problems experienced from a shared and very long gas lift supply line. From GC2, this line stretches a total of 81,000 ft (15 miles) out to L-pad (see Fig. 4), supplying lift gas to the three other West End pads along the way. Additionally, the line’s diameter reduces along its length, beginning at just over 10-in internal diameter (ID) and ending at just under 8-in ID.

The segments are connected to represent the actual pipeline configuration of the field. It is not necessary from a mathematical solution perspective to have the end model look exactly as the field looks from a bird’s eye point of view, but it has been found it is much more intuitive to work with and understand a model if it has the overall general shape and arrangement as the actual surface network. This is especially true if the system to be modelled is a large one. Nodes are placed to connect the segments together and to act as computation points for the program when it solves for pressures and flow rates. Nodes are also placed at the most upstream points, where individual wells feed into the surface system (see Figs 5, 6, 7, 8, and 9).
