For the study reservoir, the percentage of channel sand body, channel width, extended direction and extending length are important factors that affect the results of development plan. According to the study results, combined with regional sedimentary background and the statistical laws of the width/thickness ratio of deltaic channel sand bodies and its uncertainty, the sedimentary facies maps of each sublayer with different sand body scales are drawn up. Fig.1 shows the Upside model and Downside model of one layer. In Upside model, the percentage of channel sand body is larger, the channel is wider and the extending length is longer. But it is on the contrary for Downside model.

Geological reserve is a parameter that can comprehensively reflect the uncertainty of the entire geological study. For example, the uncertainty of structure and reservoir distribution is bound to affect reserves. In the uncertainty study for geological reserves, the uncertainty of structure, sand body distribution, property cutoffs, OWC and saturation parameters are taken into consideration, in order to comprehensively evaluate the uncertainty of geological reserves. As shown in Fig.2, Upside, Expected and Downside cases and their combination are considered for each uncertainty parameter, in order to evaluate the Upside, Expected and Downside reserves of the reservoir.

For a typical small sector with dense well pattern, the uncertainty of structure, sedimentary facies, reserves and physical parameters is relatively small, but the uncertainty of lateral and vertical continuity of sand bodies is large. During the process of model construction, the lateral and vertical continuity of sand bodies are considered, and three models Upside, Expected and Downside geological models that meet the constrained of well data are established. As can be seen from Fig.3, the lateral and vertical reservoir distributions of the three models are significantly different. For Upside model, the lateral and vertical connection is good and the sand body scale is large, followed by Expected model, and Downside model is the last.

The 8 samples have quite different fluid properties (saturation pressure, gas oil ratio, viscosity, volume factor, etc.) due to different sampling depth. Fluid saturation pressure and gas oil ratio linearly increase with buried depth. However, since the sampling depth of each sample is an interval rather than a specific point, it is difficult to accurately determine the variation relation between saturation pressure, gas oil ratio and buried depth. As shown in Fig.4, the left map shows the relation between saturation pressure and sampling depth of the samples from 6 samples of the south part. In order to characterize the uncertainty of PVT property, the Upside, Expected and Downside relation expressions between saturation pressure and depth are considered respectively, corresponding to the red, green and blue dotted lines in the left map of Fig.4.

Based on the optimum development strategy and three dynamic models, the development performance of basic well pattern - 1000m inverted nine-spot is determined. Fig.5 shows the average daily oil production histogram of the whole reservoir of Upside, Expected and Downside model. It can be seen that the peak daily oil rate is significantly different for the three cases. The peak daily oil rate is 0.39 million barrels/day for Upside model, but only 0.29 million barrels/day for Downside model with 30% in difference of Upside model. Therefore, the influence of parameter uncertainty is prominent.

All the uncertain parameters are ranked according to their influence and occurrence probability. Then, targeted measures are proposed for the uncertain parameters that are easy to occur and have great influence on performance, which can avoid risk. Fig.7 shows the 13 major uncertain parameters and 5 evaluation performance indexes of the reservoir. The influence of the 13 key uncertain parameters on the 5 indexes is evaluated. The right plot of Fig.6 shows the influence of the 13 major uncertain parameters on peak oil production.
