As shown in Fig. 5, the compound meander belt model consists of 4 zones. The bottom surface of zone 2 with grid lines was shown. The plan extent of zone 2 was divided into 2 areas, the simulation area which indicate the channel belt and un-simulation area which indicate inter channel deposits or flood plain. The large scale facies distribution in plane can be determined easily according to limited well logs and seismic attribute study. For example, the maximum amplitude attribute can be used to determine the lateral geometry of meander belt.

Meander loop will be extracted stochastically from database and sequentially placed in the simulation zone. As shown in Fig. 6, a meander loop was selected stochastically from database. A control point in the outer boundary of meander loop was chosen as simulation point.

The point bar model is a surface model. The top surface of single point bar is consistent with top horizon of meander belt model and its horizontal boundary is the same as envelop of meander loop. The bottom surface of single point bar is consistent with bottom horizon of meander belt and its boundary is the enlarged version of meander loop (Fig.7). The original envelop of meander loop will expand outward half width of a channel.

The reservoir is in the Minghuazhen formation at an average depth of 1100m. The vertical reservoir profile is composed of 2-3 fining up cycles. Each cycle corresponds to a single meander belt. Each meander belt is formed due to long term migration, swing and repeatedly cutoff of multiple meandering rivers. The internal architecture of each meander belt becomes more complicated with lateral division of abandoned channels(Fig.8).

The M oilfield was put into production in May 28, 2002, start with vertical wells, nine spot injection pattern, well spacing of 300-400m. The basic well positions were designed through seismic attribute study, which can approximately describe the horizontal distribution of compound channel belt (Fig.9).

After more than 10 years of water-flooding development, NmI3 interval has entered into the high water-cut stage with low production rate. The production dynamic response becomes more and more complex(Fig.10).

How to design horizontal wells to restore the production rate is the most important thing. 90°phase seismic data can recognize the envelope of compound sand body, but hard to recognize single sand body(Fig.11).

Firstly, we need to characterize the single channel belt and construct the compound channel belt model. Because the tops and bases of the channel belts can not be resolved independently from the seismic data, their horizontal extent can be determined. An inter well 3D correlation technique was used to characterize the channel distribution and internal structure of compound meander belt. As shown in Fig.12, the NmI3 zone was subdivided into 2 to 3 sub-zones, the reservoir architecture was well reproduced.

Firstly, a geometry database of meander loop was built. The regular well pattern provides abundant conditioning data which control the placement of meander loop.
