A horizontal well is established in the module. It is 1335.5m deep, its KOP is at the depth of 990m, its horizontal interval is 200m long. Initial reservoir pressure is 13MPa and the perforated interval is in the 3rd layer, as shown in Figure 11.

From Figure 12 we can know that temperature of liquefied CO2 varies a lot with different injection speeds, in that the heat diffusion time is different. The faster CO2 is injected, the shorter the heat diffusion time is, which makes downhole temperature change less. Besides, liquefied CO2 becomes hotter and hotter from KOP (at the depth of 990m) due to the lack of thermal isolation pipes (Figure 13). It is proved that the thermal isolation pipe plays an important role in heat isolation on vertical segments, making the casing temperature close to the initial formation temperature and stabilizing casing as well as cement sheath.

From Figure 14 we can see that CO2 does not dissolve by high rates in heavy oil, while its dissolution is much lower than that in light oil, in that gum and bitumen affects CO2's dissolubility. CO2's dissolubility declines as temperature goes up and improves as pressure increases, which is due to CO2 molecular motion and molecular gaps. In low pressure condition molecular gaps of crude oil are relatively large and CO2 molecular motion affects the dissolubility. As temperature goes up, CO2 molecules move faster and thus hardly dissolve in tight oil.
