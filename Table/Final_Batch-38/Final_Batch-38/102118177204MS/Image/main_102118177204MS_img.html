The objective of this project was to define the optimum exploitation strategy and maximize the recovery factor through different simulation studies with Integrated Asset Modeling approach to evaluate a mature field under reactivation (FDP), water injection as secondary recovery due high pressure decline, for the current operating wells, the definition of the artificial lift strategy was needed to maintain production. Different strategies were evaluated (Figure 1) considering actions to mitigate all these challenges mentioned above taking into account the effects related with: open new wells to production, water injection, artificial lift systems, etc.

Field [1]A[1] was discovered in 1984 and is located in the south region of Mexico with an initial reservoir estimated area of 3.1 km2 (figure 2). The formation is sandstone producing oil of 32° API, and average porosity of 24%, and permeability of 975 mD. The estimation of gross and net thickness was of 48 and 35 m, respectively; while the average field depth was around the 2000 m (Figure 3). In terms of fluid, two wells with PVT analysis were used.

This section describes how the production network was built in order to satisfy the requirements of this study. Basically all the wells were modeled and calibrated independently and the network was built adapted for the artificial lift evaluation of different systems in the same model to be used in the same simulation run, Figures 12 and 13. Several analysis were performed well by well to identify their potential and represent such behavior with models and reproduce the conditions currently measured and reported of the wells.

The construction of the production network was prepared including the new wells that are considered in the field development program and additional networks were built with different artificial lift systems with the purpose of evaluating the profitability and operative response of the wells under several lift systems over time; as described before, and extra network was built with the artificial system in different branches to evaluate the conversion from one system to another under specific conditions of efficiency and water-cut behavior, just interchanging branches triggered through rules using AMS in the coupled model, Figure 14. Moreover this model was used to analyze all the behavior per well and the interaction among them supporting the debottlenecking and production capacity evaluation.

The Figure 15 Appendix A shows the scenarios analysis by plotting NPV (Net Present Value) vs PIR (Project Investment Ratio) vs Np (Total production, shown here as bubble size), then we divided the graph into four quadrants by every mean for each axis. With this we could identify the key projects with more attractive indicators over the average for both indicators. Then well conversion from production to injection would offer the best PIR, mainly the staggered injection offers the best NPV, so the best scenario is the well conversion with staggered injection.

Additionally a sensitivity analysis was performed to validate what parameter can be more sensitive to changes like price, volume, etc., and verify the validity of the scenarios under different circumstances that can affect the decision or the value expected, based on the current variability for example in price on the market, production, associated cost, etc. Figure 16 Appendix A. By the Tornado Diagram, we identify the key variables that have the main impact on the before tax Cash flow and NPV, Production has the main impact and the Oil price. We could also identify that considering a variation for price or production, the project was still profitable.

By the Monte Carlo simulation for the NPV we can make a deeper analysis for the scenarios, we can have not only a single indicator value, but the wide range from de P10 to P90, the standard deviation and the mean value. We can identify the risk (in this case there is no risk for having a no profitable project with this considerations) and make a deeper comparison for the scenarios. We can identify the gas lift – ESP as the higher value, but also the one with the higher risk (higher standard deviation), helping us to make the best decision not only with single values, but with a wide range analysis figure 17 Appendix A.

The initial case to be run is the Base Case that emulate the current condition without any change only maintaining the rates, number of wells, artificial lift systems remain the same to have this scenario as base for comparison purposes.

For the base case describe before, the figure 20 shows the production rate and cumulative of the base case, these results shown the importance about the oil rate control and its optimization, in other words how this production plan can be optimized assigning rates that extend the life, well by well and contribute to improve the production profile. In this figure can be seen how the production trend has a high decline indicating that the reservoir is not capable to sustain this production for a long period, in this case the water will continue increasing and the oil recovery will be affected.

The well chosen for conversion was A-351. This well raised interest from engineering team by the features that it has. Available simulation model was used to perform the sensitivity for this test. The history match for WCUT in the simulation model for this well has a very good quality, the proximity of this well to the WOC and the properties quality to their level of connectivity made it strong candidate. As main result of this exercise, was observed that the sand A2 won pressure and this effect generated a positive impact on wells that are engaged in prediction lack of power with natural flow, figure 21 Appendix A.

Other scenario was to use new wells in the peripheral zone. The best recovery factor appear where different injection times and expenses are combined for a total period of 3 years and 2 injection wells. For this particular case, the effect is positive with 2 wells more than 3 wells, because the location of the third well (INY 3). This position is not very favorable level of connectivity in the area. This aspect highlights the importance of proper geological modeling of properties when the site has a strong stratigraphic component in their properties, figure 22 Appendix A.

Finding the best plateau was possible generating several combinations and comparing vs cumulative production (figure 23), giving attention to some wells producing at higher rate, reducing production can favor not only to obtain the plateau but also control wells with high WCUT and GOR, helping to artificial lift systems to have better performance and compensate this reduction with other wells with more potential or no restriction for water for example.

Using the simulation model, different rates were assigned to the wells as described before to balance the production per reservoir, the figure 24 Appendix A, shows two strategies after several tests to reduce the production in A2 to mitigate the high pressure declination. The proportion in production per reservoir that can change at a given plateau for the field. In strategy B the proportion for A1 is 58% vs 25% of A2, it means that after this analysis was defined the rate per well can be assigned and avoid several problems satisfying the production plateau.
