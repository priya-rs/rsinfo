North-Komsomolskoye field is located in Purovsky and Nadymsky Districts of Yamalo-Nenets Autonomous Okrug (Fig.1) The field comprises 80 deposits of hydrocarbons, including 28 oil, 6 oil and gas, 12 oil and gas condensate, 14 gas and 20 gas condensate deposits of Upper Cretaceous (PK1, PK102 formations of Cenomanian stage), of Lower Cretaceous (PK12-PK21, AP2-AP11, BP1-BP72, Ach3, Ach4 formations) and the ones of Upper Jurassic (Yu11, Yu2 formations). Prospects of the field development are associated with bringing of hard-to-recover reserves of high viscosity oil PK1 formation of North-Komsomolskoye field.

Figure 2 shows geological section of North-Komsomolskoye field.

A pilot project on construction of two long horizontal wells was implemented in 2015 (well A and well B). To solve the above-mentioned issues a decision was made to test new completion technologies for these horizontal wells. Figure 3 shows the profiles of these wells. These wells are horizontal wells with a J-shaped profile with a horizontal end and are 1067 m and 1024 m long respectively. At the same time, the spatial intensity does not exceed 1.5g/10 m till PK1 formation, and does not exceed 1g/10m in the geonavigation mode along the target part of PK1 formation.

The lower completion for well A is shown in Figure 4. Two technologies are used to fight against solid particles production: 114 mm multilayer sand screens and gravel packing. Also, the completion includes circulating screens that allow you to create the necessary path of circulation of the carrier brine to create an alpha -wave when gravel packing. A valve was installed inside the circulation screens to insulate the circulation screens after the gravel packing and thus to direct all flows of formation fluids exclusively through the sand screens.

When the gas passes through the inlet of the control device, the pressure on the disk surface on the flow side is reduced due to the high gas flow velocity. The resultant force acting on the disk shifts it to the inlet, which reduces the flow. When a viscous fluid passes through the inlet, friction losses increase. The force constraining the disk to move to the inlet is decreased, and the inflow is intensified. As a result, more viscous fluids pass through the control device with less resistance compared to less viscous fluids (Figure 5).

Completion of well B is shown in Figure 6. The main difference from well A is the absence of gravel packing and division of the well into several sections by open hole water-swellable packers.

For well A and well B, overall, 6 RESMAN oil intelligent chemical marker systems (RES·OIL) and 6 RESMAN water intelligent chemical marker systems (RES-H2O) were installed into special carrier pup joints with a 127 mm diameter (as shown in Figure 7) between the blank pipe and the perforated shroud. The intelligent chemical marker carrier is open to the external flow in the annulus.

For this type of study, samples are collected at the intervals of 5 … 60 min during 1-2 days. Measurements of the time period (or volume) required for the indicator material flushing-out from each section of the well (the indicator concentration curve decline rate for each interval) and the comparison of the reservoir model parameters with the actual production data can provide the quantitative assessment for each interval of the horizontal well (Figure 8).
