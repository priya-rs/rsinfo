Based on core observation, grey and off-white argillaceous, or argillaceous sandy interlayer develops in mouth bars in the study area. The electrical characteristic of the interlayers is that natural gamma and resistivity curves obviously returns. And the thickness is about 0.3m to 1.5m, with an average of 1m. Thus, typical cored wells are selected to identify the interlayer in the single mouth bar. Through the electrical parameters calibration of the interlayer, the interlayer logging response template is established for the study area (Figure 3). Then the interlayer and sand-body architecture unit is explained with the established logging interpretation template.

The interlayers symmetrically distributed in both sides of the river, which usually shows upper arch pattern (Figure 4). The profile analysis revealed that the bottom of the accretion is flat and the top is protuberant. After the formation of first-stage accretion, the later accretion presents as arched shape because of the upward protruding shape of the bottom and the radians gradually decreases from the bottom to the top. The uplift amplitude of the sand body in the study area varies from 1m to 2m and the width of interlayers ranges from 150m to 600m (Figure 5).

According to the above research on reservoir architecture, a conceptual model is designed based on the distribution pattern of the interlayer in single mouth bar. The length of the mouth bars is 3.1km to 5.3km. The width of the mouth bars is1.5km to 2.6km. The thickness of the mouth bars is 2m to 10m. In the parallel source direction, the scale of the interlayer is 200m to 1000m and the dip angle is 0.4° to 1.0°. While in the vertical source direction, the scale of the interlayer is 150m to 600m and the dip angle is 0.4° to 1.0° (Figure 7a,b).
