The Triassic Yanchang Formation Chang7 Member tight oil reservoir in Ordos Basin, featuring complex pore-throat structures, low porosity, low permeability, rich micro-fractures and low pressure coefficient, is difficult to produce by advanced water-flooding from cluster vertical wells with low individual-well producing rate. With one Block A of Chang-7 tight oil in China as an example (Fig. 1), with the lower formation pressure factor about 0.8 (Fig. 2,Tab. 1). The reservoir’s mid-depth is around 2000m.

With one Block A of Chang-7 tight oil in China as an example (Fig. 1), with the lower formation pressure factor about 0.8 (Fig. 2,Tab. 1). The reservoir’s mid-depth is around 2000m. The tight oil is currently under primary depletion development with an annual production declining rate higher than 50% at the first year. The water-flood huff and puff in Chang-7 tight oil increase productivity to some degree, however, the recovery rate is low finally.

 

 

The water-flood huff and puff in Chang-7 tight oil increase productivity to some degree, however, the recovery rate is low finally. The stress sensibility of Chang-7 is measured from lab (Fig. 5~Fig. 8), and the permeability decrease around 20%. Horizontal well water injection - Horizontal well production of linear water flooding improve production yield 3-4 times than the vertical well water injection - vertical wells production, and the recovery rate increased by 5-10% (Fig. 9~11).

After water flooding in the conventional areal well pattern, the injection water will flow toward the direction of the maximum principle stress, and finally forms the linear water injection, resulting in the low oil recovery due to the heterogeneous lateral water flooding. After a near 30-year water flooding development, the core data from 9 infill wells have proved the vertical sweep efficiency achieved only 50%, while the plane sweep efficiency 60% (Fig. 12~16). Both water injection rate and oil production decrease with the increase of water cut.
