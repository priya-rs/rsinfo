Bayelsa field is located offshore Nigeria about 40 km northwest of Escravos tank farm. The structure is a faulted rollover anticline situated down thrown of two major NW-SE normal fault systems. The stratigraphy of the field is typical of the deltaic sequence of the Niger basin. It consists of continental Benin formation which is underlain by the paralic Agbada formation, which in turn is underlain by the marine shale of the Akata formation. As noted by Frankl and Cordry (1967), the Agbada formation is the predominant reservoir in the Niger delta with multiple reservoirs being a common occurrence in a field. The sands of the Agbada formation constitute the reservoir sands in the Bayelsa field. The reservoirs were characterized using available data which included the field seismic volume, SCAL and routine core analysis data, wireline data from wells and dynamic data from production performance. The reservoir grid covers an areal extent of 15.3 Km × 2.7 Km and had the E3, E4, E5 and E6 lobes as shown in Fig. 2 which was further divided into 204 layers with average thickness of 2 feet representing the log sampling in these reservoirs. The cell dimensions for the grid are 100m × 100m × 2ft while the model has 80 × 160 × 204 cells.

Porosity simulation was carried out for each of the facies region identified. The permeability was populated with a cloud transform which utilizes correlation of core porosity with core permeability and a synthetic cloud. The water saturation distribution in the model was populated using a cross-plot of porosity and water saturation. Regions were created in the earth model to capture the reservoir units already identified in Table 1 and for ease of control of reservoir properties and reporting reservoir information.
