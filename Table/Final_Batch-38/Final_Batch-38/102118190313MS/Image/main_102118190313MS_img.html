The Dina Cretáceos (DK) Oil Field is located in the Upper Magdalena Valley (UMV) Basin, north of capital city of the Huila department, Colombia (Fig. 1). Dina Field has a productive area of 750 Acres and reported initial pressure and temperature of 2,800 psi and 152°F, respectively.

Fig. 2a presents the number of open producers and injectors with respect to time as well as the voidage replacement coefficient. Fig. 2b presents the water and oil production rate and water injection rate. The field was operated above voidage ratio of 1 for quite long time, however, in 2010 the injection rate was reduced and voidage replacement coefficient approached near 1 prior CDG pilot implementation.

Based on the screening study, detailed injection/production data analysis, conceptual simulation and detailed laboratory work, the crosslinked polymer also known as CDG or microgels was implemented in 4 patterns in the field between the years 2011 to 2015 (Fig. 3). Sandoval et al. 2010 provided a more detailed review of the pilot area section criteria based on the information available (i.e., perf intervals, well correlations, injection profiles, injection pressure vs. frac gradient, well injectivity, and well mechanical integrity).

The CDG pilot started on June 9th of 2011 at the injector DK-03 with a polymer concentration of 400 ppm, a polymer: crosslinker (P: CLX) ratio of 40:1, at an injection rate of 1,500 bbl/d (Fig. 4). Since pilot start-up operating parameters (i.e. polymer concentration, P: CLX, injection rates), were adjusted to control maximum operating pressure to manage and maximize CDG injection. Based on the positive results observed during this first phase of the project the expansion of the pilot was decided.

Fig 5 shows cumulative CDG vertical injection distribution for the four injectors, in which it was observed that the preferential layer was B1 with 71.1% of CDG volumes, and to a lesser extent layers B2, A and Ter taken a CDG volumes of 20.9%, 6.3%, and 1.1%, respectively. During the injeccion of CDG vertical distribution was practically the same as the ones, observed in previous waterflooding for all injectors, however, it was possible to observe a change in the vertical injection profiles after displacements with CDG in two of the four injectors.

Fig. 6 shows the behavior of vertical distribution before and after the injection of CDG for wells DK-04 and DK-32 which presented a significant change in the distribution. In the well DK-32, there was a high reduction in three intervals. Nonthless, wells DK-3 and DK-6 maintained practically the same injection distribution before and after CDG injection. An increase in injection pressure was observed in all of the wells.

The effect of the CDG injection on each one of the producing wells of the pattern was evaluated, for which the historical flow rates (oil, water, and gas production) and bottom hole pressure for offset producers, and the injection rates and well head pressure for injectors were monitored. Fig. 7 shows the oil production rate and water cut in 6 offset producers affected during the pilot. There was a significant reduction of water cut during the injection of the crosslinked polymer and an increase in oil production. For example, the production rate in well Dk-24 has increased from 80 bbl/day to 200 bbl/day.

Fig. 8 presents total field oil production rate before and after CDG injection. Changes in oil production response and decline trends are clear. The increase in oil field production rates confirms the efficiency of the injection of cross-linked polymeric systems. With applying CDG injection, it was possible to improve the vertical and areal efficiency, and improve the injection profile in some wells as evidenced by the changes reported in injection logging tool (ILT), displacing mobile oil that has not been contacted during the water injection stage,

The incremental recovery factor and oil production responses in each of the previous feasibility stages allowed to demonstrate the applicability of the injection of crosslinked polymeric systems in the Dina Cretaceous field. (Fig. 10). When advancing in the evaluation of the injection technology of crosslinkedpolymeric systems from an initial screening stage (RF: 3% - 23%) and simple conceptual evaluations, until reaching an experimental laboratory stage (RF: 8% - 14%) where small scales are managed to recreate reservoir conditions (rock properties, fluids, pressure, and temperature) the applicability of the technology was determined.

After performing numerical simulation, current well status, and incorporating the pilot results, the final development strategy consider drilling 11 new producers and 5 new injectors, 16 workovers, reactivating one well and 14 stimulated wells. Fig. 11 shows how the development strategy contemplates the development of the southern and northern area of the Dina cretaceous field, decreasing the spacing and acreage by drilling new wells and optimizing the current water injection process.
