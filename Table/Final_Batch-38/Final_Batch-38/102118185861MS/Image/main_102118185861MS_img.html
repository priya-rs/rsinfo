Before analyzing the rela well data, the effect of an ICD completion on PTA was analysed with an integrated, transient, dynamic, coupled wellbore-reservoir model built with a commercial simulator. This was necessary to provide insight on how to approach the real data in the context of this problem. The wellbore model (Fig. 2) consists of a three zone, horizontal intelligent well with both nozzle type ICDs and ICVs for production control and monitoring. The flow path in this completion is designed such that the reservoir fluid first goes to the annulus through ICDs and then ICVs are used to connect the annulus to the tubing.

The reservoir simulation model was built with 30*30*11 gridblocks and local grid refinement (LGR) ensured proper representation of the pressure changes near the wellbore (Fig. 3). The well is placed in the middle of a homogenous reservoir with no flow boundaries and the five connections per zone is designed to link the reservoir to wellbore. Both fixed flow rate and tubing head pressure were used for production control. The well test schedule is required the zones to be tested sequentially by closing the ICVs across each zone.

Five distinct flow regimes (Fig.4) can be traditionally expected to be identified from the derivative plot (pʹ = dpwf / dlnt) during conventional PTA of horizontal well data, subject to the reservoir boundaries configuration. Each flow regime provides specific information about the reservoir and/or the completion. For example, the damage skin (Sd) and the product of the permeability in x and z direction (Kx.Kz) can be estimated from the early radial flow regime using for instance Eqn. 2, 3 and 4 (John Lee et al., 2003).

The Golden Eagle Area Development, located in the UKCS approximately 100 km North East of Aberdeen, comprises the Golden Eagle, Peregrine and Solitaire fields (Fig. 19). The three fields are produced via a combination of platform and subsea wells from three drill centres: a wellhead platform adjacent to the central processing platform (Golden Eagle wells), a Southern manifold subsea drill centre (Peregrine wells) and a Northern manifold subsea drill centre (Golden Eagle and Solitaire wells). Production commenced in October 2014 and water injection began shortly thereafter.

The development strategy for the Punt is also via waterflood, but use a pattern flood along the length of the Punt channel. This is because the Punt sands are fully oil bearing across most of the Golden Eagle accumulation with sand pinchout to the east and west. The structure only dips below the free water level in the extreme north and southeast of the field. Punt producers are located towards the Punt sand highs, with injectors generally in the structural lows. Producers and injectors alternate along the axis of the Punt fairway with a higher density of producers towards the crest of the structure near the 20/1-8 appraisal well, where the greatest thickness of net pay is encountered (Fig. 20).

Fig. 21 is a schematic diagramme of a three zone, production well. The pressure and temperature sensors are located immediately above the ICV for each zone. The uppermost gauge mandrel immediately below the production packer is a combined flowmeter and pressure/temperature sensor. A three optical-fibre cable bundle is clamped to the inner production string. It covers the three reservoir intervals; the Upper Punt, Lower Punt and Burns in this case. Distributed temperature sensing is provided by one of the fibres for inferring the water injection profile during shut-in warmbacks. Unfortunately the resolution has proved to be insufficient for production inflow profiling.

Well A is a three zone, production well with a deviation of 60°. The flow rate and annulus pressure for the upper zone, Zone 1, is shown in Fig. 22. There are three main pressure build-ups. PBU1, the longest, was conducted immediately after the initial clean-up. The total rate is measured by a multiphase, flow meter with the zonal rate being allocated by PDGs that directly measure the pressure in the annulus and tubing.

The three pressure build-up periods are overlaid in the log-log plot (Fig. 23). The the pressure and derivative plots are almost identical, hence there are no significant changes in either the reservoir or near wellbore throughout the study period.

The second data set is provided by well B, a three zone IW of similar completion design to well A. Fig. 24 presents the PTA of zone 3 for two PBU periods in Febraury and April which conducted after a production period with two different flow rate. There is small observable change in skin between the two PBUs but the quality of data is not as good as the Zone 1 in well A and the duration of the PBUs are even relatively short. This can affect the interpretation results and hence reduce the confidence interval. Nevertheless, the proposed workflow was followed for the longer and better quality data set in February resulting in ICD pressure drop of 0.63 psia which is equivalent to a ICD skin of 0.1, and therefore based on Eqn.12 the Formation damage skin of 0.34 (Table 6).
