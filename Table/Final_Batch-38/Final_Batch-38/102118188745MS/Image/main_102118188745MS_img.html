The presence of faults and lineaments in the overburden and reservoir intervals and the possible effect on successful delivery of any planned well is often a cause for concern. For Well#1 as show in Figure 1a below, due to seismically observed disturbed overburden zone along well path, possible mud-losses and overburden pipe sticking was predicted. PDA indicate the prediction to be accurate as pipe sticking, partial and complete mud losses were encountered at locations corresponding to the disturbed zone observed from seismic. Within the reservoir interval, one lineament with associated seismic hump was interpreted near end of lateral section for the planned wellbore.

Figure 6a summarizes the outcome of comparison of simulation results with actual performance for Well#1. The top left chart is a line graph profile of pressure, oil rate and water cut comparing actual and model results; the symbols are actual data points while the continuous solid lines are simulation results. While the chart indicates good alignment between model and actual oil rate, discrepancies exist in water cut, FBHP and SIBHP. The top right chart is a traffic light snapshot highlighting the history match quality per production variable with green, yellow and red corresponding to good, fair and poor matches respectively. For the candidate well, oil rate is denoted by the green bubble indicating good match to actual data. The yellow bubble represents a fair match in water cut, while the FBHP and SIBHP are poorly matched as indicated by the red bubbles.
