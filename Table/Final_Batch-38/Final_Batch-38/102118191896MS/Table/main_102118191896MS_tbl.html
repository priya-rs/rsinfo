The middle member is dominated by mudstone, which is mainly dark grey to black, finely laminated to bedded (Warren et al., 1998). Within the middle member three organic-rich units are differentiated: A, B, and C shale (Close et al., 2016). The B shale is the most continuous of the three units (Origin, 2017) and has a net pay of more than 40 m (Falcon, 2017). The kerogen contained in the organic-rich zones is type I-II. Other key properties (total organic carbon, vitrinite refelectance, porosity) are presented in Table 1.

Two sets of drill cutting samples from the Amungee NW-1 well (Figure 2) were characterized as part of this study. Set 1 are 10 samples from the lower Kyalla shale, Set 2 are 8 samples from the middle Velkerri B shale. The middle Velkerri B shale samples were recovered from a depth of around 2450 m, while the lower Kyalla shale samples are from a burial depth of around 1300 m. Sample details are in Table 2. The drill cuttings are of different shapes and sizes and were not further sieved to achieve a particular particle size range due to the limited quantity of sample available as to obtain representative measurements (approximately 90 g per sample).
