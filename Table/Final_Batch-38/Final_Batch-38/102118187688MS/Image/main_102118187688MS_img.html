The Katangli oilfield is located to the West from the Northern part of the Sakhalin island nearby the Okha city, which is the focal point for oil and gas industry in the region (Figure 1).

The entire cycle consists of three stages: steam is injected into the well for several weeks [Butler 1991, p 104], then the well is shut and the second stage called soaking begins. During the soaking stage the steam condenses transferring the heat to the oil in the near wellbore zone and reducing oil viscosity. The soaking stage lasts up to two weeks [Speight, p 231]. During the third stage the well is opened to production. Production of oil continues until the rate becomes too small to produce further. At these point a new cycle starts: the well is stimulated with steam once again. Schematically this process is shown on the Figure 2 [1].

In order to perfrom calibration the most representative time section was selected based on the availability of the injection parameters data (pressure at the steam generator and well tubing heads, steam mass injection rate for different wells). A model was built to represent the state of the network, which covered both the surface equipment (from steam generator to the well tubing heads) and the wells (from the tubing head to the bottomhole). The graphical representation of the network surface model is shown on the Figure 7.

The integration point between the two models was taken at the bottom hole (for horizontal well – at the heel), which is graphically represented on the Figure 8.

By using the integrated model the network model receives a new range of boundary conditions values in different combinations from the reservoir model. Usually some data sets cannot be successfully run in model because conditions may contradict each other so that the model would not converge. It can serve as an indication that either some measurements are invalid or certain model elements are inconsistent. Figure 9 shows different realizations of the integrated model with varying boundary conditions in time.

If steam quality is high (for example 0.4) and temperature is also higher (around 175 °C), then injected fluid occupies about 10 times more volume compared to water (corresponds to zero quality) at the same pressure. Since the enthalpy of this fluid is more significant and it penetrates deeper in the reservoir, the more oil volume will be heated and thus produced. This is graphically presented in the Figure 10.
