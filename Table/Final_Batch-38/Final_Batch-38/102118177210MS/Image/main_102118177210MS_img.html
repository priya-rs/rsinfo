The Figure 1 shows the location in the reservoir of interest of the water reinjector well where the chemical tracer was injected, and the location of the two oil producer sampled wells where the injected tracer arrival was monitored.

The injector well water injection line and injection water sampling line were identified. Both lines are connected at the wellhead of the injector well (Figure 2).

The tracer injection was performed with a portable high pressure air pump of dimensions 0.7mx0.5 mx0.5 m(Figure 3).

The compressed air source for the pump was obtained from the platform compressed air lines facilities (Figure 4).

The tracer injection was done by connecting a hose from the high pressure pump to the injector well sampling line (Figure 5). The injection of the 100 liters of tracer lasted approximately 9 hours.

With the injection of the 100 liters of fluorobenzoic acid (FBAs) chemical tracer through the injector well, the real tracer arrival time compared with the simulated time was 258 to 300 days for PRODUCER-1 well and 319 to 350 days for PRODUCER-2 well, resulting in a difference of 14% and 9% correspondingly (Figure 6).
