In this stage, each reservoir was discretized in sectors as shown in Figure 3. These sectors were identified as Field-sectors. The corresponding STOIIP of each Field-sector was calculated, as well as their respective fractions of STOIIP, for each category H, M and L.

The extrapolation function was validated with the numerical simulation of 6 additional Field-sectors that were selected in a random way. A comparison between the simulation results, and extrapolated results using Equation 2, showed a reasonably deviation, with average errors in the cumulative production of -6% for CP, -5% for the case of CP+CSS, and -4% for the case CP+CSS+SF. It is important to note, that in Field-sector level it is possible to perform a lot of important analysis about the behavior of each recovery method. As an example, in Figure 5 is presented and sketch showing the technologies with higher production per Field-sector, in MU reservoir.

Among others, the application of the complete methodology previously explained to the studied giant-XHO field allowed the evaluation of different field development scenarios. In Figure 6, we studied the fullfield behavior of just Cold production in a horizon of 40 years. In the figure, it is represented the oil production rate as a function of time, for a sensitivity to 5 fullfield production plateaus. Also, it is highlighted the corresponding duration time for each plateau. The results showed that cold production alone would not be enough to meet very high and long production plateaus and, if it was required, thermal EOR should be applied in less than 6 years. Also, the results look to indicate that for this field, it is unlikely to keep a production plateau of 400MSTOB/D.

The previous analysis was extended to the thermal behavior, and a comparison of the different oil recovery technologies was performed. Figure 7 presents the results of oil production rate as a function of time, obtained for a fullfield oil production plateau of 200MSTOB/D.

Finally, in Figure 10, we are showing the incremental NPV (respect to cold production and before tax discounting) as a function of Np (in 21 years) for each combination of recovery process evaluated in this study. As can be seen the sequence of CP + CSS + SF, using interspaced vertical steam injector wells, is the technology that accumulates the highest amount of produced oil (≅1600MMSTOB, 5%OOIP). On the other hand, the sequence of CP + CSS + HASD with slightly less produced oil, has a higher incremental NPV (2250 MMUSD), basically because HASD does not need to drill additional injector wells.
