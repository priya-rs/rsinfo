Sensitivity studies were conducted to evaluate the possibilities of Jump Start-up under various scenarios. As shown in Table 1, for a challenging well such as A-04, Jump Start-up had a small operational margin and most cases were on the border of failure/success, mainly due to the low reservoir pressure support (Case 1-4). With several bars change in test separator (Case 2) or lower GOR than expected (Case 3, and the field data after start-up confirmed a much lower GOR), the start-up would not be successful according to the prediction. This led to the decision to consider a more reliable gas-lift start-up.
