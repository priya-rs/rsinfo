The Oriente basin is located to the east of Ecuadorian Andes is the most prolific segment of the Putumayo-Oriente-Marañon oil province, which typically is subdivided in the following formations: Basal Tena, M1, M2, "U", "T" and Hollín. It contains three petroleum plays, Sub Andean system or West Play, Sacha Sushufindi Corridor or Central Play and Capiron Tiputini System or East Play, each of them with their own trap, reservoir and crude oil characteristics, Fig. 1, (Baby, 2004).

Reports with complete analysis were selected for separator adjustment (OFVF and GOR); those with nonphysical trends were disregarded. Quality check on composition data was performed (Lawrence, 2009). Data was classified by Basin´s play and Reservoir basis. On valid data, literature procedures were followed to generate a correlation from laboratory data as first approach (OFVF, GOR, bubble point pressure and live oil viscosity). The application of each correlation was proved by comparing the properties range from which the correlation was developed against the sample data, results for different properties and some samples are shown in Fig.12.
