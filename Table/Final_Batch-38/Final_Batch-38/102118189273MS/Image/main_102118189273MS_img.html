Tracer flowback mainly involves two opposite motions of tracer injection and flowback. The basic procedure for tracer flowback includes four consecutive steps (Fig. 1): (1) Tracer slug injection along with fracturing fluid. (2) Tracer-free fracturing fluid chasing period during which the tracer slug moves farther into fractures and matrix. (3) Shut down of the flow for a period during the introducing of a downhole packer for next stage fracturing. (4) Tracer flowback along with the fracturing fluid after the well is opened.

The Junggar Basin, one of the most oil productive provinces in northwestern China with an exploration area of 130×103 km2 (Wang et al., 2014), it is a large-scale intra-continental superimposed basin developed on Precambrian crystalline basement and Paleozoic metamorphic rocks (Hu et al., 2016; Fig. 2).
