The SUCS reservoir extent is easily mapped on the Base Chalk seismic reflection (top seal) by pronounced differential compaction. The reservoir is circa 500m wide by 1500m long, with a thickness up to 120ft. Its average reservoir properties are similar to the Upper Captain Sandstone (UCS) main reservoir: 96% Net to Gross, 31% porosity and 5 Darcy permeability, see Table 1. Although, no core was taken in the SUCS, wireline logs (gamma ray, resistivity and neutron – density) also demonstrate strong analogies with the UCS. Horizontal development wells show a high degree of lateral continuity. These characteristics make the SUCS pilot a good proxy for estimating full field performance potential.
