Xanthan polymers are biologically generated by dehydrating bacteria17. A relatively new type of polymer called associative polymer is designed to sustain more saline environments. Polymers are also susceptible to degradation under high shear rates. But the shear velocity inside the reservoir in EOR applications is very low and the shear effects on viscosity are therefore negligible17. Table 1 consists of different types of commercially available polymers used for EOR purposes. FP3630 is a conventional polymer while D11S, S255 and B192 are associative polymers.
