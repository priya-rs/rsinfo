The Wilcox trend in GOM ultra Deepwater is an emerging oil production frontier ranging from northeast Mexico Burgos Basin to the southern American states of Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, and Alabama. As demonstrated in Figure 1, the trend occurs in the western and central GOM covering three primary protraction areas: Alaminos Canyon, Keathley Canyon, and Walker Ridge. The frontier hydrocarbon potential was discovered by well, Trident-1, which was drilled in the western GOM on the Alaminos Canyon protraction area. Chevron has major discoveries in the Wilcox trend.

In our miscible gas study, gas was injected into the Wilcox reservoirs above their MMP in some cases above their FCP for those reservoirs where it could be measured. By injecting above the MMP, the injected gas mixes with the insitu undersaturated oil to form a single phase, reduces oil viscosity and improves microscopic sweep efficiency. To achieve optimal recovery, gas injection should be implemented at reservoir pressure above the MMP and if possible above the FCP. Figure 4 depicts viscosity reduction due to gas injection.
