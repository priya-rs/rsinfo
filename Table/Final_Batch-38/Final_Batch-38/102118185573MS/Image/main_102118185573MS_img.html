Fig. N° 8 displays the CDF of the PND reserves for the 1PX Project, estimated by the probabilistic decline curve method.

According to the Hybrid Model, the CDF of the reserves (1P, 2P and 3P) estimated by the probabilistic volumetric method and the CDF of the PND reserves obtained by the probabilistic decline curve method must be integrated. In this way, both probabilistic distributions are correlated and normalized in order to find a unique CDF of reserves. Fig. N° 9 and Table N° 12 illustrate how the reserves estimated by probabilistic decline curve method are coupled coherently with those obtained by the probabilistic volumetric method.

The CDF of Reserves for Project 1PX is shown in Fig. N°10. Using this probabilistic distribution, the proved non-developed reserves are estimated. From Table N°12 and Fig. N°10 we can see that the limit between proved and probable reserves is 102.64 M STB with a probability of 61 % (certainty minimum condition). These values are obtained inserting the reserves estimated by the decline curve method up to the maximum (102.64 M STB) into the 1P, 2P and 3P reserves estimated by the volumetric method (105.1 M STB in the 40 % percentile). Therefore, for 1PX project, Proved reserves are determined using P61 percentile with the Hybrid method instead of using P90 percentile according the PRMS classification.
