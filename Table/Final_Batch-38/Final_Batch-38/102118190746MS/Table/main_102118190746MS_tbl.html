The sample chambers were over pressured on sampling (~660 bar). After recovery to the surface, the samples were dispatched in their sample chambers to the onshore laboratory where they were conditioned at 60°C and 500bar and then drained into suitable containers. These samples and one mud filtrate sample (collected when the sampled zone was drilled) were analysed for a range of cations, anions and organic acids (see Table 1).

There was no evidence that Cl, Br, and acetate were affected by pre- or post-sampling processes other than mud filtrate contamination. Therefore, it was possible to estimate their concentrations in the formation water by applying linear regression analysis to the mud filtrate and water sample analyses for these ions and for SCN (see Ramstadt et al., 2017). The concentrations (and associated uncertainty, 1σ) were calculated from the regressions where SCN is 0 mg/L (i.e. in the formation water) (see Table 2).
