Deposition is fluvial/deltaic with almost all sands being weakly consolidated to unconsolidated and therefore requiring downhole sand exclusion completions. The sands are thin and have small grain sizes with typical D50 values in the 40 to 140 micron range. Gas permeabilities are good with core measured permeabilities in the 10 to 10,000 md range and flow test measured zone-average permeability values in the 10 md to several thousand md range. As indicated in Figure 3, below, the sands are thin and spread over long gross intervals providing low net-to-gross ratios averaging less than 30%.

While the 3-1/2” production inner string and 2-7/8” interval control valves were considered small for low pressure gas flow, frictional pressure losses were considered acceptable in terms of long term well flow performance. Maximum single zone rates were expected be around 25 MMSCFD with total well rates in the 65MMSCFDrange as shown in Figure 6, below

The solution was to manufacture the flat-packs with a trapezoidal shape, curved to closely match the diameter of the IWS Production Tubing. The curved trapezoidal shape shown in Figure 7 above, created a smooth profile which eliminated square edges and thereby allowed the BOP’s Annular element to provide an effective seal. The new flat-pack design was successfully tested by repeatedly opening and closing the Test BOP Annular on the 4-1/2” production tubing with an 8-line flat-pack.
