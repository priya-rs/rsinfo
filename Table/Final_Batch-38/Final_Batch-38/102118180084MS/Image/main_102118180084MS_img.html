The results from the analysis indicate that sealing formations including the overlying Morrow shale and Thirteen Finger Limestone units are excellent seals capable of providing storage of supercritical CO2 up to a column height of 10,000 ft. The structural model currently used for this work is shown in Figure 3. The well shown in the figure illustrates a typical vertical trajectory of the majority of wells within FWU. Porosity distribution in the reservoir has a range of 9.2% to 24% with a mean of 14.6%. Permeability ranges from 0.01 mD to 181 mD with an average value of 58 mD.

Chaparral Energy acquired FWU in 2009 and began the CO2 flood in December 2010. The preliminary history match for FWU was from 1956 to 2014. During the four-year CO2 history calibration, there were 12 water-alternating gas (WAG) wells and 28 producer wells. These were arranged in an inverted 5-spot pattern. The history match model showed areas where sweep efficiency was not good so 7 water injectors were distributed across less swept areas to to enhance production during commencement of CO2 flood. Figure 4 shows water saturation distribution at the end of history matching. The interior part of the field was swept during waterflood. The deep spots in the figure show locations of WAG wells.

A total of 25 WAG wells and 35 producers were introduced into the model by the end of 2018. The WAG wells were divided into four groups with similar operational constraints. The CO2 purchased profile used for this work is shown in Figure 5. The strategy involves continuous injection of 10,000 Mscf/d of purchase CO2 in addition to recycled gas until end of 2021. At year 2022, purchased CO2 was cut to 8,000 Mscf/d representing a 20% reduction.

In order to reduce the number of control variables in the optimization process, sensitivity analysis was conducted to understand effect of each parameter on the objective function. The uncertain variables include: gas-oil ratio constraints (GOR), group production target rate, bottomhole producer and injector wells’ pressures, and WAG cycles. Details of these parameters and sampling ranges are shown in Table 1. A random sampling with the Latin hypercube technique was utilized. A total of 69 simulations were run. Figure 6 (a-c) shows the sensitivity results of uncertain parameters on cumulative oil production; cumulative CO2 injection and objective function.
