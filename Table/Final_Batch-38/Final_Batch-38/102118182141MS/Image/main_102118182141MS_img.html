According to the results of indicator studies (Figure 4) the closure of fractures in the set range of depressions (up to saturation pressure) is not observed, which testifies to a high energy potential of the deposit.

At the initial stage of updating of the geological and hydrodynamic model with account of the analysis of the whole performed surveys range, the type of the conceptual model was selected, describing the chosen research target most completely. The classification of R. Nelson [2] was used for that, according to which four types of reservoirs are identified depending on the ratio of matrix and fracture properties (Figure 5).

Since the genesis of fracture formation is preconditioned not by tectonic processes (voids are complicated by vugs), the fracturing parameters depend on sedimentation indicators of carbonate rocks and are similar in the same facies. Therefore, in the process of geological simulation a map of facies distribution prepared on the basis of the seismofacial analysis (Figure 6) was used as a trend for fracturing distribution in the reservoir volume.

Simplification of the effective fracture system till several equivalent parameters is the result of geological simulation of the double environment. I.e. two 3D cubes with porosity parameters for the porous and fracture type of the reservoir and permeability cubes for axes X and Y were received as the result (Figure 7).

Therefore, the geological and hydrodynamic model was updated on the basis of completed comprehensive analysis of all information. The mathematic model of double porosity received allowed for further hydrodynamic calculations taking into account the factors determining the real pattern of liquid filtration through the fractured reservoir, which in turn led to improvement of adaptation of the calculated reservoir pressure dynamics in wells (Figure 8).

Therefore, the series of multivariant calculations was formed, within the frames of which different HWB drilling directions were considered (Figure 9). These variants, in addition to the existing wells (7 producing wells), provide for drilling of 2 designed producing wells with horizontal layout (Nos. 116, 323) with multi-stage hydraulic fracturing and 1 injection well.
