Dynamic simulation models were built to model reservoir behavior using the Chevron Extended Application Reservoir Simulator (CHEARS™). Given the high implication of breakthrough times to recovery in these wells, local grid refinements were further used near the wells. These enabled clearer understanding of the water and gas flow behavior and breakthrough around the wells the reservoir simulations. Depth prognosis were optimized using offset well depths, stratigraphic thicknesses of the most recent well data and sensitivities from the simulation model. After landing the wells at the pre-determined depth in the reservoir, pressure points were taken to confirm at various points in the reservoirs and plotted against depth to confirm the gas-oil contact. The pressures provided a second validation of the contacts, particularly considering the non-classic log signatures encountered in some of the wells. Figure 9 shows an example from one of the M-01 reservoir wells, with oil showing gas-like log signature.
