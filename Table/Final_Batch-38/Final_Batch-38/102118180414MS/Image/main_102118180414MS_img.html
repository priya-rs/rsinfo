Well completion techniques include both stimulated and unstimulated openhole and pre-perforated liners, to a myriad of modern multistage hydraulic fracture completions. Current completions typically include a 10,000 ft horizontal leg drilled through a continuous pay zone and propped hydraulic fracture stimulated in 20 to 100 mechanically isolated compartments. To date over 10,000 completions have been executed in the Bakken/Three Forks, with the geographic extent shown in Fig. 1.

As a result of enhanced quality control of developed macro datasets, a true 92 comparative recompletions have been identified, with 49 reentry and 43 refracturing efforts. The remaining difference (and large amounts of previously categorized refracturing treatments) actually exist as problem-well remediation cases. As the budgets to execute within these three categories have reported as much as a 26-fold variance in recompletion cost, a strict comparison of these individual bins will be used in this analysis. Fig. 3 illustrates this by comparing the zone area stimulation intensity of the completion versus the 12-month BOE divided by the oil to liquid ratio. The production metric is 12-month BOE divided by the oil-to-liquid ratio. Gangiredla and Westacott (2014) presented this quantum as a uniform measure of induced flow capacity across a discontinuous oil-to-liquid ratio within the Williston basin Bakken/Three Forks play.

These wells exist in the lower right quadrant of the primary plot in Fig. 4.

Fig. 15 is a bubble map geospatially showing wells that were analyzed with more than 360 producing days post refracture. The most prolific 12-month post refracture production has been achieved towards the depocenter of the basin. The P50 cumulative production results in Dunn County, ND were 56,222 BOE; 45,826 BOE in Mountrail County, ND; 38,145 BOE in McKenzie County, ND; and 31,850 BOE in Williams County, ND.

Fig. 16 is a bubble map geospatially showing 40 active reentry wells of similar methodology that were analyzed from the initial vetted dataset of 49 wells, parsed to be independent of significant remediation efforts. The most prolific reentry P50 results have been achieved in Mountrail County, ND. The P50 12-month cumulative production yielded 144,587 BOE, whilst McKenzie County, ND has a P50 of 101,471 BOE and Dunn County, ND with P50 of 80,500 BOE.

A histogram of incremental production calculated from the actual production minus pre-recompletion forecast is presented in Fig. 17. These results demonstrate significant uplift in production and ultimate recovery. A properly engineered program that minimizes the risks associated with reentry has the potential to increase PDP reserves and add net present value to the asset.

If completions with large zone areas are perceived to have bypassed pay, one could test the concept that wells initially completed with large zone areas will be the best recompletion performers. The bottom plot of Fig. 18 is a cross plot of 12-month incremental BOE post recompletion versus primary completion zone area. What is notable is that there is no significant correlation between initial zone area and refracture performance.
