Palogrande Cebú Field is located in the Magdalena Upper Valley Basin, north of capital city of the Huila department, Colombia (Figure 1). Palogrande-Cebu Field, was discovered in year of 1971 and began production in 1979.

In general, PG-CB field is an anticline structure in NW-SE direction (Figure 2), and is considered a heterogenous reservoir with Dykstra Parson Coefficients about 0.7.

Sands are typical of marine depositional environment, and are subdivided into eight flow units (K-4A, K-4B1, K-4B2, K-4B3, K-4B3sh, K-4C, K-4Cpor and K4C_basepor). The more important units are K-4B1, K-4B2 y K-4B-3 contributing with most of the field’s production (Figure 3). In a smaller proportion, production comes from the basal sands of the Guaduala Formation (TKGBAS), sands of the Barzalosa Formation (TB) and Honda Formation (TH). The reservoir is a subsaturated black oil, with an estimated OOIP of 221.7 MMbbls.

Reservoir permeabilities range from 6 to 150 mD and porosities between 15 to 22%. Sand units have been in production since 1980, with the highest level of production in 1987 with 10950 BPD. The oil gravity is between 15 and 22°API and its viscosity at reservoir conditions is between 9-12 cP. Static model including petrophysical model was builded in function of facies determination, reservoir quality index and rock types defined to assign water saturation in models (Figure 4).

The analysis of historic production / injection data of PG-CB field demonstrate the positive effect of water injection (Figure 5) with a maximum oil production peak (~ 10950 Barrels) by secondary recovery at end of 1987. Before water injection, the water cut in Palogrande area was low (<10%) because the reservoir was isolated from the aquifer by a layer of asphalt or tar. On the contrary, in the Cebu area, the proximity of the oil/water contact caused water conification in the lower wells, which led to the isolation of units B2, B3 and CPOR.

A simulation model of Palogrande-Cebu field was built from a static model previously updated. The original model had a total of 6,541,920 cells and took a total of 997,248 grid simulation cells (Figure 6).

Once the sector was chosen as the area with the best conditions to implement the pilot test, a detailed analysis for each well was developed in order to select the most appropriate pattern for polymer flooding. The patterns PG-34 and PG-37, were considered to be the best options to carry out the polymer pilot (Figure 7). These two injectors and their producers formed irregular inverted patterns, where most of the wells have good mechanical integrity.

Subsequently, polymer was injected until October the same year, reaching an accumulated injection of 0.428 MMbbl and 65.22 tons of polymer, this volume corresponds to 2.93% of the porous volume of the sector. Injection into well PG-37 was made with the same unit installed for well PG-34. Injection is made with independent pumps but the polymer solution is the same for the two wells. Regarding the response of the polymer injection process, there has been a significant reduction in water cut in most of the producers, where drops of up to 12 points have been observed, together with an increase in oil production in some the wells (Figure 17).

Figure 18 shows the WOR and GOR versus the cumulative oil production-.A reduction in the WOR is evident, going from 11.1 bls/bls in May 2015 to values of 8.4 bls/bls in June 2017, which indicates an increase in the efficiency of the injection process. Regarding the GOR, an increase was initially observed in the first 6 months and then a reduction and stabilization in the last year.

Figure 19 shows the incremental recovery factor agains the pore volume injected. The green line shows the expected response, the black line the actual response, and the blue line the expected response from the pilot results. As can be seen, the polymer injection has been more efficient than expected, and the response has been faster as well. This faster response has been observed in most of the chemical EOR pilots at Ecopetrol, in reservoir with medium to low permeability and high heterogeneity.

Fluids production of all offset wells in the PG-34 pattern are presented below (Figure 20), where the vertical blue line indicates the start of polymer injection in May 2015, and the pink lines the expected base line. Dark green line represents allocated oil production for each offset well, where producers as PG-08, PG-11, PG-14, PG-24, PG-28 and PG-30 shows clearly an oil incremental in the range of 7 Bopd (PG-24) to 185 Bopd (PG-14) at June-2017.

The first-line producing wells of PG-37 pattern are producer wells PG-22, PG-23, PG-25, CB-4 and second-line well PG-16. Liquid, oil and water cut production behavior in pattern PG-37 is shown in Figure 21. It can be seen that during the injection of polymer into the well PG-37, despite the small injected volume so far, a change in water cut is clearl observed, presenting a reduction of 3 points of BSW after two months from the start of injection. This is another indicator of the polymer acting as a conformance agent.

Figure 22 shows the behavior of both WOR and GOR against the cumulative oil production of the PG-37 pattern. A reduction in the oil-water ratio was observed, going from 31.8 bls / bls in February 2016 to values of 25.4 bls / bls in June 2017, which is a meaningful reduction in the volume of water produced barrel of oil. As for the GOR, a reduction and subsequent stabilization was also observed.

Figure 23 shows the relationship of injected porous volumes versus incremental recovery factor. Current estimated final incremental recovery factor is approximately 2.41%. Such low incremental is because only 3.1% of the pore volume was injected, compared to 20% in the original design. However, once the field goes into expansion more polymer will be injected in this pattern.

BSW reduction has been observed in the producer wells such as CB-04, PG-22, PG-23, in range of 1%to 2.5% respect to the BSW prior to polymer injection (Figure 24). Oil incremental in previous wells are in the range of 1 Bopd to 57 bopd.

On average, the two patterns show a reduction of 1.7 percentage points of water cut as of June 2017. In terms of oil production, an incremental oil rate of 442 Bopd and cumulative incremental oil production of 158,885 barrels. It is projected to recover around 485,000 additional barrels for a total of 643,600 barrels of cumulative incremental oil production (Figure 25) to the technical limit.
