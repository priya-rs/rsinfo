The case study presented in this paper describes a reservoir simulation study in the Powder River Basin with varying fluid regimes across the field (Figure 1). This particular field has three different fluid systems driven by differences in basement heat flow: black oil, volatile oil, and gas condensate. Two different reservoir simulation studies within the same field will be described in this paper. The first reservoir simulation study was developed on an unbounded well in an area of the field interpreted as black oil.

MFrac models showed a frac half length (xf) of 586’ to 635’ and a fracture conductivity of 900nD extending to 460ft. (see Figure 2)

Stratigraphy was tied to horizons from 3D seismic tied to well logs and steering files from horizontal wells. Petrophysical properties (Vclay, porosity, saturations, permeability) were interpolated between pilot wells using seismic attributes as a secondary input. After areas were selected for reservoir simulation, a subgrid was extracted from the regional model and exported to the reservoir simulation software. Figure 3 shows a portion of the geomodel in the area of Wells 1B, 2B, 3B.

A black oil reservoir simulation study using CMG-IMEX was performed on an unbounded well in the central portion of the field with sensitivities defined for several parameters such as fluid phase, hydraulic fracture dimensions, fracture permeability in the non-fractured zone (NFZ), and relative permeability curves. Figure 4 shows a 3D representation of the model

The model needed permeability enhancement in the non-fractured zone (NFZ) for it to be able to run and achieve acceptable results. (Figure 5)

NFZ PERM was enhanced using a multiplier factor of no less than 10 in the grid blocks in between fractures to allow the fluid to move to the fractures in the grids hydraulically stimulated and produce fluids. (Figure 6)

An acceptable history match example is shown in Figure 7.

A compositional reservoir simulation study using CMG-GEM was completed for three wells in the southern portion of the field initially defined as gas condensate (Figure 8 shows a 3D representation of the simulation study). After performing sensitivity evaluations on completion design, petrophysics parameters, and relative permeability curves in addition to developing a PVT characterization for a gas well nearby, models for this study could not achieve acceptable history match results (Figure 9 shows the P-T diagram developed).
