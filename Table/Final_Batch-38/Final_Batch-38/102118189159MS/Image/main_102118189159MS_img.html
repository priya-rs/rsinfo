The reservoir of interest consists of one exploration well, two appraisal wells and five development wells. The development objective is to ensure security of gas supply to NLNG. The sand development of the reservoir is in darcy range hence the wells were developed as high rate producers doing ca 120MMscf/d average gas production with 71/2-inch tubing sizes. They flow through individual flowlines to a central bulkline operating at ca. 110 bar. From the bulkline the aggrgated stream flows to a dedicated field slug catcher (figure 1).

The field extends approximately 3.5km in the north – south and 11km in the east-west directions. It is a closed structure system an East-West elongated anticline, bounded to the north and south by east-west trending synthetic growth faults and to the east and west by SW-NE and NW-SE trending synthetic growth faults, respectively (Figure 2). There are east-west trending intra reservoir faults with minor throws within the field.

The reservoir is developed by 5 gas development wells producing from 2010 with CGR of ca. 25 bbl/MMscf (figure 3). The wells flow into a central processing facility with inlet manifold operating pressure of 110 bar. Because the field production is part of contributing gas into long term NLNG contract coupled with limited well stock potentials, planned shutting periods were relied upon for critical reservoir shut-in pressure data.
