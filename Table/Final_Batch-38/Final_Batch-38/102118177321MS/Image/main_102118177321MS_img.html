In this paper, type curves are established for geographic breakouts of the Marcellus and Utica, each consisting of four separate production areas. The Marcellus is divided into West Virginia, West Pennsylvania, North Pennsylvania, and South Pennsylvania. The Utica is divided into Dry Gas, Lean Condensate, Rich Condensate, and Oily. The production areas fall within the states of Pennsylvania, West Virginia, and Ohio. The geographic locations of the breakouts are provided in Figure 3.

The prominent gas-producing areas used for comparison are the Fayetteville, Haynesville, Ft. Worth, Woodford, Piceance, Green River-Overthrust, and the Cana Woodford. In the Permian, the Midland and Delaware basins are considered. The Eagleville and Briscoe Ranch are the areas evaluated in the Eagle Ford. The Marcellus and Utica will realize growth in natural gas, crude oil, and NGLs moving forward. Gross natural gas production will reach 27.1 Bcf/d by 2020 (Figure 12). The bulk of gross natural gas production will come from the Marcellus, particularly the West Pennsylvania and West Virginia production areas.

Associated crude oil production will continue to grow as drilling continues in the Northeast. The production is on track to reach almost 200 MBbl/d by 2020 (Figure 13). The majority of this crude oil can be classified as condensate. Refineries in the region will struggle to accommodate the growing volumes of light crude oil, as they are particularly tooled for heavier crude oil from Canada.

NGL production growth will reach just shy of 550 MBbl/d by 2020 (Figure 14). This growth will require additional processing capacity in the Northeast and lead to more NGL pipelines being built to the coasts. The same forecast is also provided by product in Figure 15.
