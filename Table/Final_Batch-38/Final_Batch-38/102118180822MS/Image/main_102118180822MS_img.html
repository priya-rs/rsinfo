Both faults, the main slipping fault and the shearing fault provide the structural closure of columns of oil and gas in the field. The intervened well in its path crosses a fault with a vertical jump no greater than 40 feet with dextral component (transcurrent movement), mechanism by which faults are sealed, preventing communication of fluids. Nevertheless, range of interest is behind the fault mentioned, between 6945 ‘and 8400’ STB. (Figure 1)

From the petrophysical evaluation, absolute porosities were obtained, along with permeabilities, Gamma Ray logs, Density and Sonic logs for 70_2 sand in the IDE-1 well. The zones of interest were: 1st zone (5775’-5800’) tvd, 2nd zone (5730’-5772’) tvd, 3rd zone (5675’-5715’) tvd. (Table 1,Figure 2). The input PVT information entered for simulation comes from background bottom sampling performed in the AP01-PH well at level of 70_2 reservoir. Pb = 2246 Lpcm, API = 25,3 Rs = 357 PCN/BN, Bo = 1,191 BY/BN, μO = 2,61 Cp and surface conditions 139 psi and 140 °F.

After the petrophysical evaluation, the well nodal analysis was performed completed in the 70_2 sand. Pseudo Steady State equation was used to represent fluid flow in the porous medium and Hagedorn & Brown equation to vertical flow. The results are shown in Figure 3 and Table 2.

Figure 4 shows that the system implementation of barrel 7 ‘’ HSD PJO 4505 HMX in the shelling of the 70B sand, generate lower total damage due to the effects of penetration into the formation, reducing pressure drops when producing the hole. In Figure 5, the penetration in the formation produced by each penetration system and load is showed; it is important to notice the loads Power Jet Omega (PJO) beyond the damaged 5 ‘zone’ (low pressure drop) and PF 7018 loading just pass the damaged area with a value of 5.11 area ‘’ generating high pressure drops.
