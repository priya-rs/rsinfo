The South China YQ basin is one of the three major offshore HTHP regions in the world (shown in Fig.2). The basin is located at the intersection of the Eurasian, Pacific and India – Australia plates and has a complex geological structure. The bottomhole temperature (BHT) is 249°C and the BHP is 142MPa (pore pressure gradient is 2.4MPa/100m). Consequently, the basin is typical ultra-HTHP. The comparison of three major global offshore HTHP is shown in Table 1 and Fig.3.
