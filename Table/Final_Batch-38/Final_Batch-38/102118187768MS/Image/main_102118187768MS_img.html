The Yu. Korchagin field is located in the Russian sector of the Caspian sea (fig. 1). Distance to the nearest shore (delta of the Volga river) is 120 km. Nearest seaports are located in the city of Astrakhan (175 km) and in Makhachkala, Kizlyar and Derbent. The Yu. Korchagin field is the first field put into operation in the Caspian sea. It was discovered in 2000 by drilling of well No. 1-Shirotnaya, and put into production operation in 2010. Oil and gas content is identified in deposits of lower cretaceous (Albian, Aptian, Neocomian formations), in upper (the Volgian) and in middle Jurassic, Paleoigene deposits.

The deposit is a thin oil rim clastic reservoir with an average thickness of 20 metres, bounded by a massive gas cap approximately 100 meters in height and an underlying active acquifer (fig.2). The Neocom formation’s GOC and WOC is located at - 1517 m and 1537 m respectively. Effective thickness of the oil rim has considerable gas and water-oil transition zones and is characterized with heterogeneity of filtration properties.

At present the development of the field is close to its completion with 23 production wells. Their arrangement in the oil-saturated horizon is shown on figure 3. The development strategy aimed at high permeability zones of the uppermost Neocomian K1nc (bench 1), oil-saturated in periphery, which contain major geological reserves.

The trend of reservoir pressure decrease was defined on the basis of analysis of all hydrodynamic surveys of the wells available by the date of the reservoir model creation. About 82 hydrodynamic surveys were analysed (as of September 2016). On the basis of the hydrodynamic surveys the trend of the weighted average pressure decrease was calculated, and material balance equation was aligned with it (Figure 7).

In material-balance model the deposit’s energy state has been analysed (Figure 8). The analysis has shown that the principal mechanism to maintain the energy of the deposit is the gas cap expansion and gas injection back into the formation, cumulative effect of these mechanisms amounts to about 90% of the deposit energy. This conclusion has shown the necessity to proceed with gas injection.
