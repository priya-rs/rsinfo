Some other minor sands also occur above and below the SC3 sand and are mostly concentrated in the western part of the field. The first discovery well was K-1 which was drilled in 2003 based on the interpretation of year 1988 and 1998 2D seismic lines. In October 2005, K-2 appraisal well (approximately 3.5 km east of K-l well) was drilled to firm up the volumetric assessment of the discovery. In 2006, another appraisal well K-3 was drilled approximately 10 km west of K-l to validate the extension of SC3 sand. This well penetrated the main target SC3 sand at 1005 m MD and shallow secondary reservoirs (Figure 1).

The classical Static P/Z plot at different time spans of the field resulted in different GIIP which was increasing versus time as shown in Figure-2. The early PBU was leading to 250 BScf as GIIP while the later measurements widen the GIIP up to 560 BScf. Thus, the first question was the GIIP value as the main uncertainty in the field. As the field is compartmentalized, only main Fault Block is developed through 5 horizontal wells as shown in Figure-1.

For data diagnostics the FTHP vs gas production rate of all five wells has been plotted in Figure-3 which confirms good communication through the reservoirs as they follow each other in all transient, quasi-steady-state and Pseudo-Steady-State (PSS) flow regimes. This is a very good indication of reservoir connectivity using purely production data. A simple 2D model was also built based on PTA results and basic reservoir engineering and petrophysical properties like average permeability, thickness, porosity and water saturation.

As shown below in Figure 4, the production driven drainage radius of the five producers interfere with each other at some times during the production life of the fields and behave like a single big producer.

RTA workflow as established before by the authors required integration of production data through RTA. The well by well RTA results are plotted in Figure 5 for well A1. In this Figure the Flowing Material Balance (FMB) are shown on the right and Blasingame type curve analysis is on the left. The RTA analysis use Agarwal-Gardner (AG), and Flowing P/Z plot as well as the limited shut in data as plotted in conventional P/Z plots. Well PI, as calculated from flowing pressure data during PSS low regime, is also plotted in the bottom left corner of FMB plot in that Figure.

The PSS flow regime begins when the production data falls on the straight line on both AG and Flowing P/Z plots. This observation validated with the Blasinngame type curve on the right when production data at the same time falls on the boundary dominated flow stem of the curve. We have also checked the radius of investigation and pressure plot using our 2D model which matches well with FMB and Type curve results (Figure 6).

Figures 7 also shows similar results for well A2, A3, A4, and A5. Similar results can be concluded from the other wells.

We have also looked at the total connected volume to these producers by analyzing the total field production data with the well flowing bottom pressure in RTA. The combined production will point to the total connected volume when FMB are used. Four wells as shown in Figure 8 were used in this analysis. Wells A1 and A2 show a very good match on the straight line analysis with identical gas well productivity index as seen in Figure-7 when the wells were analyzed individually. Well A3 and A4 have also shown a reasonable match with the scattered data and can be addressed by the quality of pressure and rate readings for those particular wells.

IN Figure 1, two gas wells, A1 and A2 are structurally located toward the flank and near GWC. Towards the end of the production data, there is an indication of extra energy to those two wells by showing a deviation of data from expected straight line in FMB. The gas well productivity index in those wells are also deviating from the straight line as can be seen in Figure 10 and 11 for wells A1 and A2 respectively. This additional energy could be explained by having extra energy support from aquifer as they are close to GWC.

All 5 wells gas producers were included in this 2D model and successfully history matched. Figure 12 shows a good match between the calculated and predicted FBHP. The calculated connected GIIP for the 2D model based on average properties and matched by PTA data is 527 BSCF which is very close to what FMB has shown.

With two pressure buildups available for well A3, deconvolution has been done to address the reservoir quality, connected volume and any boundary as well as reservoir compaction illustrated in Figure 13. It is clear that both available PBU results were honored in deconvolutions. Deconvolution cannot match the early production data due to the lack of available pressure buildup. However, the last 3 years of production data matches well. The calculated connected volume for well A3 by deconvolution is 141 BSCF which is comparable with the estimated volume by FMB as shown in table 1.
