To evaluate the effectiveness of the SWAG method, a pilot project was introduced in the field. The pilot design of SWAG injection consisted of five wells of the AA platform. AA is the platform with the most oil production of the XYZ field, comprising nine producer wells and four injector wells (Fig. 6). Of the five wells chosen for the pilot study, four injector wells and one producer well are placed in the five-spot injection pattern. During the water injection process, there is more influx of injection water in the wells lying on the rising flank of the basin.

Spinner response indicated a consistent flow of gas-water mixture inside the smaller-size casing and a clear injection profile was obtained. The injection profile indicated gas was being injected preferentially in the top part of the perforation relative to the bottom part (Fig. 7).

The amount of variation of the flow regime from that of the expected fine bubble flow behavior helped to identify the most suitable completion strategy among the three wells. Wells with small distance (about 2 m) between the perforations and the tubing shoe had the preferred injection regime, with minimal segregation in the casing section compared to the well with a large (about 6 m) distance between the perforation and tubing shoe (Fig. 8). The best completion strategy would be the one that allows the injected mixture of gas and water to enter the reservoir in the form of fine bubbles.
