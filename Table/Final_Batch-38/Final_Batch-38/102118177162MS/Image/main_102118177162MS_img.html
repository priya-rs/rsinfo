The area of study is located in the Sacha field, Oriente Basin, in Ecuador. This area in which four horizontal wells were drilled at Platform Sacha-470 targeting to the Lower "U" Sandstone has 2D seismic data with poor quality vertical resolution (Fig. 1).

The importance of an accurate landing within the target sandstone is crucial when navigating within the zone of interest. Well correlations are essential to performing an adequate landing because they allow comparison and identification of lithological aspects between correlation wells and the target (Fig. 2) well.

Logging results from zones where the bar/cannel system is present reveal predominant shapes of gamma ray curves are box and funnel (is tuned up); RHOB and NPHI crossing curves show a good quality reservoir, and cuttings reveal a coarse good porosity sandstone (Fig. 3).

With geological studies and knowing the existing hydrocarbon potential in the field, it was decided to drill in the flank of the structure (Fig. 4).

However, during the lateral section drilling stage, the depth of investigation of the sensors is essential, which allows a proactive geosteering operation (Fig. 5).

To land at the desired point, the thickness of the previous strata, drilling parameters, and particularly the gamma ray readings and bottom cuttings were considered (Fig. 6).

In this zone, the presence of kaolin was encountered for the cuttings, which caused drastic decrease in terms of quality of the logs (Fig. 7).

From this point until the end of the well, the trajectory of the well was modified proactively. With the real-time information, the geosteering team can perform necessary recommendations to avoid exiting the well mainly to the base (Figs. 8 and 9) and then to the top of the reservoir.
