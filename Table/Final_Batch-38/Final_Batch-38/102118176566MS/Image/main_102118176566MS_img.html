Nowadays more and more of resources being developed are hard-to-recover. One of the major hard-torecover categories is low-permeable formations. Developing low-permeable formation with traditional slanted wells systems is often inprofitable. Even massive fraccing of every well does not allow to develop formations with permeability less than 0.3 mD in current economical situation. The decision is drilling horizontals with multi-fracs. The number of such wells increases every year (Fig. 1.)

Geological profile is clinoform, and productive layers are non-anticline, lithologically screened hydrocarbons deposits (Fig.2).

A pilot well was drilled, oriented to make fractures transversal to the wellbore. The productivity was high and decline rate was low, but eventually problem occurred with pressure maintenance. Placing injectors on the axis, crossing the producer wellbore leads to producer drowning due-to auto-frac effect (Fig. 3, left, wells 093, 296). Placing injectors outside this zone maintains pressure in only parts of the reservoir (Fig. 3, left, wells 095, 46, 244). Thus, in case of transversal fractures there is no safe way to provide an effective displacement. So the decision was made to use lateral fractures. It provides comparatively high productivity due to long fractures systems and at the same time prevent lateral coning (Fig. 3, right).

However, the surveillance of such wells is very difficult. For example, it takes over 3000 hrs of build-up to get a radial flow and estimate permeability, skin and fractures geometry. Thanks to PDG systems, we can use drowdown data with no need for stopping the well. Finally, it turned out, than these wells do not get pseudo-radial flow at all (Fig. 4).

Pressure fields in time after well launch arer shown at Fig 5.

Pressure distribution shows that at early time fractures really work independently. It is easy to see that at this time the well works similarly to a well where several formations are exploited and fractured (Fig. 6).

In this case permeability calculated by this “early” radial flow (if it is detected) is N times multiplied formation permeability (where N being a number of producing fractures), while Xf is √N times multiplied average Xf.
