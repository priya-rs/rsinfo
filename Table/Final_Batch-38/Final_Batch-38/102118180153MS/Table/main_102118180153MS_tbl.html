Fractures 3, 4, and 5 were performed using the same procedure as for the diagnostic testing. In all cases, a single dominant fracture was indicated, the 100-mesh slug was omitted on all treatments, and schedules similar to those shown in Table 3 were successfully pumped with concentrations of 4 to 16 lbm/gal (480 to 1920 kg/m3). The proppant plugs for Fractures 3 and 4 were placed in such a way that testing with CT was not required. Fracture 5 ended with a proppant concentration up to 7 lbm/gal (840 kg/m3). As this was the final treatment, a proppant plug was not required. An overview of the treatments is given in Table 4. A total of 1.5 million lbm of proppant was pumped using more than 19,000 bbl of fluid. The well situation after the final treatment and before cleaning out the well is shown in Figs. 9 and 10.
