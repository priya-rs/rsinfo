A five-component Peng-Robinson equation of state (EOS) was used to generate pressure-volumetemperature (PVT) properties of the crude oil for CO2, C1, C2, C6, and C25. The mole fractions of these pseudo-components and their interaction parameters were refined to match reservoir fluid properties measured in the laboratory. The mole fractions of the pseudocomponents in the refined EOS are 0.01, 0.10, 0.16, 0.14, and 0.59 for CO2, C1, C2, C6, and C25, respectively. Reservoir modeling results for both pilot scenarios predict significant incremental oil production compared to the Mumford Hills pilot because of the interrupted CO2 injection and short duration of the pilot. Model results predict an incremental oil recovery of about 48,000 stb (11% of OOIP) and 62,000 stb (14% of OOIP) for pilot scenario 1 and pilot scenario 2, respectively (Table 3), which are greater than the CO2 EOR of 2045 stb achieved in the pilot.

Field scenario 1 simulated four regular five-spot patterns consisting of 11 existing wells and three new wells (Fig. 8). Field scenario 2 simulated seven regular five-spot patterns consisting of 12 existing wells and nine new wells. CO2 was injected for 20 years in both scenarios. Model results predict an incremental oil recovery of about 106,000 stb (10% of OOIP) and 170,000 stb (12% of OOIP) for field scenario 1 and field scenario 2, respectively (Table 4).
