In 2011 pilot steam flooding study was conducted and recommended that to convert 4 wells after the second cycle of CSS (FNE-50, 51, 52, 53) to be as injectors with 9 producers in the pilot area as shown in Figure (1). With "The Steam flooding parameters are: steam injection temperature of 270°C, the steam injection pressure of 5~7MPa, steam injection quality of 0.6, steam injection rate of 1.6m3/d/ha/m, and the recovery ratio of 1.2". [4]

As shown in Figures (2) steam flooding has shown an impact in the total field production, this clearly appeared in the water cut reduction and the increase in oil production, the decline in the production in the mid of August-2015 was due to shut in of 4 wells (FNE-50, 51, 52, 53) and transfer them to injectors, FNE-50 started steam injection in later October. Steam generation unit was adopted at the field. the Steam generation has been stopped many times due to maintenance of water treatment unit.

At the field site, the steam injection was inturrepted due to the frequent water treatment unit stoppage. Hence, the intermittent injection mode (injection for two month and stop for one month) was compared to the continuous injection as it is shown in figure (11), summary of the results are in Table-1. Other parameters remained the same.

From the distribution of temperature Figure, (12) after 10 years of steam injection, for the intermittent case the high temperature zone is much smaller than the continuous case. So the oil production is also less for the intermittent case. Therefore, continuous injection case considered as the optimum case.
