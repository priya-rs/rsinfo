The absence of traditional signs of PMS work is caused mainly by low current and accumulated replacement ratio on the early stages of injection (Fig. 1).

This experiment also proved low efficiency of surface water injection. Actually from the first days of an experiment dramatical downhole and wellhead pressure raise was observed, along with injection rate decrease (fig. 2). Further investigations showed that effective permeability near the well dropped to non-reservoir values, practically causing injection to stop. It makes it impossible to increase replacement ratio. Wellhead pressure increase was not used to prevent development of auto-fractures.

Concept model is shown on Fig.3.

PMS effectiveness has been decided to estimate with analysis of producers behavior within every injection cluster. All the dynamic data of producers was taken for the analysis, such as bottomhole pressure, daily rates and water-cut during all working period (fig. 4).

Then extended transient analysis was performed with use of all pressure and rate measurements, allowing to define flow regimes, caused by drainage area borders and well interference. An example of this analysis is shown on fig. 5.

Most of the wells analyzed, allegedly influenced by injection, did not show robust derivative slope, meaning constant boundaries (–1/2). An example of such derivative behavior is presented on fig. 6.

History matching shows significant deviation of model data from the actual data in case of stable nearby wells behavior. In opposite, taking into account an effect of injection makes a perfect match on both pressure and production. The match of actual and model data of one of producing wells in both variants are shown on fig. 7 and 8.

Of course, the group of analyzed wells contained wells, not influenced by injection at all, despite they are close to injection wells. An example of log-log analysis of one of such wells if shown on fig.9, representing typical pressure derivative behavior in case of closed boundary model, i.e. depletion mode. In different cases, it was caused by long distance to injector or geologic boundaries effects, which if quite logical and excludes false influence conclusions on previous well groups.
