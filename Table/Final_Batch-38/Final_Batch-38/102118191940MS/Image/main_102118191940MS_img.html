The mud window at a given depth is the range between the maximum & minimum mud weight (MW). Minimum mud weight is defined as the MW below which the wellbore starts generating breakouts. Maximum mud weight is defined as the MW above which the formation starts generation tensile failures and mud loss. Figure 10 below illustrates different mud weight windows responsible for kick, breakouts, losses and fracture. The intention is to always keep the mud weight in the range of safe or stable window highlighted by the green flag in figure 10 below.

PP is defined as the pore pressure, UCS is defined as the unconfined compressive strength. All these parameters have been computed during the one-dimensional mechanical earth model (1-D MEM) stage.
