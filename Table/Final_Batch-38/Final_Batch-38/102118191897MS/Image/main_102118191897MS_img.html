Linear well pattern is an effective well pattern for fire flooding. Field tests and numerical simulation studies in China show that the recovery degree of linear well pattern is almost 10% higher than that of area pattern, and has the better production indicators, such as maximum monitoring temperature of oil well, CO2 content, N2/CO2, carbon and hydrogen atoms ratio, O2 utilization and single well daily oil production (Huang Jihong et al., 2010) (Refer to table 1,figure3-6).

Segmental top fire flooding is the only way to realize effective thick layer development in G3 block. The fire line overburden of thick reservoir is obvious (Yue Qingshan et al., 2000)and the thicker the thickness the more obvious the fire line overburden for its over 60m reservoir model. When the thickness of oil layer is over 20m, the override of fire line is obvious, furthermore, the upper limit of reservoir thickness is determined to be 20m (Refer to figure9). The thickness of the reservoir in G3 block is more than 60m, general gas injection in whole section will lead to override of fire line, and the interlayer is not well developed, so there is no good geological condition for oil extraction from the bottom up (Refer to figure8). We can inject gas to the small layer at top postion, thus it can increase the gas storage in per unit, regain formation pressure, replenish formation energy, and is good for high temperature oxidation(TURTA A T, 2008).

Vertical-horizontal well configuration is a new direction to improve fire flooding effect. The horizontal production well at the bottom of the reservoir can pull down the fire line effectively (Partha S Sarathi, 1998), thus the time of forming gas channeling channel is reduced, through this new method we can displace crude oil in a relatively uniform way longitudinally and expanded plane sweep volume, (Refer to figure10-11). A fire flooding test with vertical-horizontal well configuration has been carried out for one well group in G3618 block. After the horizontal production well was put into operation, the production of the well group increased clearly (Refer to figure12-13).

The optimization of segment position. For a 60m thickness reservoir, when the igniter is at the bottom of the reservoir, the fire line expands upwards as a result of gas overriding. When the oil above is heated, it is forced by gravity to spill into the burned area below (A.Doraiah et al., 2007), then the secondary combustion occurs, and the fuel consumption is nearly 10% higher than the initial combustion (Refer to figure14-15). In order to reduce the loss of reserves, the longitudinal section should be adjusted to the upper position of the reservoir.

The linear well pattern arrangement. In view of the complex and non-monocline characteristics of the G3 block structure, the linear well pattern should be vertical to the main channel of main river, and it can start driving at high position. In this case, the fire line moves in one direction, so it is easy to extract the remaining oil. In the end the displacement efficiency is high, and the stage recovery degree will be up 20.85% (Refer to figure16-18).

The optimization of ignition mode and temperature limit. We redefine the temperature limit and select the ignition mode for the improvement of combustion state (Refer to figure19-20). We can determine the temperature limit value of high temperature combustion is 454 degree centigrade through calorimetric and thermogravimetric reactions to.Electric ignition is preferred as the main ignition mode, because its monitored temperature is 317 degree centigrade higher than the 249 degree centigrade of chemical ignition and the electric ignition heats the layer thicker and more uniform, Moreover, the time for electric ignition to reach the high-temperature oxidation combustion limit is faster, which only takes 2~3 months, while chemical ignition takes 8~9 months (Zhang Yue et al., 1999).
