The BACH-01 reservoir is located on the eastern shore of Lake Maracaibo 120 km southeast of the city of Maracaibo. This is one reservoir of heavy oil largest, of the offshore field Bolívar, in western Venezuela, (Figure 1). The reservoir is OOIP 6,621 MMSTB, with a current recovery factor of 16.2%, 12 ° API, viscosity between 700-1200 cps and a depth of 3000 feet at Datum, average reservoir properties can be seen in Table 1 and 2.

Stratigraphically the reservoir is comprised of Miocene sediments (Member Bachaquero, Fm Lagunillas) with thicknesses averages 750 feet, the product of a prograde sedimentation domain fluvial-deltaic, being divided into 8 informal intervals (AP, H, G, F, E, D, C, AB), who have marked differences in thickness, quality of rock facies and fluid distribution structurally is formed by a homoclinal failed southwest 2-4 ° (Figure 2).

Continuity and prospectivity of the sands to the full extent of the reservoir area was evaluated to obtain the plot A-230 as the best area (Figure 3), for the implementation of CSS+Gas project. The largest reserves are associated with the upper sands (sand AP) and in this particular area just these sands are developed, just as it recovering reserves has been through the completion of horizontal wells with very good results after It is stimulated by steam.

For the behavior of production in the area A-230, they were taken into account all data recurente wells until Jun-2015, where the matching production came to that date, likewise predicting production was performed without drilling, and without steam stimulation of wells in the area for a 20 years period (Jun-2035), see Figure 4.

The study area (A-230), has a 567 MMSTB OOIP and recoverable reserves of 90.82MMSB. For Jun-2015 has a production rate 4.58MSTB/D, with a GOR 25.3stcf/stb, 11% WC and oil cumulative 52.81MMstb, with total of 137 wells and 53 active. The simulation grid consists 28×31×19 blocks X, Y, Z respectively with 16,492 total cells see Figure 5
