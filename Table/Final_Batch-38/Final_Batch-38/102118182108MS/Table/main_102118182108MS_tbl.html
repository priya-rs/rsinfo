According to that preliminary assessment, the minimum flow rate was estimated as 300 million standard cubic feet per day (MMSCFD) with a constant pressure at the outlet of export pipeline of 1100 psig (pounds per square inch, gage). A reduction in required inlet pressure of the pipeline, while maintaining the expected gas flowrate at referred outlet pressure, would increase the operational life of the production well and effectiveness of the well exploitation. As an alternative solution, a pipeline multi-sizing approach is suggested. Figures 11 and 12 present results of the multi-sizing approach. Optimal combination of diameters led to a needed inlet pressure of 1247 psig (86.96 bars), which has not significant difference with previous optimization initial pressure of 1273 psig. Significant difference is found in the liquid holdup and slug volume, which is dramatically decreased from 766 bbl to 422 bbl (see Table 7.)
