The case study was carried out on a gas field onshore Niger – Delta. The field is located in the eastern part of Northern Delta Depobelt, and in the north-eastern fringe of Niger Delta Basin. It lays onshore Nigeria (Inland Basin) in the northern-most part of the Niger Delta Depobelt which contains the Akata-Agbada Petroleum System. Structurally, the field reservoirs are typical simple four way closed roll-over anticlines formed at the downthrown side of a major North-West (NW) – South-East (SE) synthetic fault. The shallow reservoir is a four way dip closure; both intermediate and deep reservoirs are mostly four way dip closure assisted with a major fault. The top structure of the reservoir is shown in Fig. 1.

With the absence of core data, an earth model was constructed for the entire field by integrating well logs, 3D seismic and cores from analogue field (Fig. 2).

From DST results, full field GOR was observed to range from 8.7Mscf/stb to 55 Mscf/stb. See Fig. 3.

Three major static reservoir models with varying structural geometry with depth were chosen, based on their GRV, from among the 14 established static reservoir models encountered in the field. The first is relatively shallow sand with 4 way dip closures against a major fault. It has a gross rock volume (GRV) of 2092 Acre-ft. It was chosen as the “Low” case. The second reservoir is at intermediate depth with a GRV of 52281 Acre-ft. It is also a 4 way dip closure and taken to be the mid case. The third reservoir is relatively deeper with the largest GRV of 374079 Acre-ft. It is a fault dependent closure and a “gas-down-to”. See Fig. 4 below.
