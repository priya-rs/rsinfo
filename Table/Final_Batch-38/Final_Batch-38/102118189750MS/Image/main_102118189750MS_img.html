Based on the lessons learned from the THAI trail, impermeable shale barriers and low-permeability zones are detrimental to steam chamber growth. In this study, detailed distributions of each layer are characterized and their maps are delineated. Two of the maps are shown in Figure 3. The brown regions represent the shale layers.

Then position and thickness data of the shale layers are digitized and imported to Petrel. Given special settings, the 3-D model of the shale layers are established (as shown in Figure 4). By accounting for geological and operation variances, numerical simulation method is adopted using CMG STARS (2014) to carry on optimum scheme of SAGD development of S1-3832 after history matching. Permeability and porosity numerical models are shown in Figure 5.

Simulation model has been adjusted according to development history. Simulations end by year of 2016, the results shows that the recovery factor is 20%, reservoir pressure is 2.0 MPa and oil saturation is 0.52, which satisfy the requirements of the SAGD transition. Temperature, oil saturation and pressure distributions are illustrated in Figure 6. To initial a SAGD process, a pre-heating (or start-up) process is needed. According to the temperature distribution, reservoir is not well heated.

According to experiences of injection test of Liaohe, curves of steam quality versus depth at different injection rates are plotted in Figure 7,indicating injection rate should be greater than 120t/d to ensure the desired steam quality (> 70%) at bottom hole. Insufficient injection rate will lead to low steam quality and less heat delivered to the formation, whereas high injection rate may result in an unfavorable manner along preferential paths of the steam, causing steam-channeling issues.

To determine the optimum injection rate, different rate cases of 100t/d, 120t/d, 140t/d 160t/d and 180t/d are compared (shown in Figure 8), indicating 120t/d is the best rate.

Then, according to different ratios of production and injection rate, 1.0, 1.1, 1.2 and 1.3 are simulated and the results show that 1.2 is the optimum (Figure 9).

Because of the permeability of S1-3832 is a little lower than X-VI, the perforation length of injectors can be almost the same or a little longer. Based on the length of 5~8 m of X-VI project, numerical simulations cases of 4m, 6m, 8m, 10m, 12m and 14m are simulated and results show that optimum length is 10m (Figure 10). In addition, according to experiences of SAGD development in Liaohe, the lower part of perforation distance to horizontal wells is 3~5 m in general.
