Applied Post-Job Analytics (APJA) is a post-job engineering based analysis and methodology designed to determine the number of contributing transverse fractures (clusters) in horizontal wellbores, fracturing pressure components and the fracture characteristic half-lengths per stage. Table 3 presents the APJA cluster performance results for Wells A-F. Although the industry usually communicates in terms of cluster efficiency, a more useful metric is the number of fractures per 1,000 ft and the equivalent cluster spacing between open clusters. All three metrics should be considered. Figures 4 and 5 show the APJA cluster analysis results for Well D.

Each subsequent stimulation design change has made a marked difference in the well production results with approximately 15% improvement on average for approximately 10% increase in proppant loading. Results are presented in Table 7. These early improvements were encouraging and gave the team confidence about the potential uplift from further completion design changes. The 1,200 lbm/ft hybrid fluid design increased to 1,600 lbm/ft hybrid and resulted in 20% uplift.
