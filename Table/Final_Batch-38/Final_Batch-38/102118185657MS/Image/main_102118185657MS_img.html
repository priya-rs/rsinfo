The Pyramid Hills field as shown in Fig. 1 (DOGGR) is a truncated anticlinal structure in the San Joaquin basin of central California. It is located in Township 24S, Range 18E, Mount Diablo Base Meridian (MDBM) in Kings County. The Kreyenhagen (KR) formation (Eocene age) contains three different sands, the KR-1, KR-2, and the KR-3 sands. The KR-1 and KR-2 sands are truncated unconformably by the base of the Miocene. The Eocene KR beds are steeply dipping (~ 60-70°) to the southeast. The base of the Miocene surface is relatively flat overlying the KR-2 sand beds on the Pyramid Hills field, but increases in dip to the southeast. The porosity fraction of the reservoir ranges from 0.03 to 0.38 and the average porosity is 0.26.

The permeability is estimated from a porosity permeability transform and it ranges from 100 md to 7000 md with an average of 700 md. The geomodel as shown in Fig. 2 was constructed in the Petrel software (Schlumberger) using available well logs and surface maps.

The Computer Modeling Group (CMG) STARS software was used in isothermal mode to conduct the simulation study. Structure and model properties as built in Petrel were imported into STARS. PVT was modeled in CMG PVT Package WinProp software. Basic reservoir simulation data were either obtained from the field Operator or constructed from correlation. Initial water-oil relative permeability is shown in Fig. 3. These lab curves were obtained from Core Laboratories where Pyramid Hills field cores, synthetic field water and lab oil (10~12 cp) were used in the experiments.

However after adjustment to the water-oil relative permeability relationship considering our approach, we found a satisfactory and acceptable history match. Fig. 4 and Fig. 5 shows oil and water production from the field history and simulation run. Some of the minor mismatch may be attributed to inaccurate historical production allocation resulting from commingled wells present in the study area. There are no field gas production data available. The produced gas is only a small amount of solution gas and therefore this reservoir is behaving like a water-oil system.
