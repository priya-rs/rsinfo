The FE analysis can tackle the conformance of deformation in between formations that stratigraphically vary in rock properties. It is also advantageous in that detailed stress and pressure fields can be predicted in a reservoir formed of several formations when field test data are quite limited. The stress and pressure disturbances attributed to finite fault slips can then be superimposed onto the stress and pressure fields derived from the FE analysis. The analytical process can be schematically depicted by the diagram in Fig.7.

The Weiyuan (shortened for W) region of Sichuan Basin in Sichuan Province, southwest China is currently experiencing booming development of its abundant shale gas reserves, mostly concentrated in the Silurian Longmaxi formation. The region is marked by the red polygon and the blue rectangles in Fig.8.

The formation in the blue rectangle encompassing well W1 and W2 has a thickness of 280-450 m and a burying depth of 1525-3793 m. It is conformably in contact with the underlying Wufeng shale formation, whereas unconformity exists between it and the overlying Liangshan formation.
