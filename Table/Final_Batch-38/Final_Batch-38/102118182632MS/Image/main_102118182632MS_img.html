Figure 1 shows a comparison of three different approaches applied to the same WLO problem involving optimization of thirty well locations within a limited number of available function evaluations (reservoir simulation runs). The application of an approximated adjoint-based approach using the L-BFGS-B [22] optimizer produced larger objective functions quickly but stalled after several iterations, the pattern search algorithm APPS [23] converged slowly towards the optimum and obtained slightly improved objective function values, and the SPMI optimizer [17] outperformed the other two.

A deterministic history matching approach was applied to each of the 104 model realizations. Namely, adjoint gradients were utilized with the L-BFGS-B [22] optimizer to generate grid permeability multipliers that minimized the mismatch between model response and data. The mismatch function included water rates and bottom-hole pressures. The permeability multipliers (uncertain parameters) were allowed to vary between 0.01 and 10. Figure 2 shows selected realizations of the unconditioned x-direction, permeability distributions.

Respectively, Figure 3 shows selected realizations of the conditioned x-direction, permeability distributions.

Figure 4 presents a comparison of the base case locations (left) and the deterministic WLO results (right), it can be seen that the new producers were translated up dip to delay water breakthrough time.

Figure 5 shows improvements of the deterministic WLO regarding cumulative oil (left) and cumulative water (right). The cumulative oil was increased 26% with respect the base case and the cumulative water was reduced 12% with respect to the base case.

Figure 6 shows the performance of the base case well locations in all the model realizations compared to the Poor man's Robust WLO results in which the deterministic WLO results from Realization 15 were evaluated on all remaining realizations. This simplified approach is applicable in situations where computational resources are limited and prohibit the application of robust optimization; the expected Net Present Value (NPV) was improved by 16%. Although approximate, this step tests the robustness of the deterministic solution over all the realizations. It also provides means to compute the variance on the results.

Figure 7 shows the base case, deterministic (Realization 15) and robust results evaluated on all realizations. The robust well locations showed improvement of 22% in expected NPV with respect to the base case locations, which was also larger than the expected value of the single realization optimization (16%).
