AB reservoir acquired 54 fluid samples as part of gathering data and in order to characterize the fluid properties. Thirty six (36) of these fluid samples have been validated for Pressure Volume Temperature (PVT) experiments. Samples collected include both crest and flank locations, in order to characterize the reservoir both aerially and vertically. This is shown in Figure 7.

PVT data was validated before building PVT model used in the reservoir simulation. Validation test includes material balance and Y function. Figure 8 and 9 respectively shows the validations, for the most recently drilled well where MDT PVT sample was acquired i.e. Well 140. After validation of the PVT samples, fluid composition from the validated PVT report was input into the PVT software. In total forty two (42) components were characterized i.e. Methane (C1) to C36+Plus components.

In total forty two (42) components were characterized i.e. Methane (C1) to C36+Plus components.

Components were grouped (lumped) together based on similarities in molecular weight and mole fraction of the individual components. The lumped components composition used in the creating the equation of state (EOS) is shown in Figure 10. The components were combined (lumped) together, in order to reduce the simulation (CPU) time. As the simulation (CPU) time is a function of the number of the equation solved, which is in turn a function of the number of components.

In tuning the different parameters, a focus was made on the heavier components as these are the parameters with the higher level of uncertainty. Figure 10 shows the variation of Al-Dabbiya zone B fluid composition between the wells. Figure 11 shows the matched achieved after tuning in well 68, which includes conventional and advanced PVT experiments.

Fluid contacts was characterize in reservoir AB using all existing cores, logs and MDT data. Logs, cores and MDT were compared to ensure a match between the saturations. Figure 12 shows the match between logs, MDT and cores used in characterizing the fluid contacts in the reservoir. A gradient of 0.43ft/psi, 0.33ft/psi and 0.1psi/ft were used for the water, oil and gas gradients respectively. Both MDT data, cores and logs were used, in order to quality check each set of interpretation. Well 5 and Well 14 MDT fluid contacts showed very swallow contacts i.e. 8975 and 8905 respectively.

As such they were excluded as outliners, they did not match with core and log of the respectively wells. Figure 13 shows the variations in fluid contacts seen in the different wells. The dynamic reservoir model uses an average oil water contact (OWC) of 9025ft, a gas oil contact GOC of 8900ft and a free water level of 9070ft.
