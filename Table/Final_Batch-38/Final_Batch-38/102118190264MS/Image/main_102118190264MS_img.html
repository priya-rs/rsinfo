Fig. 2 shows an example in a certain oilfield. The inverted nine-spot pattern is adopted in this field (Fig. 2 (a)). Influenced by WIFs in West-East direction, the performances of production wells in different positions are totally different. Quick water breakthrough, accompanied with low oil rate and high water cut, occurs in producers along the WIF direction (Fig. 2 (b)) while long-term low production is sustained for producers that perpendicular to WIF (Fig. 2 (c)). Therefore, such dynamic behaviors of WIFs need to be timely and carefully evaluated in the waterflooding program.

The pressure behavior of water injection well C is shown in Fig. 7. The pressure and its derivative curves coincide as unit slope and last about three log cycles, which signifies the severe storage effect. After the early storage effect, pressure and its derivative curves show a small opening. This illustrates the poor flow condition in the reservoir. Due to the large storage effect, the true reservoir response is masked completely. Saphir (Ecrin 2011) is used to match this pressure behavior (see Fig. 7).

Fig. 8 shows another representative pressure response of water injector in the field. Pressure and its derivative curve coincide in the early time and then separate, followed by the upward of pressure derivative curve in the late time, which is generally considered as the existence of closed boundary near the well. However, it has already validated that there is no long fracture or fault found around this well by analyzing the performances of its adjacent wells.
