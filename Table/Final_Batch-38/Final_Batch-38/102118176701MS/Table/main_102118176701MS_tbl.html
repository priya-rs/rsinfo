ROXAR reservoir simulator has been used to study the optimal well placement in the layered low-permeability Domanic deposits of the Bavlinskoye field. Geological and physical characteristics of the reservoir are listed in Table 1. Model dimensions are 1000×1000×40 m. Two cases have been simulated: with natural fractures and without natural fractures. The completion model consisted of a 300-meter-long horizontal wellbore with a descending profile and 4 fracturing stages. The displacement front (in waterflooding) and oil-water contact (in depletion drive) propagated parallel to the horizontal section (simulation of two-dimensional radial flow).

This horizontal well intersected the thickest part of the target reservoir in the purely oil-saturated zone (Fig. 5). Once the drilling was completed, four-stage hydraulic fracturing was performed. As seen in Fig. 6, hydraulic fractures propagated at 30–60 degree angles relative to horizontal wellbore. The treatment was a success and it enabled to involve into development significant volume of reserves. Well 2917G performance data are compared with oil production rates and water cut in offset vertical wells drilled in April-May 2015 from the same well pad in Table 2.
