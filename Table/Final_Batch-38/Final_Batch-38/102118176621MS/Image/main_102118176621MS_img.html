Fig. 6 shows that permeability and skin factor have the maximum effect on the output parameters. As expected, permeability has the maximum effect on the field development because it controls the reserve recovery dynamics. Skin factor is important in terms of hydraulic fracturing. To minimize the development risks and to reduce the permeability influence, a detailed core test should be performed to update the relationship of permeability and reservoir porosity. To reduce the skin factor influence, well tests should be performed before and after hydraulic fracturing to define the updated well skin factor.

The spread of the predicted development indicator values (Fig. 11) reflects the current range of data uncertainties. Based on the diagrams provided, the current risks of the gas field A development were assessed.

Because the whole core tests were performed on solid samples, the results of the tests are applicable only to the matrix component of the reservoir. No investigation was performed for the fractures, which is the primary factor of the data uncertainty. To reduce the time for the reservoir simulation, it was decided to divide the area into two parts, consisting of the area with production history and the area with planned horizontal wells (Fig. 12).

The multivariate approach was used in the model because of a significant lack of quantitative source information about the fissured-cavernous component of the reservoir. The objective function was used to perform matching of the simulation model. The difference in operation parameters (rate, water cut, and pressure) between the model values and actual well operation data was established as the objective function parameters. The optimal values of the objective function were achieved after 4,000 iterations of the simulation model (Fig. 13).

The result of the history match was not a single simulation model, but rather a series of models with the distribution of properties providing the minimum value of the objective function (Fig. 14). The series of models made it possible to consider all realizations of the obtained matched parameters on the forecast model to calculate the horizontal well completion optimization and determine the optimal well spacing.
