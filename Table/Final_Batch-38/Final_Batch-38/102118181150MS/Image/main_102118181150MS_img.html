The G field is a mature heavy oil field in Ecuador which started producing in 1996. The initial development plan included drilling horizontal wells through M1 sandstone, which is a high permeability reservoir, to increase drainage area and improve sweep efficiency of heavy oil that is produced under a regional water drive mechanism (see figure 10).

As an alternative, active inflow control devices (AICD) allows equalizing the influx of oil and waterby creating additional backpressure in zones that produces at higher rates. Heavy oil takes a relatively nonrestrictive path while water or gas (lower viscosity fluid) flows through a tortuous path causing a decrease in its flow rate; thus, improving recovery efficiency and increasing NPV (see figure 11).

The application of AICD in G field by sectioning horizontal borehole in new wells, according to permeability and fluid saturation, contributed with reducing water cut in 6.5% and rise oil production by 13% (see figure 12 and table 1).

On the other hand, real time monitoring of ESP performance allows measuring pump efficiency and energy consumption (Kwh); some criteria with three ranges were defined to determine the proper consumption of energy. Based on this analysis, it is possible to determine automatically how efficient the oil production rate is in a well, as compared to the energy consumption required to generate this production. Therefore, it is possible to determine which wells are working ineficienlly, and eventually, when they would need an intervention to repair or replace its ESP (see Figure 13).

Production deferment average rates in Jibaro, Jibarito and Yanayacu fields exceeds 7%. An implementation of a real-time monitoring program + an automatic ESP production performance system would be useful to reduce downtime production, enhance runlife of ESP pumps (currently in 700 days), as well as, to assure that pump performance keeps at proper design parameters. According to well logs interpretation and structural section, the traditional completion system might be replaced by a completion with equalizers. For instance, in one of the horizontal wells of Yanayacu, there were identified 6 zones with potential use of AICD. that would be isolated with 7 packers. (see Figure 15 and Table 5)

Results of numerical simulation assessment during 6 months of execution show potential benefits of an alternative completion system with AICD; water production shall be reduced by 15% while oil recovery would be increase by 10% (see Figure 16 and table 6)
