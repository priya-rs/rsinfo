The subject well is the first dump flood well planned in the eastern part of the Raudhatain Lower Burgan. This is a pressure sink area where pressure reduced from 3500 psi to about 2400 psi due to production offtakes exceeding edge water support. The producing zones are Lower Burgan Layered/Channel sands (LBL) which are supported by edge water drive. The reservoir is still above bubble point pressure (Pb~2300 psi). As can be seen from Figure 1 that from 2009 onwards, well count and offtakes increased from 5 to 25 wells and 10,000 to 40,000 barrels of fluid per day (BFPD), respectively, which resulted in pressure depletion from above 3000 psi to 2300-2400 psi.

The subject well is the first dump flood well in the Raudhatain Lower Burgan North East (NE) area as shown in Figure 2. The water source reservoir was the Lower Burgan Middle layers (LBM) and the recipient was the Lower Burgan Lower layer (LBL) which is a coal formation. Initially the well was completed as an artificial lift dump flood well but as part of the future program it was decided to evaluate the well as a natural dump flood and the artificial lift plan was abandoned. A workover job was carried out and an electrical submersible pump (ESP) was pulled out. Later, an injection logging test (ILT) was performed and it was discovered that the natural injection of the zone was 7,000 barrels of water per day (BWDP).
