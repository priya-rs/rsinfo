Evolution of approaches to the development of oil rims is presented, based on the results of full-scale development of large oil and gas fields of East Siberia (Verkhnechonskoye, Srednebotuobinskoye, and Yaraktinskoye) put into production, in which a significant share of oil reserves (29% to 69%) is concentrated in the areas having a gas cap on top (figure 1).

Reservoir of Vc horizon is characterized by non-uniform structure. Net-to-gross ratio (NTG) varies over a wide range of 0.1 to 0.8, with an average value of 0.44 (stratification factor is equal to 6.9). At the same time, the Vc formation can be described as a highly productive reservoir: on average, the productivity index (PI) of exploration wells is equal to 2.1 m3/day /atm, and that of horizontal wells (HW) is equal to 5.9 m3/ day /atm (figure 1.1), due to relatively high permeability of 170 mD (the average for the major blocks varies within 109 mD to 441 mD).

Based on the core data and field data, halite crystals (NaCl) were detected in the pore space [7]. It is assumed that salt migration occurred along faults, which is indirectly proved by the increase in the proportion of salts in the pores in the direction towards the tectonic faults, in some cases up to complete salinization of the reservoir (figure 1.2).

In particular, at the Verkhnechonskoye field, as part of the 1998 Development Plan, for GOZ zones it was planned to drill horizontal wells with the length of the horizontal sections of 600 m, arranged in 9-point waterflood pattern where in the middle of a cell there were located vertical injection wells, and in the rows containing injection wells between them there were planned slant production wells (figure 1.3) [10].

As the experience was acquired in producing and operating wells in the areas where the OZ zones adjoined the GOZ zones, and in the marginal areas of the GOZ zones, decisions were made to install perforated liners at 3 to 5 meters below the oil and gas contact (GOC), and to limit the drawdown to 20 atm within the approved development plan. If necessary, drawdown is limited to less than 20 atm in order to reduce production of breakthrough gas from the gas cap (figure 1.4).

There are four major tectonic blocks: the Western, Northern, Central and Eastern. The bulk of the hydrocarbon reserves of the deposit is concentrated within the Central Block. The gas-oil and water-oil contacts (tilted [14]) cause different fluid zones - gas-water and water-oil zones (figure 2.1). In view of the field isolation by tectonic faults, the activity of the aquifer zone is practically not manifested.

For the Bt target, the solution for the location of the development system, its type, HW well lengths (Table 2.1,Figure 2.2 - indicators without changing the distance between the wells in a type plot), the organization of water flooding (WF), the placement of the effective part of the holes in the section and the recoveries (drawdown in a well depending on a zone) was developed in 2014-2015 with the use of numerical simulation based on the geological and commercial information available at that time [1].

Top-level solutions are: the use of HW wells; with a two-stage WF system and water as a displacement agent - for the W/O zone; with the return of produced associated petroleum gas (APG) (dissolved oil and gas of the gas cap breaking through the bottomholes of production oil wells) from the target to the gas injection wells located in the dome part of the gas cap. In the W/O zone, the WF system is formed in two stages; I - formation of a 9-spot system; II - conversion of the second-stage wells to injection, formation of a line-drive system. Yaraktinskoe Field is attributed to gentle monocline. Separate blocks with different fluid contacts are sealed with lithology change or non-conductive faults. Average structure dip is 2.9 m per 1000 m, min=0.03/1000 m, max = 11/1000 m, standard deviation = 2.2 m. Structural uncertainty may lead to +/- 3000 m change in gas cap edge in planar view. Pore volume of gas to oil relation is 0.8:1, gas and oil are in contact within 37% of field area (figure 3.1).

Permeability is strongly variable vertically and horizontally. According to core data arithmetic average is 93 mD, max = 1698 mD, standard deviation = 232 mD; well test data indicate arithmetic average having 204 mD, max = 5000 mD, standard deviation = 508 mD. Histogram for both data sets is similar and bimodal (figure 3.2 a). Two modes in core data illustrates the presence of two petrotypes of different flow capacity. Well test data is bimodal due to local high permeability zones within low permeability background (figure 3.2 b).

According to the pilot test in oil bearing zone the optimal injection/production wells ratio was defined as 1/3, which allows providing sufficient voidage replacement (no less than 120%). Taking into account the pilot test results and increased reservoir volume production (as result of gas breakthrough) along with significant lateral heterogeneity the flexible flood pattern was proposed. Approach implies the 9-spot system application with additional selective well conversion to injection according to 5-spot pattern (in relation to drilling results and ability of voidage replacement ensuring). The Verkhnechonskoye field (VCNG) is a large field according to reserves classification, hydrocarbon productivity is associated with the Verkhnechonskiy horizon of the Nepskaya suite (a depth of 1600 m), the Preobrazhenskiy horizon (formation of Pr) of the Katangskiy suite (a depth of 1500 m), the Ust-Kutskiy horizon (formation of Uk) of the Teterskaya suite (a depth of 1300 m), and the Osinskiy horizon (formation Os) of the Usolskaya suite (a depth of 1200 m) (figure A.1) [4].

In the lower part of the formation there are confidently traced facies of alluvial fans. Formation of alluvial fans took place in conditions of active bringing in of coarse-grained material, gravelstone, from the continent into the edge zone of the sedimentation basin, which is found at the bottom of Vc1 and Vc2 layers. Above the section lies the main productive pack of Vc reservoir formed by migratory channel facies of temporary paleostreams that accumulated in the active fluvial hydrodynamics (figure A.2) [5].

Important geological features of the Vc formation are the presence of block tectonics and the presence of salts in the pore space [6]. Within the field, nine main tectonic blocks are distinguished. Besides, the Usolsky graben of 0.5-0.9 km width is distinguished with confidence, based on seismic data and productivity confirmed by drilling. Tectonic faults cause different levels of waterand gas-oil contacts in blocks, dividing them into gas-oil and pure oil zones (Figure A.3). Due to the numerous tectonic faults, aquifer beyond the outlying contour is almost absent.

Therefore, to assess the risks of gas breakthrough, the influence of the value of the range of a sandstone/clay variogram on the dynamics of flow rates and ratios was evaluated. The current geological model with a range of 15 km and a riskreduced model with a range of 2 km were considered (the ranges of variograms for the layers of Vc1 and Vc2 could not be selected because of high reservoir variability) with the subsequent calculation of the predicted parameters in a simulation model (SM) (Figure A.4).

The main purpose of creating geological models with different variogram ranges and subsequent calculations based on them was to show the change in the waterflood system development depending of the continuity of clay intervals (Figure A.5). The types of wells, production or injection, vary depending on the continuity of the Vc2 formation due to the dominance of gas filtration in the horizontal or vertical direction, which is clearly shown in Figure A.6. By the way, reservoir pressure dynamics is not lower than 120 atm (initial pressure - 155 atm) in depletion cases (due to abandonment of the wells).

Srednebotuobinskoye Field (SBB) belongs to large-reserves deposits, with the productivity confined to Botuobinsky Horizon of the Lower Bucksky Sub-series (the depth of occurrence of productive deposits is 1900 m) and Osinsky Horizon of the Bilirsky Series of the Lower Cambrian (depth of 1540 m). The field cross-section also includes the following horizons which can be traced regionally: Khamakinsky; Arylakhsky; Talakhsky; Ulakhansky horizons of Kursovsky Series confined to a different part of the crosssection. These horizons are characterized by varying degrees of knowledge, mainly poor, and at the moment have not been booked in the state balance sheet (Figure A.7) [13].

A barlike body, with the axial part of a northeasterly strike, was formed under conditions of a slow transgression of the sea basin. To the northwest of the field, there was an extensive lower shore zone, stretching along a very gently sloping coast. The bar-like body was probably formed by along-coast tidal currents (northeasterly). In the southeast direction, the sandstone thickness of the Botuobinsky horizon is reduced within the prefrontal shore zone, up to complete absence in the slope shelf zone (Figure A.8).
