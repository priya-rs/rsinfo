In figure 5, a situation is observed when the wells GUN088, GUN092, GUN295, GUN286 are substantially displaced in the graph to the right relative to all other wells. This means that in the hydrodynamic model the reservoir pressure is much higher than the actual pressure, and the wide range of changes in the GGR given over three months of operation of these wells indicates a rapid decrease in actual reservoir pressure and condensate drop-out in the reservoir. These wells are situated next to each other (marked red in fig.7) and, possibly, are located in a separate isolated block in the northwestern part of the SWG area (shallow part of the Guneshli area) (Fig. 8).

Figure 9 shows results of two hydrodynamic model runs with two different reservoir pressure patterns. As can be seen, the actual average CGR curve in the 1st run is shifted to the left with respect to the CGR curves obtained from the PVT simulation results, which indicates an underestimation of the model gas cap reservoir pressure as a whole. The second run differs from the first in improved inter-well communication, resulting in a less intensive decrease in reservoir pressure due to the redistribution of gas reserves to the developed zone from the DWG (deepwater part of Guneshli) and Chirag areas, while the dynamics of the average CGR lie on simulated PVT curves.
