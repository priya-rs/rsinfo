Figure 2 shows the flowchart illustrating the connections between all modules used in the proposed methodology. Modules 1 to 4 use only the initial strategy as a reference to evaluate field potential for intelligent wells and prepare the data required to start the intelligent well optimization. Modules 5 to 8 belong to a loop cycle that optimizes each pre-determined time section individually, forward in time, creating new strategies to replace the last reference if the new EMV is higher.

Figure 3 presents a complementary diagram to aid the explanation of Module 5, which generates the possible strategies for a given time. It evaluates WMRS potentiality and creates combinations between those that show potential for each model individually. Table 2 exhibits the logic equations required to appraise field, wells and WMRS potential to install ICV. Note that when a WMRS has potential, the respective well and the field must have potential at the considered time.

The top wells produce the maximum allowed, a swing well respects a production value between the maximum and the minimum and other lower ranked wells are allocated the lowest rates allowed. Figure 9 shows the effect of a smaller platform on RM2. The simulator operates to maintain production at about 91% of the set maximum. In RM2, it restricts water but barely affecting oil production, increasing the NPV by USD 8 million, that is USD 2 million more than the platform saving.
