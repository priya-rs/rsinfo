For core studies, a custom-fabricated fixture designed to investigate electric and acoustic properties of core samples under both ambient and simulated conditions was used. This fixture runs in a broad range of pressures (rock pressure up to 1000 bar, pore pressure up to 680 bar) and temperatures up to 95°C. A rock sample is placed inside a high pressure chamber, special ultrasonic sensors measure the time of wave propagation with high accuracy followed by velocity calculation (fixed rock sample dimensions) (Fig. 1).

The simulation of reservoir conditions based on temperature variation outputs velocity values (Fig. 2). Using known densities, Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio values can be calculated.

Common depth point stacking was made in the traditional mode. The stacked data obtained was subjected to Kirchhoff migration with the use of an average speed obtained from the final velocity function VCDP(t0, x). Final processing procedure applied was a coherence filtering to remove the residual diffraction pattern, which have not been eliminated by migration. There is a number of seismic events showing a general structure of a near-surface section, which is observed in the final time section obtained by relative amplitude preserved processing (Fig 15 – 16).

Based on the seismic result analysis, it can be assumed that the low-velocity zones are located in vicinity of SAGD well pairs: 101-102, 107-108, 111-112 (Fig. 19). Minimum velocities are observed likely in the "toe" of the horizontal wells indicating the formation of the steam chamber. Actual injection data also show a greater injection in the "toe" of wells.

The basic well logging suite was conducted in all the monitoring wells and extended well logging suite including an acoustic logging (Fig. 20) was conducted in 10 wells. Different types of acoustic logging tools were used to define the most efficient method.

The initial model was built according to acoustic logging in a single well, for which interval values (logs) of Vp, density log, Vs, Porosity, DT were built. Interval values of acoustic logging have been added to the project as a basis of a simple horizontally layered model with Vp and Density parameters of the layers (Fig. 21).
