Neag-1 field was discovered on 2007 and first production from Bahariya was in June 2008. Bahariya reservoir consists of a sequence of thin bedded shaley sandstone that shows variable permeability ranges (Table 1) showing vertical and lateral heterogeneity. The main driving mechanism is depletion drive thus, the recommendation was to apply early water flood to increase the wells productivity and keep lifting pumps running in the optimum conditions of operating envelop. The former perforation strategy applied to specific layers of the reservoir assuming one frac could be beneficial for each layer but latter; analysis of wells performance and production recommended to perforate and frac all layers in order to enhance sweeping and maximize production potential. Water flood has started in 2010 and the strategy was to adopt a commingle injection and commingle production from the wells. Later on, production and pressure performance proved that there are different flow units in Bahariya.

The main objective of the static modelling was to construct a detailed 3D model using Petrel with the aim of reservoir simulation and history match as quantifying the subsurface uncertainties usually done in early stage of development. After years of production and large wells number; this field become mature enough with low chances of extra drilling but still there is a room to tackle the performance and get use of new inputs as well logs, RDT pressure, Attribute semblance, detailed zonation, Multi sand maps, reservoir analogues and Bore hole Images to build a new model of high resolution than former trials and compare between results of different inputs & workflows (Table 2).

Based on the relative permeability data analysis, we most likely have three groups of permeability's, high, mid and low with ranges of >100 mD, 10 to 100 mD and <1 mD md respectively as showed in the table 6. The routine core analysis "RCA" for three different wells subjected for modeling is well distributed in the field, 6 samples have been used for relative permeability measurements along layer-3 and 4. There is some issues in the relative permeability data retrieved from samples 6R and 8R because it does not match the grouped absolute permeability hence these two samples are biased by the effect of anisotropy resulted from heavily flazer bedding. Remembering that distributing these relative permeability curves for top layers might be an optimistic option.
