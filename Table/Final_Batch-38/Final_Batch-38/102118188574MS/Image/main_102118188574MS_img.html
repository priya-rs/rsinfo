North East Abu Gharadig-1 field is located in the onshore of northern part of the western desert in Egypt. About 180 km west Cairo city, Bahariya formation of Cenomanian Cretaceous age is the main reservoir in NEAG_1 field that is 90% of the Western Desert fields are producing from with several effective and possibly laterally Continuous seal (EGPC 1992), in this field Bahariya sandstones is found at shallow depth+ 1200 m.ss and consists of a sequence of thin bedded sandstone, shales and siltstone with vertical and lateral heterogeneity. Neag-1 development lease is a part of North east Abu Gharadig concession (Neag extension). Located in northern part of the western dessert in Egypt (Fig. 1) at the southeastern end of the inverted Mubarak Ridge. The Badr El Din Petroleum Company (Bapetco) operates this concession on behalf of the NEAG stakeholders.

A Complex three way dip fault closure at top Bahariya level, two separate accumulations lie at the intersection between an inverted northeast trending reverse fault (to the West) and trapped with northwest trending normal faults. Both structures are similar in structural style and separated by normal faults of± 200 m throw (Fig. 2). The reverse bounding fault was resulted from compressional tectonic forces of the late Cretaceous age known as Syrian arc deformation of northeast-southwest trending folds (Moustafa et al. 1998). The northwest trending normal fault is broken into three normal en-echelon faults, with the main structural closure along the central of these three faults. These faults may result in fault block compartmentalization while smaller scale faults (below seismic resolution) may also create additional fault block compartmentalization resulted in poor connectivity on thin sands.

The reservoir section of the field under study is subdivided by definition of the lateral extended geo-markers (flooding shale or paleosoles) into five units in addition to episodes of glauconite pellets formation and subordinate carbonate precipitation, obvious variation in Bahariya reservoir properties and geometries and lake of unique pressure regime even at same unit propose multi stories of stacked channels. Bahariya Formation showed complex marginal marine setting mixing between tidal influences and estuarine-marginal marine that Characterized by a relatively low energy. (Fig. 3)
