Four case studies were created to reproduce possible working environments of water or nitrogen inside the CT and water or gas in the well (Table 1). Under these scenarios, 1.75-in. [44.45mm] CT was suitable to reach planned depth with sufficient weight on bit (WOB) to drill. Weight of approximately 1,000 lbm [450 k> could be considered as a minimum with a positive displacement motor (PDM) requiring more WOB compared to the turbodrill (Table 1). Pulling ability at TD was also considered as a contingency.

The reasons for selecting the 2.125-in. [54.0-mm] turbodrill drive plus impregnated bit have been described above. This turbodrill was designed as a short one-piece construction for use on CT with all-metallic construction for use in high temperatures.
