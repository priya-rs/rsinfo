The main concern about pressure communication between stacked reservoirs is that it not only affects the management of these fields/reservoirs [Ozkaya 2007],[SayedAkram 2012] it may affect potential well recoveries. The field investigated for this study is one of the large fields in the Nigeria Delta region. It is comprised of two stacked sandstone reservoirs: Reservoir X1 and Reservoir Y1 having approximately same hydrocarbon contacts but separated by shale about 8-50ft thick (Fig 1). Reservoir Y1 is unproduced while Reservoir X1 has been on production for over 40 years. The reservoir properties are given in Table 1.

The three scenarios considered are Scenario 1: Sand Y1 in hydraulic communication with Sand X1 in the aquifer leg - This was built by sealing off the vertical transmissibility across the shale and permitting communication within the aquifer. Scenario 2: is the complete isolation of Y1 sand from the X1 by sealing off areal and vertical communication between reservoirs. Scenario 3: Was built by honoring the permeabilities in the earth model thereby allowing flow across the shale where pressure differential and relative permeabilites will permit. After validating the simulation model by attaining pressure & saturation history for reservoir X. The next step was to evaluate the recovery of a new well. A number of development options were run, Case 1: horizontal wells; Case 2: 3 vertical wells; Case 3: 1 horizontal well up dip; Case 4: 1 vertical well; Case 5:1 horizontal well for each scenario in order to determine the optimal development scenario shown in Table 3. All cases were run for a well economic limit of 100 BOPD; 90% Water cut & 10Mscf/stb terminal rate limit. Max oil constraint was imposed for the 2 producers to conform to historical production for these wells.
