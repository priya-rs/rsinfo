The graph in Figure 21 shows the dynamics of microbial growth in oil well of MJ-125 during production phase. The overall growth of bacteria increased from 1×105 CFU/mL to 5×108 CFU/mL for 122 days. On the first day of production, 1 December 2015, the number of bacteria was 1×105 CFU/mL and remain constant until 9 December 2015 with the number of bacteria 1×106 CFU/mL. Bacteria in the first 9 days of production is undergoing a lag phase or adaptation phase. The lag phase period of bacteria in MJ-125 was longer than MJ-122, which was assumed to be caused by internal factors such as the activation of the enzyme and/or external factors such as the limited availability of extracellular compounds so that the accumulation of the required extracellular compounds took longer (Madigan et al., 2012). Bacterial growth increased from December 9, 2015 (the ninth day) until December 16, 2015 (the sixteenth), from 1×106 CFU/mL to 3×108 CFU/mL.

Based on the results of GC-MS analysis (Figure 22) of the samples before and after the injection process (1 to 4 months after injection), it shows that there are changes to the high baseline chromatogram. Compared with a sample before production (September 2015), the samples from the 1st month to the 4th month shows a decrease in baseline after a retention time of 20.00. Similarly, production samples of 5th month compared with the 6th month shows a decrease in baseline after a retention time of 20.00. Decrease in baseline indicates a decline in the amounts of hydrocarbons due to the activity of hydrocarbon degradation by bacteria. The establishment of more peak before the retention time of 15:00 also indicates that the degradation occurs to utilize complex hydrocarbon chains to be simpler hydrocarbon chains.

Therefore, simulation was made by using a conventional simulator by using Eclipse ©. Figure 41 illustrate the location of the two wells that will be simulated. MJ-125 well is located at the center, while the MJ-122 well is on the edge of the reservoir model. Principle that used in reservoir simulation is to perform fluid flow matching produced by the fluid PVT data adjustment based on the changes of viscosity data which were obtained in the laboratory. The viscosity data were changed based on the time of MEOR implementation as an adjustment of the changes of fluid characteristics in the reservoir. Thus, the simulation results are able to demonstrate the increasing performance based on these implementations.

Figure 42 is the simulation results before the adjustment of fluid viscosity was conducted.

Figure 43 indicates a matching fluid rate based on the production data until 9 March 2016 as the matching reference. It can be seen that the reservoir model was still able to make production with constrain liquid rate suitable with the production history.

 

The simulation of oil production rate necessarily needs to be increased, while the water production needs to be decreased. Therefore, there has to be done an adjustment to the data simulation by adjusting it into a circumstance after the MEOR implementation. The changed parameter is the oil viscosity based on the decrease of the data in the laboratory, the saturated oil residue (Sor) value resulted from biosurfactant forming mechanism and water viscosity. The increase of water viscosity was performed by enhancing the viscosity value of 4.5 times higher than before, so it reached 1.25 cP. Sor value was lowered by 10% from 0,23 into 0,203. Meanwhile, the viscosity of the oil was reduced by 50%, illustrated in Figure 45.
