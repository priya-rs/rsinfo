While the overall correlation between the drain length and productivity is quite well-established for Karachaganak field (the longer the drain, the higher is the productivity), the benefit of drilling extended drains (beyond 700m) should be looked at in more details. Data such as PLT from these wells can clearly show the percentage contribution of the total inflow coming from the part between 700m of the drain and TD of the well. Table 1 below provides the list of all 49 wells that have extended drain lengths, and 39 out of 49 have measured PLT data. According to these data majority of the wells have significant contribution coming from the drain sections located beyond 700m from the start of the drain, and it is evident that in case of not having drilled these wells to the existing TDs the overall potential of these low GOR well-stock would reduce dramatically.
