Technical feasibility of CEOR in Colombian reservoirs has been evaluated over the last five years using different methodologies (Alvarado and Manrique, 2010; Castro et al., 2010; Trujillo et al., 2010). Yariguí-Cantagallo was one of the best candidates identified to implement polymer flooding. Field selection was also supported by the identification of several field analogs under CEOR including polymer injection. This contributed reducing risks and uncertainties associated to the applicability of polymer flooding at Yariguí-Cantagallo conditions. Table 1 shows average reservoir properties of the field.

Therefore, it was decided to use water from the aquifer for the pilot test. Half of the remaining (6) samples failed screen factor and/or filter ratio. Mechanical degradation studies showed that all samples evaluated suffered viscositiy losses over 40%. However, test conditions were considered as an extreme shear scenario and were not considered as an adverse result to move forward with the laboratory studies or pilot project plans. Based on the laboratory results, polymers EOR-80 (TIORCO LLC) and FP-3230S (SNF INC.) were selected for further testing (Figure 3).
