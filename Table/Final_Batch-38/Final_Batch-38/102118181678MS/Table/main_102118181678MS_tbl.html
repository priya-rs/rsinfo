Overall, results indicate that over 50 years of continuous injection, injection pressures are expected to remain 450 psi below the lowest estimated fracture pressure. The maximum bottomhole pressure calculated was 2,350 psi, which is much lower than the lowest estimated fracture pressure (2,800 psi). Pressure increases within one mile from injector are forecast to remain below ~70 psi. Calculations of the amount of leak-off through the (essentially impermeable) shale confining layers above the injection zone indicate that leakage volumes for the full range of cases studied will be effectively zero. In this study, a brine composition from an injector in the AoR was used in the simulator (Table 4). The brine analysis shows a TDS of 9,200 mg/L and a pH value higher than 7 because of the high bicarbonate (HCO3) containment (>1,700 mg/L). The measurement shows that the pH value for Inyan Kara formation brine has an alkalinity value of 7.95. However, this does not necessarily imply that scale is forming downhole, as the true pH may be lower than the measured pH if the formation brine samples underwent degassing of CO2 after collection.
