The Daqing oil and gas field in Northeast China is located in the Songliao Basin as shown in Fig. 1. The Songliao Basin is the largest Cretaceous oil and gas-producing lacustrine basin in China, with its greatest aerial extent in the middle Cretaceous. (Xiaoqiao Wan, 2012)

Denglouku lithology is a siltstone interbedded formation containing reddish fine sandstone and mudstone (Fig. 2). The unconfined compressive strength (UCS) is around 1w~1.5w Psi and the friction angle is between 40° to 45°.

Based on nearly 30 years of exploration experience, the well structure fixed gradually (Fig. 3). The kick off point set is around 3500 m in Denglouku III and it lands after the 800 m curve section in the Yingcheng group as the reservoir is in a volcanic basement. The measured depth (MD) of the well is approximately 4,700 meters at the true vertical depth (TVD) of above 3.000 meters. The mud system is a waterbased drilling fluid with a density of 1.17 g/cm3~1.22 g/cm3 and the viscosity is aproximatley 65~70s.

The formation of the Denglouku group is interbedded, which causes strong vibrations (Fig. 4). Therefore, most of the wells in the XS block were drilled with tungsten carbide insert (TCI) bits with the rate of penetration (ROP) lower than 1 m/h. Polycrystalline diamond compact (PDC) bits cannot drill through the Denglouku group and would result in damaged gauge cutters. The measure tools lost connection and the top drive got stuck. According to the experience of the offset wells, mitigating vibration is the key issue to longer durability and increased ROP of bits.

Run 1 is the origin hybrid bit with two each cone and four each blades decorated by 16 mm stay-sharp dual chamber cutters. The cutter layout is shown in Fig. 5. This bit ran with the AutoTrak tool with a top drive speed of 50-60 rev/min. Kicked off at 3350m in Denglouku group with WOB control between 6-8 tons.

Run 2 is a new design bit to increase aggressiveness. 19 mm stay-sharp dual chamber cutters were placed on the PDC blades. To balance the loads on each cutter, the frame of PDC blades was redesigned (Fig. 6). New shaped cones and TCIs worked together with PDC components to increase the ROP of this bit. The BHA for this run was 8.030 in. ATK Steer Stab, 8 11/32-in. Flex sub with Stab stabilizer, 6 ¾-in. BCPM, 6 ¾-in. NMSub, 6.693 in. NaviTrak, 8 3/8-in. NMStab. The revolution of the top drive was 50-60 r/min and WOB was 6-8 tons, almost the same as Run1.

Run 3 drilled the longest—121.36 m and the ROP was 2.26m/h. This bit ran with a low speed motor with the AKO of 1.3°. The tool face was hard to control because the formation changed a lot. The total sliding time was 29.9h @ 1.5 m/h, rotating time was 23.8h @3.22 m/h. The ROP dropped 54% when turned from rotate to slide (Fig 7).

Due to the use of a motor, the downhole lateral and stick slip is not high compared with Run1 and Run 2. (Fig 8.) While the side force is very strong, there were broken cutters on the shoulder and nose, and the cones with rounded gauge.
