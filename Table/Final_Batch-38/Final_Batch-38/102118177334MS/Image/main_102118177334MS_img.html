One of the approaches to analogue identification for RF evaluation is described in the paper (Wickens et.al., 2010). This approach is based on the calculation of field [1]complexity[1] index, which is a sum of four parameters, depending on oil viscosity, vertical heterogeneity, oil in place for the area unit, reservoir geology (faults, unconformities). Therefore the higher is the index, the more [1]complex[1] is the field. It should be noted that structure factor of scoring is based on United Kingdom Continental Shelf and the United States fields. The last step – values of injection correction on borders of every waterflooding cell are distributed by wells. Additional injection is distributed among wells proportionally to the well weights. These weights are maximum OIP density in the area between particular injector/potential injector and all neighboring producers. If injection rate should be decreased for the border, well weights are the inverse values of maximum OIP density (Figure 21).

his EOR method is a combination of waterflooding with alkali, surfactant and polymer (Figure 30). Surfactant is needed to decrease dramatically interfacial tension to values of 10-3 mN/m and mobilize capillary trapped oil according to capillary desaturation curve. Alkali is added in order to reduce surfactant adsorption on the grains and lower therefore the usage of the most expensive component of solution. Another positive effect of alkali is related with a soap production in-place (surfactant) which is caused by chemical reaction between alkali and oil. Polymer allows to stabilize the displacement front and improve the sweeping of microemulsions in the reservoir.

In 2011 there were 193 EOR-projects (including chemical, steam and CO2 methods) which gave additional production 663 Mbbls per day. 111 CO2 injection projects among them provided production 280 Mbbls per day which was about 5% of total oil production in USA (Mohan 2011). Currently, practice of CO2 injection for mature oil fields in USA continue to expend, from 2011 till 2014 oil production due to CO2 injection increased from 270 Mbbls per day to 470 Mbbls per day.
