Because there is not enough CO2 to satisfy the demand, it is necessary to find a supplement to CO2. McGuire et al (2016) presented that ethane is an excellent injectant for gas injection EOR. Additionally, both rich gases and volatile oils in unconventional reservoirs are promising candidates for ethene injection EOR. Their conclusions were based on both simulation data and three field examples. For the Lawrence Field in the Illinois Basin, the simulation results indicated that the ethane water-alternating-gas (WAG) recovered 48.2% of original oil in place (OOIP),which is 11.8% higher than water flooding, 9.8% higher than CO2 WAG, and 12% higher than methane WAG. Availability There is an increasing demand for CO2 injection, especially in the Lower 48. But there will be not enough CO2 supply, even the carbon capture technology, which is expensive, has been taken into consideration. 1. Due to horizontal drilling and stimulation in shale reservoirs, there is enormous ethane production from the rich gases and volatile oils in unconventioanl fields.
2. Ethane production is forecast to increase substantially.
Infrastructure For the produced gas from CO2 injection projects, acid-gas needs to be removed and removal plant is required before gas sales. 1. Ethane infrastruacture has been built and is growing in ethane-rich shale plays that include Bakken, Eagle Ford, and Marcellus/Utica.
2. This provides opportunies for ethane EOR in these shale plays and the areas that do not have sufficient CO2.
Economy The supplies of low-cost CO2 are limited, especially for the fields that do not have CO2 sources. 1. Ethane prices dropped drastically due to the substantial quantities of production. It is likely that the low price will continue for a long time.
2. Large amount of ethane is being flared currently.
Properties When compared with ethane, CO2 is about 20 times more soluble in water. It makes CO2 to be easily trapped by reservoir brine. 1. Ethane has higher solubility in oil and gas, lower minimum miscibility pressure (MMP), higher viscosity reduction and better solvent efficiency.
2. Ethane has lower density than CO2, which may impact vertical sweep efficiency.
Operation Corrosion problems in surface facilities, pipelines and wells require special materials for injection wells. 1. Ethane is noncorrosive.
2. Injected ethane should be diluted with methane to avoid three-phase region and form stable solutions.
3. The diluted ethane reduces the partial pressure and corrosivity of acid-gas components.
Technology CO2 EOR projects are successful, very mature with low risk. 1. The engineering, economic issues and opportunies of hydrocarbon EOR have been well understood.
2. Enriched gas injection with a mixture of methane, ethane, propane and butane has been applied in the Alaskan North Slope projects.
