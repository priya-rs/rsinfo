The A4 reservoir is a shallow anticlinal structure that is bounded by two main faults. An internal fault divides the reservoir into two fault blocks: Main and West fault blocks. The two fault blocks share a common OOWC and are also connected in the down-dip oil leg. No OGOCs were logged by the wells that penetrated the reservoir rather Highest Known Oil (HKO) were encountered (see Figure 1)

The comprehensive simulation of A4 reservoir involved the use of a structured workflow for the simulation project (see Figure 2). First, the earth models with static uncertainties were created by the geologist. The earth models are reviewed by the engineer to ensure that the static data were consistent. Then, the engineer reviews the historical production/injection/pressure data and quality checks the data to identify any erroneous and inconsistent data. Thereafter, the engineer reviews the dynamic input data before carrying out deterministic history-match

Each of the above static properties has their low, mid and high realization values. Thus, there were 243 realizations of earth models. The dimension of the earth model is 64 × 102 × 41 (I × J × K); while the average dimension of each grid block is 45m × 45m × 1.5ft. The total number of active grid blocks in the base model is about 81,000 grid blocks. With this low number of active grid blocks and considering the fast run time for the model, there was no need to upscale the model. Thus, the model used in this work is the original fine grid from the geologist. Figure 3 shows fine grid of the base model.

Table 2 shows the values of the input history-match parameters that gave acceptable history-match. Figures 8 and 9 show the history-match plots of GOR, Oil Rate, SBHP/FTHP and water cut for A4-1p and A4-2p, respectively. For A4-1p, good history-match was obtained for initial produced GOR, SBHP, FTHP and water cut. In the same vein, reasonable history-match was obtained for A4-2p with respect to the GOR, FTHP and water cut.

The histogram for the OGOC of the Main block suggests that OGOC of the Main block is more likely to be shallower than 4350 ft-tvd and less likely to be deeper than 4350ft-tvd. The histogram for the West block suggests that the full range of the OGOC of the West block resulted in acceptable history-matches.
