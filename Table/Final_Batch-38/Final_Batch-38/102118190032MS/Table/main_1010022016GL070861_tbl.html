The mechanics problem is initialized identically for the four cases of Figure 2. The mechanical boundary conditions are chosen such that a normal faulting stress regime prevails. We initialize the stresses under mechanical equilibrium with zero displacements. We use the slip weakening model to describe dynamic frictional failure of the fault. We assume that the model is saturated with water and use hydrostatics to initialize pressure. The topography of the reservoir in a vertical cross-section is shown in Figure 4. The wells are located in the hanging wall and footwall blocks of the fault. For the four cases studies considered here, the well in the hanging wall block is a producer with a constant flow rate of 3000 bbl/day.
