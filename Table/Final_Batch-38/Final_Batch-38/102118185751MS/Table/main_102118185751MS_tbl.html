The training set used to train the model consists of past completion intervals, and the training attributes are derived/calculated specifically over these intervals. For instance, a permeability training attribute is averaged from log values extracted specifically from the completion interval. Once the model is trained, the testing dataset is collected by deriving the testing attributes specifically over the target missed net pay intervals. Table 1 shows various training attributes together with their corresponding testing attributes. While neural networks can incorporate relationships between several types of attributes, their reliability greatly depends on the size of the training dataset.
