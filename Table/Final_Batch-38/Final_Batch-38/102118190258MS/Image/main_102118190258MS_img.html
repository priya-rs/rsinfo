At present, the plugging problem of some oil wells in W oilfield has resulted in the obvious drop of liquid production, such as X45 well (Fig. 2). It is urgent to study the blocking mechanism and influence law of the polymer flooding oilfield, to provide support for the plugging removal and acidification measures, and to ensure the effect of the polymer flooding.

A typical 1-injector & 1 producer polymer flooding reservoir model was established with reservoir and polymer parameters from W field. Model grid number: X direction 31, Y direction 5, Z direction 3; grid step length: DX=20m, DY=20m, DZ=5m. There are 1 injector and 1 producer at end of the model separately (Fig. 3).

Through simulation with the detailed description of reservoir and polymer properties, the impacts of key parameters (injection time, polymer concentration, injected volume, relative permeability cures, etc) affecting the production rate of producers in different stages of polymer flooding were analyzed (Table 1,Fig. 4). On this basis, the numerical simulation of water flooding and polymer flooding is carried out (Eclipse technical manual 2017). Water flooding was carried out by 60% water cut, then polymer injection was conducted by 0.3 PV, finally the following water flooding was used till water cut 98%.

The production rate was simulated and forecasted by the model given above (Fig. 3), which shows that the change of liquid production index could be divided by 4 stages: Rising, descending and quick drop section and slow return period (Fig. 5).

Firstly, the plugging at different location in the reservoir during polymer flooding were simulated, including near injectors wellbore, near producer wellbore and in the deep reservoir (Fig. 6).

The WPIMULT (multiplies well connection factors by a given value) of injectors was set to be different value (0.02, 0.03, 0.05) to simulate the production performance under various plugging situation (Eclipse technical manual 2017). It could be seen that the worse the plugging the sooner and lower the production rate drops (10%~60% in this study) (Fig. 7).

The Plugging influence around the producers was simulated by adjusting their completion skin S (including 20, 35, 50, Fig.8). It could be seen that in the case of near producer plugging, the drop rate of liquid production is higher and the hopper may appear twice during of liquid drop.

The conditions of different plugging locations (near producers, near injectors, and in the central reservoir) were simulated and the corresponding production dynamic characteristics were examined. Based on the above three cases, the diagnostic chart were given in Fig. 9 and Fig. 10.

In order to describe the characteristics of plugging around producers, the near wellbore local grid refinement technology was used to simulate the plugging in different scope and degree near to the producers (Fig. 11)(Eclipse technical Manual 2017, Zhang et al 2010).

Firstly, the grid transmissibility in different scope near to the producers was modified to simulate the different pollution radius (0.1, 1.2, 3, 5 m). It could be seen that with the increase of pollution radius, well fluid volume decline rate increased, resulting longer low liquid level duration (Fig. 12).

Secondly, in the same radius of plugging of 3 meters, the grid transmissibility in this area was modified from 0.01 to 0.07, to analyze influence different plugging degree. It can be seen that the worse the plugging, the greater the drop of the production rate, the longer the maintenance time (Fig. 13).
