The East Seminole Field covers approximately 2,000 acres in Gaines County, Texas, USA and includes both the East Seminole San Andres Unit (ESSAU) and the Lindoss Unit. The field lies on the northeastern edge of the Central Basin Platform (Fig. 2a) and produces from a succession of anticlinal traps in the San Andres dolomite. Core data revealed that the bulk of the San Andres reservoir rock contains subtidal fusulinid pack/grainstones and intertidal pelleted wacke/packstones in a shallowing upward sequence.

To further test the network and to measure the relative importance of the inputs of the ANN model on output performance, a sensitivity analysis about the mean of each of the inputs was carried out. This analysis demonstrates how the model output varies in response to variation of an input. During this analysis, the inputs were varied around their mean by adding and subtracting one standard deviation, while keeping all other inputs at their respective mean values. The output was computed for 50 steps greater than and less than the mean value of the varied input. The results of the test are shown in Table 3 and in Fig. 7.
