The changing gravitational potential of the moon and the sun as they change position relative to the rotating Earth causes sea levels to rise at some locations and fall at others. The resulting sea-level oscillation causes a pressure loading and unloading which is transmitted downward to the underlying rocks. While the frequencies of the ocean tide are known parameters (Table 1), the size of the ocean tide range is governed by the hydrodynamics and requires measurements. It is the length, width, and depth of the bay that controls the hydrodynamics.

Field A is very close to the diurnal amphidromic and experiences only one tide per day (F=3.5). This is clearly evident from bottomhole pressure measurements (Figure 3). Spectral analysis of the detrended pressure from well#1 test#4 (Figure 4) reveals a strong diurnal component and a very weak semidiurnal. Tidal recordings are available from 3 stations: Zirku, M1 and Al Mubarraz some 40 to 80 km from Field A The harmonic analysis of these stations was conducted by Poux et al. (Table 2). The tide type changes drastically from being mixed at M1 and Al Mubarraz to diurnal at Zirku while the amplitude of M2 decreases from 31 cm to 11 cm. In order to capture this trend, the harmonic constituents, both ampltides and phases, were linearly interpolated to Field A (Figure 5).
