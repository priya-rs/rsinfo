This has a profound impact on the nature of the tide in the Gulf. Tides are classified by using the ratio of the amplitudes of the main diurnal constituents K1 and O1 to that of the two main semi-diurnal amplitudes M2 and S2 known as the “form ratio”.

Defant (1961) gives the following classification for categorizing the type of tide: semi-diurnal for F < 0.25; mixed, mainly semi-diurnal for F between 0.25 and 1.5; mixed, mainly diurnal for F between 1.5 and 3.0; and diurnal for F > 3.0. According to Figure 2 the tide is generally mixed except close to the semidiurnal points where the tide is diurnal and close to the diurnal amphidromic point where the tide is semidiurnal.

It is not until 1994 that the first electronic gauges were deployed with a resolution of 0.01 psi, suitable for tidal analysis. The tidal removal procedures of Levithan and Phan with the spline model, and Zhao and Reynolds with the two log model were tested on some build-ups and interference tests. Some of the responses are complex with early pressure build-up followed by depletion from interfering wells (Figure 6) or depletion followed by repressurization after shutting neighbouring wells (Figure 7). Field A is located in the Gulf approximatively 130 km north-west of Abu Dhabi. The reservoir is a gentle anticline (Figure 8) and sits at a depth of 5500 ft subsea. It consists of alternating sedimentary cycles of porous and dense chalky limestone. The formation is subdivided into 3 main producing porous layers from top to bottom: L1, L2 and L3 (Figure 9). The reservoir quality improves overall from L1 to L3, with good matrix porosity ranging from 15 to 25% and a low matrix permeability ranging from 0.1 to 7 mD enhanced by the presence of open fractures in certain places.

Table 3 summarises the PTA and tidal analysis. We notice from Figure 11 an increasing trend of tidal efficiency with KH from 0.08 to 0.24. Indeed the diffusive fractures improve the matrix KH, typically by a factor of 10, while fracture corridors enhance it by more than 100. Thus high KH is indicative of fractures near the well. These fractures increase the rock compressibility and produce a higher tidal efficiency (Equation 3). Also flank wells well#5, 6 and 7 have higher tidal efficiencies than wells with similar KH at the crest.
