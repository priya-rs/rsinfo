Reservoir characterization in Widuri field is complicated by complex lithology and difficulties in predicting changes in fluid distribution. 33-S Sand body is interpreted as complex channel compartmentalized and part of waterflood project associated with distributary channels, mouth bars and delta front shoal (Figure 2).

The 34-3 is not the primary reservoir since the distribution of this sand reservoir is not widespread and include in the 34-series package. To maintain the production of the well, several well intervention especially change the ESP was done. In April 2014, there was increasing water cut phenomenon after this job happen. Average water cut before pump repair job was 84% WC (154 bopd, 982 bfpd), and average water cut after pump repair job was 91.5% WC 25 bopd, 1233 bfpd). This condition made the production of this well decrease about 125 bopd (Figure 10). The low oil production was keep maintaining until 2016 before the well got DHP (Down Hole Problem).

Looking at the well criteria selection, this well was one of the candidates for modified completion fluid treatment since the wellbore of WW D-29 was also did not have any mechanical restriction. This well was completed cased hole completion with open hole horizontal (440 ft) using Excluder screen in the main production reservoir 35-1 sand (Figure 11).

After evaluating the productivity of the well, upsizing the ESP is an opportunity to increase the production fluid, so the ESP was change from Q10 with capacity range 600 – 1250 bfpd to Q20 with capacity range 1150 – 2050 bfpd. With both combination of increasing fluid rate and decreasing water cut, WW D-29 become successful pilot project. The water cut was decrease form 98% to 83% in the lowest water cut, then sustain in 92%WC. We can see the chart of increasing oil production both from increasing fluid production and decreasing water cut in Figure 21 and Figure 22.
