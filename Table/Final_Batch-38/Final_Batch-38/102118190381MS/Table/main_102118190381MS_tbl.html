By 2013, six pilot tests together with six industrial field tests were conducted, basic information of these test sites is presented in Table 1. Duanxi ASP project was put on line to produce in June 26, 2012, and the information of conducted SASP flooding is shown in Table 2. About six months after the primary ASP slug was injected, the water cut of central areas had decreased by 5.5%, and a total of 150,000 tonnes more oil was recovered. Within one year, the water cut of nearly 104 wells, accounting for 88.1% of the total wells had decreased by 1.0% (CNPC, May 2013).

WASP system is composed of weak alkali, polymer and surfactant. Table 3 shows the comparison of field test results of WASP flooding and SASP flooding. Comparing with SASP, WASP has similar incremental oil recovery rate (R), higher injectivity and productivity (J, fluid productivity index), higher production rate (v), and above all, lower scaling degree.

Although WASP can weaken the impact of scaling, the injection pressure of WASP flooding still increases fast (Nie, 2017), as presented in Fig. 4, so further measures should be taken. Through using a compound scale removal agent (YC-36 +12.0% HCl+2.5% NH4F+1.0% Fe stabilizer +0.3% corrosion inhibitor) proposed by Guan (2016), more than 98.0% of the scales can be removed according to laboratory tests. To obtain more reliable field data, its commercial implementation was conducted on 14 wells in Daqing Oilfield, separately. Results (Table 4) shows that the initial average injection pressure decreased by 2.2 MPa, the average daily injection volume increased by 12.5 m3, and the average validity could be as long as 183 days by December, 2014.

Daqing conducted a SP flooding field test in Pu I 1-4 (basic information is presented in Table 5) in December 2008, mainly used as a post-polymer flooding EOR technology. In only one year, the oil recovery of all the six tested wells were enhanced, with the oil production rate increased from 47.0 tonnes/d to 60.0 tonnes/d, and water cut decreased from 96.13% to 92.87% (Jia, et al. 2011).
