Reservoir Context Reservoir 1 is a high porosity (20-30%), Pleistocene sand deposited in a high net to gross, delta front environment. This sand is gas bearing, extending across two communicating fault blocks within the field (Figure 1). The two wells completed in the reservoir stopped producing by becoming liquid loaded due to water breakthrough. After 8 years of production, the gas recovery when benchmarked against local analogues had potentially low sweep efficiency.
