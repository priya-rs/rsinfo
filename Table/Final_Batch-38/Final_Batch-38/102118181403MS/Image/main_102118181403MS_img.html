The Ahmadi, Burgan, and Magwa structural closures (Fig-1), which comprise Greater Burgan, lie along the axis of the prominent N-S trending Kuwait Arch.

Figure-2 shows the general sequence stratigraphy of Burgan field. Burgan reservoir is divided into three zones BGSU, BGSM and BGSL where all three have different pressures.

Since BGSM was expected to have less oil column in this area, it was decided to place the horizontal well A1H in BU3 which is the lowest member of BGSU (Figure-3).

The wellbore moved to deeper stratigraphy position as we see the signal strength correlates with the model log. Inclination was maintained to stay within the BU3 target sand all the time. (Figure-4).

A total of 800' oil sand was drilled out of lateral length of 1058'. Figure-5 shows the well placement.

Figure-6 shows an advanced ICD where Inflow Control Device Technology has been combined with mechanical sliding sleeve door functionality to provide a system that has the flexibility to allow the user to selectively shut-off sections of the production interval, providing the user with a simple and robust system for controlling and isolating flow while providing reliable sand control.

Single open port with small port size was used on the toe side to delay the water production whereas all ports on the heel side were kept open due to low permeability. Figure-7 shows the ICD assembly.

Figure-8 shows the flow profile and Table-1 shows the contribution from each ICD.

All seven ICDs in last two compartments were closed by shifting the sliding sleeves in closed position (push to close type Sliding Sleeves). The slack off weight clearly indicated the movement of seven ICDs (Figure-9).

Figure-10 shows the production performance of the well before and after closing the ICDs.
