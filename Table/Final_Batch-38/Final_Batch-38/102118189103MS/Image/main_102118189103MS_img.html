The geology of the Agbami field is characterized by a double-plunging anticlinal structure, cut by a significant thrust fault along the crestal axis with multiple small relief, normal faults further dividing the reservoirs into fault blocks. The reservoirs are northwest-southeast trending and were deposited in a deep-water turbidite environment through high-energy and mass mud/debris gravity flows. The field has four major stacked reservoir units 13MY, 14MY, 16MY, and 17MY. The current estimate of the oil in place is approximately 2.6 billion barrels with about 80% located in the 17MY reservoir (See Figure 1-3).1

The FPSO has 2.1 MMBO storage capacity, 250 MBOPD oil processing capacity, gas processing capacity of 450 MMSCFD, gas injection capacity of 415 MMSCFD, and water injection capacity of 450 MBWPD. 38 subsea wells (22 producers, 11 water injectors and 5 gas injectors) have been planned to develop the field with project execution optimized in 3 phases – 22 wells including the FPSO, subsea and topsides infrastructure and equipment in phase 1, 10 development wells in phase 2, and 6 development wells in phase 3 (See Figure 4).1,2

Consistent with the above logic, an Experimental design workflow was developed by the Asset team as shown in Figure 5.

The first step in evaluating the infill drilling opportunities was to history match the most-likely (or base case) simulation model using production, injection, pressure and soft (4D seismic) data. This deterministic model was run to end of lease life with wells in the approved field development plan to identify areas with significant by-passed/un-swept oil. The proposed infill wells were then tested individually to target the by-passed oil (See Figure 6). An optimum development scenario was run to test all proposed subsurface infill locations combining both injectors and producers with the wells ranked on the basis of incremental recovery.

Manifolds with excess capacity were preferred for targets that could be reached from more than one manifold (See Figure 7). An important lesson that was learned at this stage and carried forward was to ensure that all facility, drilling and completions constraints are built into the forecast workflow early in the life of the project to minimize surprises down the line. This helped the project team quickly assess the potential infill targets to know which opportunities were immediately executable using the existing subsea infrastructure and which opportunities would require new subsea infrastructure.

The historical drilling data from Agbami wells were analyzed on several levels such as days to drill to different casing depths, Non Productive Time, Major Unscheduled Events, Completion type (single or dual) by the team to establish ranges from the historical data. A probabilistic spreadsheet model was then built to forecast drilling and completions time for the new wells based on similarities with the analogue wells using the parameters earlier defined (See Figure 8).

The OVAAT approach was used to capture the impact of each uncertainty on the objective function. The objective function was defined as EUR at Lease life. The Initial production (IP) rates for the three Agbami Phase 3 producers not yet online were determined using well performance (IPR + VLP) modelling coupled to a production network model and benchmarked with the performance of existing offset wells. The OVAAT showed that the big-hitter uncertainties were the subsurface model, the Initial Production rates for the Agbami Phase 3 Producers not yet online, the Productivity Index (PI) of Agbami Phase 3 Wells and Water Injection Efficiency (See Figure 9).

For the full probabilistic approach, based on the OVAAT results, the initial production rates of the three Agbami Phase 3 producers were combined into one uncertainty (PH3_IP). The rig start, D&C days and subsea tree arrival dates were also combined into the "SCHD" uncertainty. Thus, 5 uncertainties were carried forward into the probabilistic modelling using Folded Placket-Burman methodology. The three models representing the reference case outcomes were selected to honour the top 2 OVAAT uncertainties which were: Subsurface Uncertainty (SSUN) and PH3_IP (See Figure 10-11).
