In well SCh-53 the model fitted using Skin Factor = 9 at the first period of production, then the well suffered and incremental damage that increased the Skin factor to 22, the model is shown in the Figure 9.4.

Curves of productivity index IPR, were calculated with different skin factors. The curves are shown in Figure 9.5.

In well SCh-28 the model matched using Skin Factor = 2, as shown in Figure 9.6.

IPR curves for different skin factors are shown in Figure 9.7.

Well SCh-53 was re-perforated 14 months after the PLT log had detected Joule Thomson Effect. Results are shown in Figure 10.1. The increase in gas production was 25 Mm3/d.

In the new flowing condition, a new PLT was run in SCh-53 as shown in Figure 10.2. This figure also shows a comparison with 2009 PLT profile (in violet) of the same well. It’s assumed that re-perforation has restored incremental damage, decreasing the skin damage back to 9, and that the flowing condition was similar to the conditions in the first PLT.

A repair proposal involving hydraulic fracturing of SCh-53 was made according to these results and build-up tests analysis that confirmed high formation damage. Results of fracture stimulation are shown in Figure 10.3. With this stimulation a 72 Mm3/d incremental production of gas was obtained.

Once stabilized production, a new PLT profile was run in SCh-53 well in 2013 as shown in Figure 10.4. This figure also shows comparison with 2011 PLT profile (in violet), indicating that after fracturing, the cooling effect of Joule-Thomson decreased significantly. This behavior confirms the premises that motivated this paper.

Figure 10.5 shows in detail ΔT decrese achived by well fracturing.
